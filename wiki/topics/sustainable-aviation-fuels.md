# Sustainable aviation fuels

*Updated: 2026-07-17*

*Part of [Aviation](aviation-decarbonisation.md).* T&E's aviation-fuels work runs from a 2017
campaign to bring aviation into the Renewable Energy Directive, through the years-long legislative
fight over what became ReFuelEU Aviation (2023), to today's implementation battles over the
regulation's dedicated e-kerosene sub-mandate. The throughline across a decade of briefings,
letters and reports is consistent: biofuel-derived "SAF" repeats road transport's biofuels
mistakes (scarce, fraud-prone feedstock, real deforestation risk), so the mandate's design should
steer aviation toward e-kerosene — green-hydrogen-derived synthetic jet fuel — instead.

**Contents:** [The ReFuelEU Aviation mandate](#the-refueleu-aviation-mandate) · [E-kerosene and electrofuels](#e-kerosene-and-electrofuels) · [Direct air capture](#direct-air-capture) · [Feedstock sustainability and mandate design](#feedstock-sustainability-and-mandate-design)

## The ReFuelEU Aviation mandate

Before the EU had its own aviation fuels law, T&E fought the same battle at the UN's aviation body.
[How to incentivise renewable aviation fuels through the Renewable Energy Directive](../sources/how-to-incentivise-renewable-aviation-fuels-through-the-renewable-energy-directi.md)
(2017) set out T&E's original case for folding aviation into the EU's Renewable Energy Directive.
The same year, T&E was part of the coalition that helped 25 countries — many from Europe — reject
ICAO's proposed volume-based "2050 Vision on Sustainable Aviation Fuels" at a Mexico City
conference, arguing the targets were based on poor analysis that overstated the achievable
emissions cuts ([Countries reject plan for aviation biofuels targets](../sources/countries-reject-plan-for-aviation-biofuels-targets.md);
[Aviation biofuels target rejected](../sources/aviation-biofuels-target-rejected.md)). Weeks later,
[EU Commission surrenders to United Nations' ICAO on aviation biofuels](../sources/eu-commission-surrenders-to-united-nations-icao-on-aviation-biofuels.md)
reported the Commission and member states backing down at ICAO's Council, stripping 10 of 12
sustainability criteria so that highly unsustainable biofuels could still qualify for the CORSIA
offsetting scheme — an early sign of the sustainability-criteria fights that would recur throughout
the EU's own legislation.

The European Commission launched its own "ReFuelEU" initiative under the European Green Deal, and
[How EU legislation can drive an uptake of sustainable advanced fuels in aviation](../sources/how-eu-legislation-can-drive-an-uptake-of-sustainable-advanced-fuels-in-aviation.md)
(2020) was T&E's first response to it. That December, as EU governments began drafting national jet
fuel plans, [Plans for 'green' jet fuels threaten to repeat biofuels mistakes](../sources/plans-for-green-jet-fuels-threaten-to-repeat-biofuels-mistakes-analysis.md)
found that Spain, the Netherlands, Sweden and Finland were on track to repeat the biofuels fiasco by
promoting crop-based fuels that emit more carbon than the fossil fuel they replace, with only
Germany focused on developing near-zero-carbon electrofuels — while
[EU transport plan a big step but risks rerun of biofuels fiasco](../sources/eu-transport-plan-a-big-step-but-risks-rerun-of-biofuels-fiasco.md)
made the same warning of the Commission's own mobility strategy for planes and ships. T&E set out
its sustainability requirements for mandate design in
[Making aviation fuel mandates sustainable](../sources/making-aviation-fuel-mandates-sustainable.md)
(December 2020), and made the same case to the UK government the following year in
[The UK's green jet fuel mandate needs robust sustainability criteria to set aviation on a net-zero flightpath](../sources/the-uk-s-green-jet-fuel-mandate-needs-robust-sustainability-criteria-to-set-avia.md).
[The five tests awaiting the EU's big 'Fit for 55' climate push](../sources/the-five-tests-awaiting-the-eu-s-big-fit-for-55-climate-push.md)
(June 2021), published shortly before the Commission's proposal, set out T&E's headline ask for it:
an ambitious e-fuel mandate for aviation and shipping specifically, with no role for biofuels —
essentially the same "beyond biofuels" argument the mandate-design fight above would spend the next
two years litigating.

T&E made the same case directly to the UK, whose own green-fuels mandate ran on a parallel track to
the EU's:
[T&E's response to the UK's "Jet Zero" net-zero-aviation consultation](../sources/jet-zero-our-strategy-for-net-zero-aviation-in-the-uk.md)
(September 2021) welcomed the UK's proposed SAF mandate and non-CO2 monitoring but questioned why
the UK, unlike the EU, was not also proposing a kerosene tax to close the price gap with fossil jet
fuel, and pushed back on the consultation's framing that residual 2050 aviation emissions were
inevitable rather than a policy choice.

As the EU proposal took shape, a coalition of airlines, fuel suppliers and NGOs pushed back against
Commission plans to limit the mandate's scope to intra-EEA flights only
([NGOs and aviation sector call for long-haul emissions to be covered by EU's Sustainable Aviation Fuels mandate](../sources/ngos-and-aviation-sector-call-for-long-haul-emissions-to-be-covered-by-eu-s-sust.md),
March 2021). The Commission's eventual Fit for 55 proposal mandated both advanced biofuels and
e-kerosene together
([What the EU climate plan means for aviation fuel](../sources/what-the-eu-climate-plan-means-for-aviation-fuel.md),
May 2022) — a compromise T&E's broader review of the whole Fit for 55 package,
[What the EU's climate plan means for Europe](../sources/what-the-eu-s-climate-plan-means-for-europe.md)
(July 2021), had already flagged as a weakness: mandating biofuels and e-kerosene together, without
strong e-kerosene-specific safeguards, risked letting biofuels crowd out the fuel T&E saw as the
only genuinely scalable option. That same alliance of airlines and NGOs — unusual allies on most other aviation
questions (see [Aviation § Airplane pollution](aviation-decarbonisation.md#airplane-pollution)) —
came together again to back it:
[Airlines, NGOs unite behind push for clean jet fuels in Europe](../sources/airlines-ngos-unite-behind-push-for-clean-jet-fuels-in-europe.md)
(February 2022) reported industry and environmental groups jointly backing the EU's plan for a
substantial increase in climate-friendly jet fuels. The scope of what counted as an eligible
"biofuel" became the mandate's central fight in 2022: [ReFuelEU Aviation: T&E's recommendations on the biofuel definition](../sources/refueleu-aviation-t-e-s-recommendations-on-the-biofuel-definition.md)
set out which feedstocks should qualify, and an airline-NGO coalition warned in
[Airline and green NGO urge MEPs to exclude palm oil by-products from European aviation](../sources/airline-and-green-ngo-urge-meps-to-exclude-palm-oil-by-products-from-european-av.md)
that fuels linked to deforestation and food-price rises risked flooding the European aviation
market. MEPs sided with that argument, and
[EU lawmakers stop controversial biofuels from fuelling planes](../sources/eu-lawmakers-stop-controversial-biofuels-from-fuelling-planes.md)
(July 2022) reported the resulting Parliament vote as securing sustainable fuels' role in aviation
decarbonisation, a result T&E welcomed in the accompanying op-ed
[Europe gets one step closer to a green flight](../sources/europe-gets-one-step-closer-to-a-green-flight.md),
which added that the vote also widened the definition of eligible synthetic fuels to explicitly
include renewable electricity and green hydrogen. The fight then moved to the parallel Renewable Energy Directive (RED III)
trilogue, where [Third time lucky? RED III trilogue another opportunity to move beyond unsustainable fuels](../sources/third-time-lucky-red-iii-trilogue-another-opportunity-to-move-beyond-unsustainab.md)
(October 2022) argued the same biofuel-definition fight needed resolving in the EU's broader
renewable-fuels law, not just the aviation-specific one.

The legislative fight concluded in April 2023, when EU negotiators reached a final trilogue
agreement — reported by T&E's French office as
[Aviation: the EU agrees one of the world's most ambitious mandates for sustainable fuels](../sources/aviation-l-ue-s-accorde-sur-l-un-des-mandats-les-plus-ambitieux-au-monde-en-mati.md)
and by its German office as
[EU adopts world's largest regulation for green fuels in aviation](../sources/eu-beschlie-t-weltweit-gr-te-verordnung-f-r-gr-ne-kraftstoffe-in-der-luftfahrt.md)
<!-- Spanish-language press release la-uni-n-europea-... covers the same event; the French/German
releases carry the fuller framing, so it isn't cited separately here per this wiki's near-duplicate
convention. -->
— finally opening a European market for green aviation fuels via a gradually rising SAF blending
mandate, including a dedicated e-kerosene sub-mandate T&E had spent two years lobbying for (see
[E-kerosene and electrofuels](#e-kerosene-and-electrofuels) below). Implementation questions
followed adoption:
[Implementing the EU's e-SAF mandate](../sources/implementing-the-eu-s-e-saf-mandate.md)
(October 2024) analysed the penalty regime jet fuel suppliers face from 2025 for missing SAF
quotas, finding that non-compliance is always costlier than compliance if penalties are correctly
priced — but warning that legacy fuel suppliers were still not meaningfully involved in
e-kerosene projects, with collective e-kerosene non-compliance penalties potentially reaching
€292bn over 2030-2035 versus an estimated €37bn to actually produce enough e-kerosene to meet the
mandate. A month later,
[T&E's take on the EU Transport Commissioner's hearing](../sources/t-e-s-take-on-the-eu-transport-commissioner-s-hearing.md)
(November 2024) recorded commissioner-designate Apostolos Tzitzikostas committing to a "Sustainable
Transport Investment Plan" to fund clean fuel production without saying how it would be financed or
whether it would prioritise e-kerosene over biofuels, and rejecting a "book and claim" system that
would let fuel suppliers meet SAF targets without physically supplying it where the fuel is sold, on
the grounds that book-and-claim certificates are vulnerable to fraud and double counting.

## E-kerosene and electrofuels

T&E's foundational case for e-kerosene (also called electrofuels or e-fuels: synthetic jet fuel
made from green hydrogen and captured CO2) predates the ReFuelEU mandate itself.
[Electrofuels? Yes, we can … if we're efficient](../sources/electrofuels-yes-we-can-if-we-re-efficient.md)
(December 2020) and [How to ensure the sustainability of electrofuels](../sources/how-to-ensure-the-sustainability-of-electrofuels.md)
(January 2021) set out the efficiency and sustainability conditions under which electrofuels make
sense for hard-to-electrify sectors like aviation and shipping, while
[FAQ: the what and how of e-kerosene](../sources/faq-the-what-and-how-of-e-kerosene.md)
(February 2021) is T&E's plain-language explainer, arguing e-kerosene has the greatest potential of
any SAF option to cut aviation's climate impact.
[Is it possible to fly to New York without emitting CO2?](../sources/is-it-possible-to-fly-to-new-york-without-emitting-co2.md)
(2022) illustrated why that potential remained mostly theoretical for an ordinary traveller at the
time: with only one small experimental e-kerosene plant operating in Europe, already sold out, a
single passenger seeking a genuinely CO2-free roundtrip to New York faced a price roughly €1,000
higher than a fossil-fuelled ticket and no easy way to actually buy the fuel, since only airlines —
not travel agencies or passengers — can purchase jet fuel directly. The same year,
[The most important piece of EU climate law you've never heard of](../sources/the-most-important-piece-of-eu-climate-law-you-ve-never-heard-of.md)
(September 2022) flagged the RFNBO delegated act — the technical rules defining what counts as
renewable hydrogen and e-fuels — as a quietly decisive piece of legislation for the whole e-kerosene
push.

Industry and T&E moved from advocacy to a joint campaign for an ambitious e-kerosene sub-mandate
ahead of the Commission's 2021 ReFuelEU proposal:
[Now is the time for an ambitious European e-kerosene target](../sources/now-is-the-time-for-an-ambitious-european-e-kerosene-target.md)
and the accompanying press release
[Set ambitious EU targets for synthetic jet fuel while preserving a level playing field for airlines](../sources/set-ambitious-eu-targets-for-synthetic-jet-fuel-while-preserving-a-level-playing.md)
(July 2021) saw T&E, Lufthansa, the Global Alliance Powerfuels, pilots' representatives, airports
and e-fuel suppliers jointly call for a clear e-kerosene market signal, building on the technical
groundwork in [E-kerosene mandate: key steps for ReFuelEU success](../sources/e-kerosene-mandate-key-steps-for-refueleu-success.md)
(May 2021). The coalition returned in November 2022 with
[Global Powerfuels Alliance and Transport & Environment call for an ambitious e-kerosene target](../sources/global-powerfuels-alliance-and-transport-environment-call-for-an-ambitious-e-ker.md),
arguing an ambitious target would add minimal cost to tickets while giving investors the certainty
needed to build plants.

T&E has tracked Europe's actual e-kerosene project pipeline since 2022:
[Analysis of green jet fuel production in Europe](../sources/analysis-of-green-jet-fuel-production-in-europe.md)
and its press release
[Green aviation fuels could save 5 million tonnes of CO2 in 2030](../sources/green-aviation-fuels-could-save-5-million-tonnes-of-co2-in-2030.md)
(June 2022) forecast 1.83 million tonnes of European e-kerosene production by 2030, saving roughly 5
MtCO2. The tracker's 2024 update,
[E-fuels for planes: with 45 projects, is the EU on track to meet its targets?](../sources/e-fuels-for-planes-with-45-projects-is-the-eu-on-track-to-meet-its-targets.md)
(January 2024), found Norway, Germany and France surging ahead on announced projects even as market
uncertainties persisted, while [How is e-kerosene developing in Europe?](../sources/how-is-e-kerosene-developing-in-europe.md)
(February 2024) explained why e-kerosene's near-CO2-neutral combustion sets it apart from other SAF
options. By mid-2025 the pipeline had grown but stalled at the financing stage:
[Europe risks losing its e-fuels head start](../sources/e-fuels-f-r-die-luftfahrt-europa-l-uft-gefahr-vorsprung-zu-verlieren.md)
(Germany, June 2025) found over 40 European e-kerosene projects announced, with a combined capacity
that could meet around 5% of European aviation's fuel demand — enough for the EU's targets if all
were built — but not one of them yet under construction, only four at an advanced planning stage,
and none with a final investment decision, held back chiefly by financing and by major oil
companies' continued absence from the market. Two milestones bookend this tracking work:
[First passenger flight performed using clean fuels. Sort of.](../sources/first-passenger-flight-performed-using-clean-fuels-sort-of.md)
(February 2021) reported the world's first passenger flight partly fuelled by synthetic kerosene,
and [Lift off for e-kerosene at first commercial factory](../sources/lift-off-for-e-kerosene-at-first-commercial-factory.md)
(October 2021) reported the start of production at Germany's first commercial e-kerosene plant. By
late 2024, that early promise had turned into a delay story:
[Oil companies and airlines are delaying investments in e-kerosene](../sources/lkonzerne-und-fluggesellschaften-verschleppen-investitionen-in-e-kerosin.md)
(Germany, December 2024) found most airlines showed no serious ambition to switch and oil companies
were under-investing, slowing climate-neutral flying and costing the German economy a competitive
opportunity — the same financing gap T&E's e-fuels-financing work addresses in more depth (see
[E-fuels financing and infrastructure](e-fuels-financing.md)). A parallel national fight played out
in Germany over whether to keep national e-kerosene quotas at all:
[E-kerosene providers call upon the German government to keep the country's quotas for green jet fuel](../sources/e-kerosene-providers-call-upon-the-german-government-to-keep-the-country-s-quota.md)
(September 2024) saw seven e-fuel producers press the government not to weaken them.

## Direct air capture

Producing e-kerosene requires a CO2 source, and T&E's position is that this CO2 should come from
direct air capture (DAC) rather than industrial flue gas, which risks locking in continued fossil
fuel use elsewhere in the economy.
[Why direct air capture holds one of the keys to sustainable aviation](../sources/why-direct-air-capture-holds-one-of-the-keys-to-sustainable-aviation.md)
(July 2021), a T&E-commissioned E4tech study, was the first assessment of whether and how DAC could
scale to meet European e-kerosene demand. The following year,
[How can ReFuelEU enable investment in direct air capture?](../sources/how-can-refueleu-enable-investment-in-direct-air-capture.md)
(March 2022, a joint statement with the Negative Emissions Platform) pushed for the mandate's
design to actively channel investment toward DAC specifically, and
[Scaling up Direct Air Capture](../sources/scaling-up-direct-air-capture.md)
(July 2022) set out in more detail why DAC is essential to aviation decarbonisation at scale.

## Feedstock sustainability and mandate design

Beyond the biofuel-definition fight covered above, T&E's mandate-design work also engaged with the
EU's broader renewable-fuels law that underpins both the biofuel and e-kerosene provisions of
ReFuelEU.
[Which renewable transport fuels deliver substantial carbon reductions?](../sources/which-renewable-transport-fuels-deliver-substantial-carbon-reductions.md)
(2020) and [T&E's response to roadmap consultation on the inception impact assessment for the Revision of Directive (EU) 2018/2001](../sources/t-e-s-response-to-roadmap-consultation-on-the-inception-impact-assessment-for-th.md)
(2020) fed into the Renewable Energy Directive review, which T&E continued pressing on in
[How to make Europe's green fuels law truly green](../sources/how-to-make-europe-s-green-fuels-law-truly-green.md)
and [The EU's green fuels law: A clean shift for EU transport fuels?](../sources/the-eu-s-green-fuels-law-a-clean-shift-for-eu-transport-fuels.md)
(2021), warning that the original RED had incentivised heavy reliance on crop biofuels with real
land-use and food-price costs, a risk T&E did not want repeated for aviation.
[Advanced renewable fuels in EU transport](../sources/advanced-renewable-fuels-in-eu-transport.md)
(2021) separately assessed whether the Commission's 24% renewable-transport-energy target for 2030
was actually feasible given realistic advanced-fuel supply, and
[How EU states can tackle unsustainable biofuels and promote cleaner alternatives?](../sources/how-eu-states-can-tackle-unsustainable-biofuels-and-promote-cleaner-alternatives.md)
(2024) turned to how member states should implement the resulting RED III rules on the ground. See
[Biofuels](biofuels.md) for T&E's fuller case, developed later, that the sustainable feedstock base
for any biofuel-derived SAF is fundamentally too scarce and fraud-prone to scale.

Mandate *design*, not just fuel eligibility, has also drawn T&E scrutiny.
[The good, bad and the ugly of SAF mandates](../sources/the-good-bad-and-the-ugly-of-saf-mandates.md)
(January 2022) used Air France-KLM's early 1% SAF mandate — and its passenger surcharge — as a case
study in how mandates can go wrong if poorly designed, while
[Tankering in aviation](../sources/tankering-in-aviation.md) (November 2022) warned that a
national or regional SAF mandate creates a perverse incentive for airlines to "tanker" — carry extra
fuel bought outside the mandate's jurisdiction — undermining the very emissions savings the mandate
is meant to deliver. Outside the EU, T&E also engaged directly with the UK's own SAF mandate design
(see [The ReFuelEU Aviation mandate](#the-refueleu-aviation-mandate) above for the UK letter).

# EV battery and raw materials supply chains

*Updated: 2025-11-20*

*Part of [Electric vehicles](electric-vehicles.md).*

Six T&E publications landed within two weeks of each other in November 2025, all addressing a
common theme: that a genuinely green EV transition depends on what happens upstream — mineral
sourcing, battery chemistry, materials decarbonisation and waste rules — not only on vehicle sales
targets.

## Aluminium in cars

[Reducing the carbon footprint of aluminium in cars: why and how?](../sources/reducing-the-carbon-footprint-of-aluminium-in-cars-why-and-how.md)
(report, November 2025) examines how to secure an ambitious uptake of green (low-carbon)
aluminium in cars by 2040: switching to green aluminium in EU car production could cut CO2 by as
much as removing 900,000 fossil-fuel cars from the road each year, at an estimated extra cost of
just €25 per car. *See also:* [Car and van CO2 standards](car-and-van-co2-standards.md) for T&E's
tailpipe-CO2 work on the same vehicles — this page instead covers what the car is made of.

## Battery recycling and the End-of-life Vehicles Regulation

[From waste to value: Driving circularity in Europe](../sources/from-waste-to-value-driving-circularity-in-europe.md)
(consultation response on the upcoming Circular Economy Act, November 2025) calls for greater use
of locally recycled-content targets, simplified shipment rules, materials-leakage prevention, and a
stronger focus on re-mining — recovering critical raw materials from existing mining waste rather
than only from new extraction. These are the same recycled-content and re-mining questions at stake
in the EU's End-of-life Vehicles (ELV) Regulation revision.

[Driving best practice in mining waste management](../sources/driving-best-practice-in-mining-waste-management.md)
(report with Earthworks, November 2025) provides a global benchmarking of mining waste rules,
addressing the upstream extraction risks behind the raw materials this page's other sources are
trying to secure and recycle responsibly.

## Critical raw materials and industrial policy

[ReSourceEU risks becoming a rebranding project. Here's what it needs to become a game changer](../sources/resourceeu-risks-becoming-a-rebranding-project-here-s-what-it-needs-to-become-a-.md)
(op-ed, November 2025) is T&E's critique of the EU's ReSourceEU critical raw materials plan: as
drafted, it risks simply reshuffling existing initiatives rather than substantively closing the
EU's raw-materials gap for EVs and batteries.

[Making the Industrial Accelerator Act deliver for EVs and batteries](../sources/making-the-industrial-accelerator-act-deliver-for-evs-and-batteries.md)
(position paper, November 2025) sets out T&E's four-pillar proposal for what the EU's Industrial
Accelerator Act needs to include to genuinely support European EV and battery manufacturing.

[Can't stop the market: why Europe should embrace LFP batteries (sustainably)](../sources/can-t-stop-the-market-why-europe-should-embrace-lfp-batteries-sustainably.md)
(op-ed, November 2025) argues that lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery chemistry — currently
dominated by China — has been critical to making mass-market EVs affordable, and that the EU needs
to build its own LFP capacity to catch up rather than resist the market shift toward it.

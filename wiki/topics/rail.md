# Rail

*Updated: 2025-11-19*

## Infrastructure investment

[The State of the EU's Rail Infrastructure](../sources/the-state-of-the-eu-s-rail-infrastructure.md)
(report, June 2025) finds that despite €40bn/year of EU-wide rail investment, the network remains
fragmented and falls short of the EU's own Trans-European Transport Network (TEN-T) requirements:
fewer than 20% of TEN-T corridors have the European Train Control System (ETCS) needed for trains
to cross borders safely and reliably, and average line speeds remain low in eight member states.
T&E's recommendations: accelerate ERTMS signalling deployment, raise line speeds to a
time-competitive 160 km/h, add capacity via new tracks where needed, boost electrification, convert
incompatible track gauges, and build new high-speed lines — funded through a "Connecting Europe
through high-speed" plan, with the next EU budget cycle framed as the key moment to secure that
funding (see also [EV battery and raw materials supply chains § Critical raw materials and industrial policy](ev-battery-and-raw-materials.md#critical-raw-materials-and-industrial-policy)
for T&E's parallel critique of that same budget's cleantech funding).

## Ticketing and affordability

[Youth, consumer and environmental NGOs call on the EU to improve rail ticket booking](../sources/youth-consumer-and-environmental-ngos-call-on-the-eu-to-improve-rail-ticket-book.md)
(joint letter from the Youth on Track coalition — youth, consumer and environmental groups
including T&E, BEUC and the European Passengers' Federation — September 2025) argues that even
though trains emit on average 5x less CO2 than flying (up to 50x on routes like Paris-Barcelona),
two barriers keep people flying instead: cross-border tickets that cost more than flights (up to
26x more on some specific routes, per Greenpeace data cited in the letter) and booking systems that
don't combine legs across different national operators (28% of 15-24-year-olds surveyed by the
Commission's Eurobarometer reported exactly this difficulty). The coalition's asks, timed to the
EU's Single Ticketing Package and its upcoming VAT-rules revision for passenger transport:
interoperable cross-border booking systems, automatic rebooking rights when a connection is missed,
a VAT exemption for international rail tickets, and an end to aviation's long-standing VAT
exemption — with the resulting revenue redirected to rail modernisation and affordability. This
same VAT-imbalance argument recurs in T&E's aviation tax work — see
[Aviation § Aviation taxes](aviation-decarbonisation.md#aviation-taxes).

## Military mobility as a rail-investment opportunity

[If done right, EU Military Mobility can generate clear benefits for people and the climate](../sources/if-done-right-eu-military-mobility-can-generate-clear-benefits-for-people-and-th.md)
(press release, November 2025) responds to the European Commission's EU Military Mobility Package —
a proposal to speed up cross-border troop and equipment movement — by arguing its focus on
infrastructure upgrades along priority corridors is also a rare opening to fix the fragmentation
and inconsistent national rules that have long slowed civilian cross-border rail, including faster
rollout of the European Rail Traffic Management System (ERTMS, the same signalling gap identified
above). T&E's caveat: the opportunity is infrastructure and clean-fuel investment, not a road-building
programme. The package's other elements — support for Sustainable Aviation/Maritime Fuels and
battery-powered shipbuilding — are covered under
[E-fuels financing and infrastructure](e-fuels-financing.md) and
[IMO shipping fuel standard](imo-shipping-fuel-standard.md) respectively.

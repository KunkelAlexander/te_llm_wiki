# Only a third of business flyers expect to return to normal

*News · Brussels · 2021-04-29*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/only-third-business-flyers-expect-return-normal)

> Most business travellers expect to fly less than they did before the pandemic according to a poll published in the Guardian. The huge restrictions on air travel had no impact on productivity and one in five even said it had a positive impact. It shows that reductions in corporate air travel can be part of the new normal, says T&E.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- YouGov poll of business travellers in the UK, Germany, France, Spain, the Netherlands, Sweden and Denmark: 45% said they would fly less often in future, 38% said air travel would be about the same as before.
- Airline pollution fell 64% in the prior year (per T&E data) as the pandemic nearly bankrupted the sector; the industry had received €36 billion in public bailouts by the time of publication.

- This T&E news piece covers a YouGov poll (published in the Guardian) finding most European business travellers expect permanently reduced corporate flying post-pandemic.
- T&E's Andrew Murphy frames this shift to remote business as one of the pandemic's defining social changes, arguing public bailout funds should not be used to prop up pre-pandemic business travel volumes but instead redirected to cleaner fuels and new aircraft designs.
- T&E notes IATA's call for governments to fund new air ticket/route subsidies, and pushes back that public money should not be used to promote polluting behaviour, reiterating its position (from its "One year of airline bailouts" briefing) that further bailouts should require green/social conditionality and a national aviation decarbonisation plan.

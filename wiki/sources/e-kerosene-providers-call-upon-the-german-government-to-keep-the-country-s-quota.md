# E-kerosene providers call upon the German government to keep the country's quotas for green jet fuel

*Letter · Brussels · 2024-09-16*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/e-kerosene-providers-call-upon-the-german-government-to-keep-the-countrys-quotas-for-green-jet-fuel) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/2023-10-Legal-Opinion-on-EFuels-TE_final.pdf)

> T&E, EDL, Norsk e-fuel, Arcadia e-fuels, Caphenia, Nordic Electrofuel and spark e-fuels are calling upon the German government to maintain national targets for e-kerosene

## Summary

*(This source page bundles three related documents under one entry: the open letter itself, a short T&E news summary of it, and — as its listed PDF — a 2023 legal opinion by Prof. Eckhard Pache commissioned by T&E on whether Germany's national e-kerosene quotas can coexist with ReFuelEU Aviation. All three are summarised together below.)*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Germany's e-kerosene law (§ 37 lit. a (4a) BImSchG) sets national quotas of 50,000 tonnes in 2026, rising to 200,000 tonnes by 2030 — starting earlier and more ambitiously than the EU's ReFuelEU Aviation e-kerosene sub-target, which only reaches a 0.7–1.2% minimum share starting in 2030.
- T&E modelling finds e-kerosene must contribute 20% of German aviation's total CO2 abatement by 2050 — the single largest technology contribution of any measure.
- As of the letter (September 2024), not a single e-kerosene project in the EU had reached a Final Investment Decision (FID).
- Signatories include T&E and six e-kerosene project developers/suppliers: EDL, Norsk e-fuel, Arcadia e-fuels, Caphenia, Nordic Electrofuel and Spark e-fuels.

- The legal opinion (Prof. Pache, Würzburg University, commissioned by T&E) concludes Germany's national e-kerosene quotas remain legally valid alongside ReFuelEU because the EU regulation sets only a "minimum harmonisation" standard — Member States retain the right under Art. 193 TFEU (environmental deviation clause) and, in the alternative, Art. 114(4-5) TFEU, to maintain or introduce more stringent national environmental protection measures.
- The open letter (signed by T&E and the six fuel producers) urges the German government to retain the § 37 quotas rather than scrap them under industry pressure, reinstate e-kerosene public funding cut from the 2024 budget (e.g. via penalty revenues or a UK-style "revenue certainty mechanism"), and handle any changes to the law through a transparent parliamentary process rather than administrative fiat.
- Frames regulatory stability itself as the binding constraint on investment: quoting a cited paper (Smeets & Coridass, 2024) that market confidence in the durability of blending quotas is a precondition for anyone financing e-kerosene plants.

# Leading environment and climate organisations score European Parliament's performance on protecting the Green Deal

*Press release · Brussels · 2024-04-12*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/eu-scoreboard) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/EU-Parliament-Scoreboard_2024-3_compressed.pdf)

> New in-depth data research reveals that only a minority of MEPs during the 2019 - 2024 mandate acted as protectors of the Green Deal. The majority of MEPs acted instead as either procrastinators or prehistoric thinkers, delaying real action with patchy and inconsistent voting records, or worse, completely failing to rise to the challenge of the crises Europe is facing.

## Summary

*(This is a joint publication — the "#voteFutureEU" EU Parliament Scoreboard — co-published by BirdLife Europe, Climate Action Network Europe, the European Environmental Bureau, T&E and WWF European Policy Office, not a T&E-only analysis. The scraped fulltext is a very long, country-by-country data dump of individual MEP/party scores across 27 member states; this summary covers only the methodology and top-line result, not the per-country breakdown, per the wiki's "if in doubt, leave it out" guidance on granular content.)*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Scoring covered 30 policy files (12 on climate-neutral/socially just transition, 8 on nature-positive Europe, 10 on zero pollution/circular economy), drawn from 124 separate plenary roll-call votes across the 2019–2024 European Parliament mandate.
- Each MEP received a 0–100 score per policy file, based on alignment with the five organisations' voting recommendations; scores were then averaged up to national-party and EU-political-group level.
- Groups were classified into three tiers: "Protectors" (a minority of MEPs, led by Greens/EFA), "Procrastinators" (the majority, patchy records — Renew, S&D, EPP), and "Prehistoric thinkers" (ECR, ID, denying the need for climate/nature/pollution action).
- Climate legislation covered included the CO2 standards for cars/vans, Euro VII, the Emissions Trading System (ETS), CO2 standards for trucks and buses, ReFuelEU Aviation, and FuelEU Maritime, among others — i.e. files directly relevant to T&E's transport mandate.

- T&E's contribution centres on the transport-relevant votes within this broader environment/climate scorecard (car and truck CO2 standards, ETS, sustainable aviation and maritime fuels mandates), used to hold the incoming 2024–2029 Parliament accountable ahead of the June 2024 EU elections.
- Positioned as a voter-information tool ahead of the European Parliament elections, timed to coincide with the change of Commission/Parliament later in 2024.

# EU set for major expansion of carbon market

*News · Brussels · 2022-06-30*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/eu-set-for-major-expansion-of-carbon-market)

> The European Parliament and then the Council vote in favour of major expansion of the EU carbon market to include shipping and road transport

<!-- ENRICHED SUMMARY: hand-written, preserved across regenerations by 1_build_wiki_subset.py -->

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Shipping polluters will pay for 100% of CO2 (with methane/N2O potentially added in 2026) on intra-EU voyages and 50% of extra-EU voyages until 2027, rising automatically to 100% of all EU-port voyages from 2027 — after what T&E calls a "10 year fight."
- Over 60% of aviation's pollution will remain outside effective carbon pricing, as ministers rejected extending the ETS to flights departing the EU (something MEPs had voted for weeks earlier); non-CO2 effects (two-thirds of aviation's climate impact) went unaddressed.
- A new EU carbon market for road transport and heating (covering both private and commercial users) was agreed to take effect from 2027.

- This June 2022 T&E news article reports on the EU Council's late-night agreement (28 June) expanding the EU ETS to shipping and road transport/heating as part of "Fit for 55," calling it a historic breakthrough for shipping but a missed opportunity for aviation.
- Notes ministers carved out exemptions for ice-going ships and vessels serving outermost regions (delaying their decarbonisation) and for ferries to islands under 200,000 inhabitants, and again passed on Parliament's proposal to split the new road-transport carbon price between fuel suppliers (Big Oil) and citizens.
- Quotes T&E's Carlos Calvo Ambel: "More polluters are being made to pay. But still not enough" — capturing the piece's overall assessment of the Council deal as a partial win.
- Companion source: [EU governments approve extension of polluter pays principle](eu-governments-approve-extension-of-polluter-pays-principle.md) covers the same Council decision with additional detail on the Social Climate Fund and Effort Sharing Regulation.

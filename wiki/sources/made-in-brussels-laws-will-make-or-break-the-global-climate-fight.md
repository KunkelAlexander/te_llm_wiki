# Made-in-Brussels laws will make or break the global climate fight

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2021-12-16*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/made-in-brussels-laws-will-make-or-break-the-global-climate-fight)

> The transition to electric vehicles is happening. But is it happening fast enough?

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Almost 1 million fully electric cars were expected to be sold in Europe in 2021, a record the op-ed predicts will be "smashed every year from now on."
- Clean electricity accounted for two-thirds of EU electricity generation in the first half of 2021.
- The Commission's July 2021 "Fit for 55" climate package proposes ending combustion-engine car sales by 2035, ending tax-free aviation fuel, introducing hydrogen-based jet fuel (e-kerosene) blending, bringing shipping into carbon pricing, and extending carbon pricing to road transport.

- This December 2021 op-ed by T&E's leadership reflects on a year of EU climate lawmaking and argues 2022 will be decisive for whether the "Fit for 55" package delivers on its ambition.
- It frames the fight as no longer purely environmental but now also industrial strategy: if the EU moves aggressively on batteries and hydrogen, the author argues the US, China and Japan will be forced to follow.
- It anticipates renewed industry lobbying against the package — citing auto suppliers' warnings of up to half a million job losses and ACEA's criticism of tightened car CO2 standards — echoing past (also unfounded, per T&E) industry warnings against earlier EU car CO2, ETS and renewables rules.
- Flags the social-fairness challenge of the transition: carbon pricing hits lower-income people hardest, and technologies like heat pumps, solar panels and EVs remain expensive in the near term even as scaling is expected to bring costs down.

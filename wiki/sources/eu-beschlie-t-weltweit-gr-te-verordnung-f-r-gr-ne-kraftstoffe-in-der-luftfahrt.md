## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- ReFuelEU Aviation's final agreed SAF blending mandate: 2% in 2025, rising to 6% in 2030 and 70% by 2050.
- The synthetic-fuel (e-kerosene) sub-mandate was set at 1.2% for 2030-2031 and 2% for 2032-2035 — a significant increase from the Commission's original flat 0.7% (2030-2035) proposal.

- This is the German-office (T&E Germany) press release on the same April 2023 ReFuelEU Aviation trilogue agreement covered in [Aviation: the EU agrees one of the world's most ambitious mandates for sustainable fuels](aviation-l-ue-s-accorde-sur-l-un-des-mandats-les-plus-ambitieux-au-monde-en-mati.md) — see that page for the fuller summary, including T&E's concerns about the lack of a used-cooking-oil cap and the risk of animal-fat feedstocks displacing demand toward palm oil.
- Also excludes the most controversial feedstocks (food crops, palm oil by-products/PFAD) from the sustainable-biofuel definition, while retaining animal fats and used cooking oil.
- Frames non-CO2 effects' inclusion (via fuel-quality regulation targeting aromatic/sulphur content) as a landmark first, since prior efforts to regulate this two-thirds share of aviation's climate impact had failed.
- Quotes T&E's Matteo Mirolo warning that scaling up SAF still requires further industrial policy support for e-kerosene and stronger safeguards against unsustainable biofuels — and that constrained e-fuel supply means none should be diverted to road transport, where cheaper/more efficient alternatives (electromobility) already exist.

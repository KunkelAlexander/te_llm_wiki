# How to decarbonise aviation in Europe by 2050?

*Original title (French): "Comment décarboner l'aviation en Europe d'ici 2050?"*

*News · France · 2022-03-29*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/te-france/articles/comment-decarboner-laviation-en-europe-dici-2050)

> T&E publishes its roadmap for the decarbonisation of European aviation.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- European aviation traffic grew 67% between 2005 and 2019 while its CO2 emissions grew 24%, reaching 4.9% of total pre-pandemic EU emissions (CO2 only, excluding non-CO2 effects).
- If everyone on the planet flew as much as the richest 10% of Europeans, aviation alone would emit 23 GtCO2/year — roughly two-thirds of 2019's *total* global CO2 emissions.
- A 12% cut in flights could deliver a 50% cut in aviation emissions (per an IEA finding cited by T&E), and halving business travel versus pre-Covid levels could cut 32.6 MtCO2 by 2030.
- T&E's roadmap prices carbon at €165/tCO2 by 2030 for all European aviation emissions (including currently ETS-exempt departing flights) as part of its recommended pathway; under an unconstrained-growth scenario, e-kerosene production could consume up to 24% of all EU renewable electricity generated in 2050.

- This March 2022 French-language T&E news article publishes the French version of T&E's revised "Roadmap to climate neutral aviation" (2018 roadmap updated), and contains more detailed figures than the equivalent English-language announcement (see [Why "flying less" offers the best path to sustainable aviation](why-flying-less-offers-the-best-path-to-sustainable-aviation.md)).
- Central conclusion: demand management (ending airport expansion, halving business travel) is by far the most effective emissions-reduction lever this decade, but alone cannot deliver climate-neutral aviation by 2050 — additional measures are needed across efficiency, fair carbon pricing/taxation, hydrogen/electric aircraft, and e-kerosene/SAF.
- Flags that new-generation zero-emission (hydrogen/electric) aircraft realistically won't be operational until the 2040s — too late to address this decade's emissions — reinforcing the case for demand management and fuel-switching as the near-term levers.
- Also stresses non-CO2 effects (contrails etc.) as potentially larger than CO2 warming impacts, and recommends reducing aromatics in fossil jet fuel and rerouting flights to avoid contrail-forming atmospheric conditions as low-cost near-term fixes.
- Criticises current capital allocation: "hundreds of billions of euros" go to new jets versus "only a few million" invested in new fuels, and calls for financing mechanisms that reward greener aviation rather than sector expansion.

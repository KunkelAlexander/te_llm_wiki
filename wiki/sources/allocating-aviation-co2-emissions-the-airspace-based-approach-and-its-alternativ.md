# Allocating aviation CO2 emissions – The airspace-based approach and its alternatives

*Briefing · Brussels · 2013-01-21*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/allocating-aviation-co2-emissions)

> The ICAO High Level Group in International Aviation and Climate Change, established last November to resolve political questions surrounding a global market based measure for international aviation emissions, also needs to agree an ICAO position on the geographic scope of any national or regional schemes such as the EU ETS. The three alternatives being considered are based on departing flights, nationality of carrier or a country's airspace. Suggestions that an airspace approach would be appropriate are unrealistic and can't be serious. ICAO already recognised 5 years ago that the airspace option was impractical. These talks require a more responsible and disinterested approach and here we explain why.

## Summary

*(No numeric/quantitative findings in this source — it is a procedural/positional briefing, not a study.)*

- Context: ICAO's High Level Group on International Aviation and Climate Change (established November 2012) needed to agree a geographic-scope position for any national or regional market-based measure covering international aviation emissions, including the EU ETS.
- Three candidate scope options were on the table: (1) departing flights, (2) nationality of the carrier, (3) the airspace a flight passes through.
- T&E's position: the airspace-based option is impractical and unrealistic — it argues ICAO itself already reached this conclusion roughly five years earlier (c. 2008) and should not revisit it.
- T&E calls for a "more responsible and disinterested" approach to the scope negotiations, favoring the departing-flights or carrier-nationality alternatives implicitly by process of elimination.

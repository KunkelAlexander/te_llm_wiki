# Setting 2040 climate ambition

*Publication · Brussels · 2023-07-11*

> A landing zone with ambitious transport policies for the EU

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- T&E modelling shows the "Fit for 55" package delivers only a 30% cut in transport emissions by 2040 (vs. 1990) — halving road transport emissions, but letting aviation and shipping emissions rise 13% over that period (only a quarter below their 2005 peak). T&E's own more ambitious "Road to Zero" transport scenario would deliver a 69% cut by 2040.
- Combining T&E's transport scenario with the Commission's non-transport 1.5TECH/1.5LIFE projections yields an economy-wide reduction of almost 90% by 2040 (vs. 1990), against 79% under Fit for 55 alone — the basis for T&E's recommended 90% EU-wide 2040 target.
- 58% of EU aviation CO2 emissions (64 Mt in 2022) fall outside the EU ETS because they're from flights to non-EU destinations; non-CO2 aviation effects (mainly contrails) add a further ~1.7x-3x multiplier depending on the metric used. In shipping, the EU's MRV excludes vessels under 5,000 GT and other categories representing 33 MtCO2e, on top of 92 MtCO2e left uncovered by FuelEU Maritime/ETS's 50%-of-extra-EU-voyage scope.
- Recommends a global carbon-budget-informed target given research showing the EU will exhaust its "fair share" of the remaining global carbon budget by 2030.

- This June 2023 T&E submission to the European Commission's 2040 climate target consultation sets out six target-design principles (5-year policy cycles; separating emission-reduction targets from carbon-removal targets; including non-CO2 GHGs; covering all emissions with minimal exceptions; adding transport system-efficiency targets; and keeping a global carbon budget as the guiding metric) and recommends a 90% economy-wide emissions cut by 2040.
- Presents detailed sectoral policy inputs behind its scenarios: for cars, CO2 standards of 80% by 2030 (vs. the Commission's 55%) plus a 50% zero-emission corporate-fleet mandate by 2027; for trucks, extending CO2 standards to currently-exempt vocational/small-truck segments; for shipping, well-to-wake fuel-intensity cuts reaching 48% by 2030 (vs. FEUM's 14.5%) partly via mandated slow steaming (18% emissions cut) and other efficiency measures (10%).
- Notes a menu of demand-management "system efficiency" measures quantified in a prior 2021 T&E RePowerEU response — e.g. a 50% cut in corporate travel, EU-wide truck speed limits of 80km/h, aerodynamic trailer fittings — as tools regulators should consider using targets/standards for, not just carbon pricing, since pricing alone doesn't drive efficiency once vehicles are already zero-emission.
- Argues carbon dioxide removals (CDR) must be tracked and targeted separately from emissions cuts, since the climate system responds asymmetrically to emissions versus removals and CDR permanence remains uncertain — recommending no flexibility to offset ESR/ETS mitigation targets with removals.

# T&E's response to roadmap consultation on the inception impact assessment for the Revision of Directive (EU) 2018/2001 on the promotion of the use of energy from renewable sources

*Consultation response · Brussels · 2020-09-25*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/tes-response-roadmap-consultation-inception-impact-assessment-revision-directive-eu) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/090166e5d3c39687.pdf)

> In the context of the EU Green Deal, T&E welcomes the Commission's intention to revise the Renewable Energy Directive and, in particular, its transport aspects. While we acknowledge that the adopted REDII is a step in the right direction concerning renewable transport fuels, we consider there are risky loopholes and lack of ambition in several aspects, for instance, the fact that food and feed based biofuels can still be used and counted towards the targets.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- T&E flags concern over a proposed 24% renewables-in-transport (RES-T) target referenced in the Commission's Climate Target Plan, arguing this is too high to be met sustainably without driving continued reliance on crop-based biofuels.
- Under RED II, only a 7% share of the (then) 14% RES-T target is binding for advanced fuels specifically.
- T&E cites a DG Energy-commissioned study finding all vegetable-oil biodiesel feedstocks produce more GHG emissions than the fossil fuel they replace once indirect land use change (ILUC) is counted, with palm oil the worst performer, closely followed by soy oil.
- RED II's Renewable Electricity multiplier for electricity supplied to road vehicles is 4x; T&E notes EVs are forecast to reach roughly 15–20% of new sales in some member states by the time of the target period.
- T&E calls for a uniform minimum GHG savings threshold of 70% (matching the RFNBO/RCF standard) to apply across all alternative fuels counted toward RED targets.
- For aviation specifically, T&E reiterates its recommendation (from its ReFuelEU work) of a feasible 1–2% e-fuels mandate by 2030, and proposes capping agricultural-residue-based advanced biofuels at 1% of onboard energy by mass for shipping under a proposed Carbon and Energy Intensity Standard.

- This is T&E's September 2020 response to the European Commission's roadmap consultation ahead of a planned revision of the Renewable Energy Directive (RED II), covering the directive's transport provisions.
- Central recommendation: replace the current volume/energy-based RES-T target with a GHG-intensity (carbon-based) target — similar to the older Fuel Quality Directive model — arguing this better rewards genuinely low-carbon fuels rather than simply "renewable-labelled" ones, and cautioning against setting a new target (like the 24% floated in the Climate Target Plan) before a robust sustainability/availability impact assessment is done.
- T&E calls for a full phase-out of food/feed-based crop biofuels from RED eligibility by 2030, an accelerated phase-out of "high ILUC-risk" biofuels (including reclassifying soy as high-ILUC-risk) by 2021, and elimination of the "low ILUC-risk" certification loophole it says is unverifiable and fraud-prone.
- On advanced biofuels, T&E calls for tightening RED II's Annex IX eligible-feedstock list (excluding e.g. whole/round wood), applying cascading-use and sustainable-removal-rate principles for agricultural/forestry residues, and stronger chain-of-custody verification to prevent unsustainable feedstocks being mislabelled as "advanced."
- T&E also opposes promoting energy crops (RED II Annex IX categories p/q) unless grown on genuinely abandoned/marginal land, and opposes scaling up biogas/biomethane in transport (arguing it would mostly displace as fossil gas use given limited sustainable biogas feedstock, with only a niche role in local closed-loop projects).
- On RFNBOs, T&E calls for consistent, harmonised sustainability/GHG-savings standards across RED II, ReFuelEU Aviation and FuelEU Maritime, warning that increasing road-transport renewable targets could crowd out feedstock availability needed for aviation/shipping decarbonisation, and vice versa.
- T&E recommends mandating that all member states offer a "credit mechanism" so fuel suppliers can count renewable electricity toward RED targets (moving beyond pure biofuel-blending mandates), given rapid EV market growth.
- See [Which renewable transport fuels deliver substantial carbon reductions?](which-renewable-transport-fuels-deliver-substantial-carbon-reductions.md) for T&E's detailed technical positions on RFNBO/RCF GHG accounting referenced here, and [How EU legislation can drive an uptake of sustainable advanced fuels in aviation](how-eu-legislation-can-drive-an-uptake-of-sustainable-advanced-fuels-in-aviation.md) for the aviation-specific e-fuels mandate figures repeated in this response.

# The costs of EU ETS and Corsia for European aviation

*Report · Brussels · 2020-09-23*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/costs-eu-ets-and-corsia-european-aviation) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/2020_09_Addition_to_report_Costs_EU_ETS_CORSIA_for_European_aviation.pdf)

> A study commissioned by T&E and conducted by TAKS analysed the cost impact for airlines of applying two options to integrate ICAO's offsetting scheme (Corsia) alongside the EU emissions trading system (EU ETS).

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Two ETS/CORSIA scenarios modelled: (1) ETS retained for intra/domestic EEA + EEA–Switzerland flights, CORSIA applied to outbound flights only ("clean cut," the Commission's own option); (2) ETS retained as-is, CORSIA applied to both intra-EEA and outbound flights.
- Price scenarios: high case — ETS allowances rising to ~€43 (real terms) by 2024/25 then stabilising, CORSIA offsets at US$8 (2020) rising to US$15 (2030); low case — ETS allowances flat at ~€20, CORSIA offsets at US$6 (2020) rising to US$10 (2030).
- ETS free-allowance phase-out assumed: an 8.5%/year reduction 2021–2030, reaching full auctioning by 2030 (per the European Green Deal commitment).
- COVID-19 recovery assumption: a "medium" scenario (per IATA/ACI Europe) with aviation emissions back to 2019 levels by 2024.
- CORSIA's baseline change to 2019-only (from a 2019/2020 average) roughly halves expected offset demand: from 122.2 Mt to 64 Mt CO2 for outbound-only application, and from 201.7 Mt to 100 Mt CO2 if applied intra-EEA too; airlines face no offsetting obligation at all in CORSIA's first four years.
- Applying CORSIA to outbound flights only would cost the whole airline sector just €47.6m (low case) to €70.6m (high case) per year — about 0.2% of airline operating costs, and (spread over 410m+ extra-EU passengers in 2018) roughly €0.17 added to an international ticket price.
- Over the full 10-year period modelled, the combined ETS+CORSIA (outbound) cost is estimated at €11.2–24.1 billion — well below the ≥€32 billion in COVID-19 bailout money European governments gave airlines in a single year (2020).
- Extending CORSIA to intra-EU routes on top of the ETS adds only €278m (low case) to €412m (high case) cumulatively — about 0.1% of airline operating costs.
- Cross-national data cited: ETS-covered electricity companies also face complementary carbon/energy taxes ranging from 24% (Sweden) to 100% (Greece) of emissions double-covered; for industry the range is 8% (Denmark) to 57% (Greece).
- Airlines' jet fuel tax exemption is estimated at €27 billion/year (if applied to all European outbound flights), against the €900 million estimated value of free ETS allowances in 2019 and the ≥€32 billion in 2020 COVID-19 bailouts.

- This September 2020 T&E report, based on a study by consultancy TAKS, models the cost impact for European airlines of two ways to combine the EU ETS with ICAO's CORSIA offsetting scheme, updated to account for COVID-19's effect on both traffic recovery and CORSIA's revised baseline.
- Central finding/argument: under either integration option, the added cost to airlines from CORSIA is minimal (0.1–0.2% of operating costs) — evidence, in T&E's framing, that CORSIA cannot function as an effective carbon price and that the EU ETS must instead be strengthened to drive aviation decarbonisation.
- T&E's position: given the minimal disclosed cost of compliance, and airlines having received far larger sums in unconditional COVID-19 bailout support, relying on CORSIA rather than a strengthened ETS is politically and socially untenable.
- Policy recommendations: reject reliance on CORSIA as an effective pricing tool; strengthen and extend the EU ETS (per [Revision of the EU ETS for aviation](revision-of-the-eu-ets-for-aviation.md)); introduce kerosene taxation (bilaterally in the near term, EU-wide via the Energy Taxation Directive longer term); and establish mandates for sustainable aviation fuels, particularly e-fuels, under the ReFuelEU initiative.
- See [Revision of the EU ETS for aviation](revision-of-the-eu-ets-for-aviation.md) for T&E's underlying ETS reform proposals this cost study is meant to support, and [Making the aviation ETS fit for purpose](making-the-aviation-ets-fit-for-purpose.md) for the follow-up Öko-Institut study citing this report's cost figures.

# Easyjet's new domestic routes fly in the face of environmental spin

*News · Brussels · 2021-06-29*

> EasyJet launched 12 new domestic routes in the UK this month with one - Birmingham to Newquay - less than 200 miles. Green activists have decried the new services, most of which are viable by train.

## Summary

*(No quantitative findings beyond the route details in the abstract above — this is a short reaction piece to a corporate announcement, not an analytical publication.)*

- This T&E news piece criticises easyJet's launch of 12 new UK domestic routes (including Birmingham–Newquay, under 200 miles) as contradicting the airline's own sustainability marketing, contrasting it with France's ban on short-haul flights under 2.5 hours where rail alternatives exist, a similar Spanish proposal under consideration, and Deutsche Bahn's new rail links from Lufthansa's Frankfurt hub aimed at shifting passengers from air to rail.
- T&E's Andrew Murphy argues airlines' voluntary climate pledges (carbon offsetting, "climate neutrality" claims) are undermined by continuing to launch short-haul routes with viable low-carbon alternatives, and that only binding EU legislation — ending jet fuel tax exemptions, taxing emissions on all flights in and out of Europe, and mandating clean fuels via the upcoming Fit for 55 package — can change this behaviour.
- The piece notes a separate study published that month found the offsetting schemes used by airlines like easyJet "lack credibility," and that e-kerosene had recently been shown to also reduce aviation's non-CO2 climate effects (a major but often overlooked share of its total impact).

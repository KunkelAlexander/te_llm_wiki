# German car industry lags on green steel – Volvo leads the pack

*Original title (German): "Deutsche Autoindustrie hat Nachholbedarf bei grünem Stahl – Volvo an der Spitze"*

*Press release · Germany · 2025-09-04*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/te-deutschland/articles/deutsche-autoindustrie-hat-nachholbedarf-bei-gruenem-stahl-volvo-an-der-spitze) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/Green-Steel-Ranking-IAA.pdf)

*(No abstract was published for this document; summary below written from the source text.)*

T&E and the international Lead the Charge initiative ranked 18 major carmakers on their use of green steel, based on a September 2025 briefing. Volvo topped the ranking by a wide margin, followed by Mercedes-Benz, while other German manufacturers — BMW and Volkswagen — placed only mid-table and Stellantis scored just 3% of the benchmark. No manufacturer currently discloses the actual volumes of green steel it uses; Tesla is the only one to publish full greenhouse-gas data for its steel supply chain, and Volvo the only one with model-level emissions data for steel. EU and US carmakers scored markedly better than manufacturers from Korea, Japan and China, with Honda, Toyota, GAC, BYD, Renault and Stellantis ranking weakest overall.

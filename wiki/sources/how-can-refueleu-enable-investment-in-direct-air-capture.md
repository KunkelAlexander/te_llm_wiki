# How can ReFuelEU enable investment in direct air capture?

*News · Brussels · 2022-03-07*

> Negative Emissions Platform and Transport & Environment issue a joint statement on direct air capture

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The EU's overall target is a 55% cut in net greenhouse-gas emissions by 2030 (vs. 1990), toward climate neutrality by 2050 and net-negative thereafter.
- Proposed amendment: mandate direct air capture (DAC) supply a minimum share of the carbon feedstock within the synthetic (e-kerosene) sub-target, rising from 10% (2030) → 20% (2035) → 40% (2040) → 80% (2045) → 100% (2050).
- Synthetic aviation fuel made from atmospheric (DAC) carbon and renewable electricity can achieve emissions savings as high as 100% versus conventional fossil kerosene.

- This March 2022 joint statement from the Negative Emissions Platform and T&E calls for the EU's ReFuelEU Aviation proposal to include a dedicated DAC carbon-feedstock sub-obligation within its synthetic (e-kerosene) fuel target.
- Core argument: e-kerosene made from atmospheric CO2 is genuinely close to carbon-neutral on a cradle-to-grave basis, but the draft "Fit for 55" package instead favours CO2 reuse/refining from industrial installations, which perpetuates reliance on (and thus a lock-in of) fossil CO2 sources rather than driving DAC to scale.
- Concrete legislative asks: a formal legal definition of DAC in Article 3; the phased mandatory DAC-share schedule above written into Article 4/Annex I; and a new reporting obligation (Article 9) on the origin of carbon feedstock, including the DAC share.
- Companion source: [Why direct air capture holds one of the keys to sustainable aviation](why-direct-air-capture-holds-one-of-the-keys-to-sustainable-aviation.md) sets out T&E's broader case for DAC-based e-kerosene.

# The Billion Euro Aviation Bonanza – Aviation's Participation in the EU ETS

*Report · Brussels · 2013-01-22*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/billion-euro-aviation-bonanza-aviations-participation-eu-ets)

> A new study shows that the aviation industry will receive substantial additional windfall profits from the proposed 'stopping of the clock' for flights to and from Europe under the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS). Airlines should not retain these windfall profits – that would be unjust, self-serving and a betrayal of passengers' contributions to fight climate change - but give them to the UN's Green Climate Fund established to assist developing countries tackle the impacts of climate change.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The scraped fulltext does not preserve the study's specific windfall-profit euro figure (only the title, "The Billion Euro Aviation Bonanza," signals the order of magnitude — billions of euros) — the underlying CE Delft-style study behind this report would need to be consulted directly (via the PDF link above) for the exact number.

- Background: the EU had proposed "stopping the clock" on applying its Emissions Trading System (ETS) to flights to/from Europe (i.e., only covering intra-EU flight segments), a partial climate-diplomacy concession during the ICAO negotiations of the early 2010s.
- T&E's finding/position: this partial exemption would hand the aviation industry substantial windfall profits, because airlines had already built the cost of full-route ETS allowances into ticket prices before the carve-out.
- T&E's recommendation: airlines should not keep these windfall profits; they should instead be redirected to the UN's Green Climate Fund, which supports developing countries in addressing climate change impacts.

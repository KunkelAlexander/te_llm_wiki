# Trenitalia classée meilleure compagnie ferroviaire d'Europe, Eurostar en dernière position

*Report · France · 2024-12-09*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/te-france/articles/trenitalia-classee-meilleure-compagnie-ferroviaire-deurope-eurostar-en-derniere-position) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/European-Ranking-of-Rail-operators-updated_december_20_2024_2024-12-20-152751_ubwn.pdf)

> T&E a réalisé un classement inédit de 27 compagnies ferroviaires en Europe, à partir de plusieurs critères (prix, ponctualité, facilité de réservation…). La SNCF arrive dans le groupe de tête. Eurostar et Ouigo sont dans le dernier wagon, même si cette dernière est bien classée sur les prix. La qualité des services ferroviaires peut être améliorée si les compagnies, les gouvernements et l'UE agissent pour rendre le train plus abordable, ponctuel et simple d'accès.

## Summary

*French-office coverage of T&E's "Mind the Gap! European Rail Operators Services Ranked" report — the full pan-European ranking of 27 operators across 8 criteria.*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Trenitalia, RegioJet and ÖBB rank as the best overall performers among 27 European rail operators evaluated; Ouigo, Hellenic Trains and Eurostar rank worst. Even the top scorer only reached 7.6/10.
- Only 59% of Europeans report being satisfied with rail punctuality/reliability (Eurobarometer); of 25 operators with punctuality data, only 8 (32%) achieve above an 80% on-time rate at a 5-minute delay threshold, and a third of operators don't publicly share cancellation data.
- Ranking weights: ticket price (25%), special fares/reductions (15%), reliability (15%), booking experience (15%), compensation policies (10%), traveller experience (10%), night trains (5%), cycling policy (5%).
- Flixtrain (Germany), Ouigo and RegioJet are the most cost-competitive operators; SBB, Eurostar and GWR are the most expensive — Eurostar charges nearly double the price of comparable long-distance high-speed operators for similar journey lengths, without better service quality (Eurostar scores just 4.7/10 on non-pricing criteria).
- On identical routes, price gaps persist beyond VAT/toll differences: Italo is 1.2x cheaper than Trenitalia (Milan-Rome); Flixtrain is 5.5x cheaper than Deutsche Bahn (Berlin-Hamburg).
- SBB (Switzerland) is the most reliable operator, followed by NS (Netherlands) and SNCB (Belgium); Deutsche Bahn, CP (Portugal) and Snälltåget (Sweden) had the most frequent delays/cancellations; BDZ (Bulgaria) and MÁV (Hungary) provided no delay data at all.
- ÖBB (Austria, 29 night-train lines) and CFR (Romania, 21 lines) lead Europe's night-train revival; about half of ranked operators run no night services.
- SNCB and SBB rank best for cyclist-friendliness; SJ, Renfe, Ouigo, Italo and Eurostar rank worst.
- EU rail passenger-rights rules require 25%/50% fare compensation for 60-119/120+ minute delays; UK operators legally must refund 50% for 30-59 minute delays and 100% for 60+ minutes, with some (Avanti, GWR) compensating from just 15 minutes — used as a benchmark T&E wants EU rules to match.

- T&E calls on rail operators to sell tickets at least 6 months ahead, allow third-party resale under fair terms, offer more generous delay compensation, and improve cycling/night-train provision; and on the EU/Member States to cut rail tolls and VAT, invest in maintenance/digitalisation (ERTMS), and pass the Single Digital Booking and Ticketing Regulation.

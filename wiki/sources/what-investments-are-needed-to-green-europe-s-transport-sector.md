# What investments are needed to green Europe's transport sector?

*Report · Brussels · 2024-11-04*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/what-investments-are-needed-for-sustainable-transport-in-the-eu) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/202411_investment_needs_study.pdf)

> Europe needs to shift its public investments from fossil fuel subsidies and road building to green fuels

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- T&E's Net-Zero Scenario for six transport sectors (aviation/shipping fuels, road charging infrastructure, EV production, EV batteries, heavy-duty electrification) needs €310bn/year by 2030 (€1.86 trillion total to 2030), rising to €560bn/year by 2035 and €590bn/year by 2040 (€7.6 trillion cumulative) — roughly 1.9%, 3.5% and 3.7% of 2022 EU GDP respectively.
- 87% of the 2030 investment need (€235bn, or €39bn/year) would need to come from private capital; public finance (EU + national) would need to contribute the remaining ~13% — only slightly more than the EU's existing €34bn/year in fossil-fuel subsidies to transport.
- The European Commission separately estimates €870bn/year in transport investment is needed by 2050; Institut Rousseau estimates €689bn/year; I4CE estimates a lower €253bn/year by 2030 (excluding aviation/shipping) with a €147bn gap.
- Aviation: €313bn needed by 2040; by 2030, e-kerosene capital investment alone needs €51bn, against only ~€20bn in expected early fuel revenue, leaving a €51bn public-private funding gap (T&E proposes public sources cover ~73% of it). 25 large-scale e-kerosene projects in Europe have a potential 1.7 Mt production capacity by 2030 — exceeding the ReFuelEU targets of 0.6 Mt (2030) and 1.0 Mt (2032).
- Aviation's 2022 "tax gap" (untaxed/exempted fuel and pricing) was €34.2bn; T&E proposes redirecting 25% of this (€8.6bn by 2030) plus 25% of aviation ETS revenues (€11.2bn by 2030) — a combined €19.8bn — to fund e-kerosene.
- Shipping: €559bn needed by 2040 (€21bn/year by 2030, rising to €50bn/year by 2040); the extra capital needed to kickstart e-fuel plants by 2030 is €25–35bn, of which T&E estimates public funding could cover ~86% (~€30bn), mostly via ETS revenues and the Innovation Fund.
- Under the EU Taxonomy's current (2023) aviation Technical Screening Criteria, T&E estimates over 90% of Airbus's order book and nearly a third of Ryanair's future fleet could qualify as "sustainable" investments despite running on fossil fuel.
- Recommends roughly doubling EU grid investment to €67bn/year through 2050, while halving 2021-level road-expansion funding (€61bn total that year) to save ~€30bn/year — enough to offset the extra grid spending.
- Transport was 29% of EU GHG emissions in 2022, projected to reach 44% by 2030; road transport (cars/trucks) is 70% of transport emissions.

- T&E (Sustainable Finance team) argues EU public funds should catalyse — not replace — private investment via existing instruments (Innovation Fund, InvestEU, EIB), with guarantees/CfDs to de-risk early-stage e-fuel projects, while redirecting money away from airport/road expansion and fossil subsidies.
- Calls for a "Sustainable Transport Investment Plan" under the new Commission, simplified EU funding criteria, and a revamped EU Sustainable Finance/Taxonomy framework with stricter aviation criteria to avoid rewarding fossil-fuel lock-in as "green."

## Summary

*German-office press release announcing the same T&E/Ricardo analysis as the full English-language report, [Cleaning up steel in cars: why and how?](cleaning-up-steel-in-cars-why-and-how.md) — see that page for the complete figures.*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Green steel could cut EU automotive production CO2 emissions by 6.9 million tonnes by 2030 — equivalent to 3.5 million combustion cars' annual emissions.
- Switching to 40% green steel adds only €57 per EV in 2030; moving to 100% green steel by 2040 would add just €8 more than conventional steel, thanks to carbon pricing and falling green-steel production costs.
- The automotive sector consumes 17% of EU steel, making it the industry's second-largest steel consumer.
- Europe is projected to be able to produce up to 172 million tonnes of green steel/year by 2030 — more than enough to cover the automotive sector's entire 2022 steel consumption of ~36 million tonnes.
- T&E calls for a mandatory, rising green-steel share in new cars: at least 40% from 2030, 75% from 2035, and 100% from 2040.

- T&E Germany (steel policy officer Lars Andersen) argues building a green steel industry costs "less than a tyre change" per car, but needs binding EU-wide demand commitments from carmakers to justify the billions in investment required, building on Germany's own "Green Lead Markets" concept championed by Minister Habeck.

# Consultation response to the CAA's call for evidence on consumer environmental information

*News · Brussels · 2023-04-03*

> Transport & Environment's (T&E) response to the questions posed by the Civil Aviation Authority's call for evidence on consumer environmental information

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- UK aviation carbon emissions in 2019 were over 220% of 1990 levels, versus overall UK emissions at 67% of 1990 levels — a stark divergence T&E attributes to weak aviation-specific regulation.
- 88% of people feel it's too hard to make sustainable choices (partly due to limited information), and 86% want government/businesses to do more to help them, per research cited by T&E.
- Academic research on transatlantic flights found roughly 50% of them produce warming contrails, illustrating why route-specific non-CO2 data (where available) should inform a standard multiplier for flights lacking such data.

- This April 2023 T&E submission to the UK Civil Aviation Authority (CAA) responds to its consultation on consumer environmental information for flight bookings, arguing current information is unhelpful, non-standardised and fails to reflect flights' full climate impact.
- Core recommendations: display environmental information as prominently as price, at the earliest point in the booking process; use one standardised methodology across all airlines/booking platforms; include non-CO2 effects (via a standard multiplier where route-specific data is unavailable); for UK/European routes, show comparative emissions for the equivalent car/train journey; and express a flight's emissions as a percentage of an average Briton's annual footprint.
- Strongly opposes traffic-light-style "green tick" comparisons between flights (as currently used by Skyscanner), arguing this implies some flights are environmentally "good," and insists fossil-kerosene and SAF-powered flights be labelled identically since SAF combustion emits the same GHGs as fossil kerosene at point of use — green labels should be reserved for genuinely zero-emission (electric/hydrogen) flights.
- Recommends a mandatory requirement for airlines to supply the CAA with the same fuel-burn/route data they already report for CORSIA, with fines for non-compliant airlines or booking platforms, and calls for extending climate-impact disclosure to airline promotional material, mirroring existing car-industry obligations.

# Joint letter for the Clean Industrial Deal to deliver on decarbonisation and competitiveness

*Letter · Brussels · 2025-02-05*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/joint-letter-for-the-clean-industrial-deal-to-deliver-on-decarbonisation-and-competitiveness)

> An alliance of cleantech businesses and civil society proposes 15 measures to deliver a joint roadmap for Europe's decarbonisation and competitiveness under the Clean Industrial Deal

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- 15 organisations (including T&E) sent the letter ahead of the Clean Industrial Deal's (CID) planned 26 February 2025 publication date, proposing 15 concrete recommendations across three pillars.

*(No further quantitative findings — this is a policy-recommendation letter, not a data-driven report.)*

- Three pillars proposed: (1) increasing access to clean, affordable domestic energy; (2) a sector-specific European industrial strategy advancing decarbonisation and competitiveness; (3) a "Clean Investment Plan" to unlock private investment via financial incentives and de-risking mechanisms.
- T&E's priorities within this: targeted financial support (with "Made-in-EU" and sustainability conditions attached to State Aid/EU funding, especially the future European Competitiveness Fund) to scale strategic technologies; green public procurement to create lead markets; and de-risking/competitive-bidding mechanisms via InvestEU and the Innovation Fund.
- Calls the future European Competitiveness Fund the "financial backbone" needed to scale domestic cleantech manufacturing in sectors including batteries and clean aviation/shipping fuels, and argues the CID must secure substantial new funding beyond the next EU budget cycle to close the investment gap through 2030.

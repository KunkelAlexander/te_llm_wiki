# A football star's Marie Antoinette moment and an uprising against private jets

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2022-09-30*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/a-football-stars-marie-antoinette-moment-and-an-uprising-against-private-jets)

> Calls for bans on private jets as celebrities' flying habits are revealed. But could they be testing ground for green aviation instead?

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Private jets are about 10x more polluting than commercial flights, per T&E's 2021 analysis.
- T&E calculates that a ticket tax plus a fuel tax on private flights departing France could raise €660 million by 2030.
- Italy's existing "Luxury Tax" (in place since 2012) on executive charter flights charges €10 for legs under 100km, €100 for under 1,500km, and €200 for longer trips.

- This September 2022 op-ed uses PSG footballer Kylian Mbappé's viral laughing dismissal of why his team flew (rather than took a 2-hour train) to a match, plus the @CelebJets Twitter account exposing celebrities' short private-jet hops (e.g. Kylie Jenner's 17-minute flight), as a hook for T&E's position on private aviation.
- Argues an outright private-jet ban is unrealistic, but proposes private jets could instead serve as an early testing ground for green aviation — since wealthy owners can afford early-stage expensive fuels like e-kerosene, and electric planes suit the short island-hopping/intercity trips many private jets are used for.
- Concrete policy ask: apply a ticket tax and a fuel tax to all private flights departing Europe, using the revenue to fund greener aviation alternatives, and require only "green" private jets be permitted in EU skies by 2030.
- Notes the French transport minister had, following that summer's public backlash, already raised the issue at EU transport-minister level, expecting debate over bans versus taxation.

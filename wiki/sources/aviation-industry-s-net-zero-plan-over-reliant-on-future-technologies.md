# Aviation industry's net zero plan over-reliant on future technologies

*News · Brussels · 2021-02-25*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/aviation-industrys-net-zero-plan-over-reliant-future-technologies)

> Europe's airline industry launched a plan to reach carbon neutrality by 2050. Launched with much fanfare, the document identifies the potential of clean fuels but is over-reliant on future technologies of which the costs are still unknown, says T&E.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The industry's "Destination 2050" plan claims 92% of aviation's carbon emissions could be addressed through clean technologies (hydrogen, hybrid-electric propulsion, SAFs), carbon pricing and improved air traffic management, with the remaining emissions offset via carbon removal.
- Aviation's non-CO2 effects are cited as representing two-thirds of the sector's actual climate impact.

- This February 2021 T&E press release reacts to "Destination 2050 – A Route to Net Zero European Aviation," the European aviation industry's own net-zero-by-2050 roadmap, welcoming the industry's acknowledgment that EU ETS reform (cap reduction, ending free allowances) is needed and that global offsetting schemes (i.e. CORSIA) are too weak on their own.
- T&E's core criticism: the plan relies too heavily on the hope that new aircraft technology (particularly hybrid/electric propulsion for short-haul) will be deployed on time, while being largely silent on the costs of decarbonising medium- and long-haul flights.
- T&E's Jo Dardenne argues governments and industry should instead focus on financing and deploying e-fuels (e-kerosene), which she says have significant industrial and environmental potential today and can be blended with existing fuel without aircraft/engine modification, rather than betting on hydrogen aircraft that are "far from being market ready."
- The release notes the plan was published just days after several member states (Netherlands, France, Sweden, Germany, Finland, Luxembourg, Spain) called on the Commission to require fuel suppliers to blend e-kerosene into aviation fuel.
- T&E also flags the plan's near-absence of aviation's non-CO2 climate effects (cited as two-thirds of the sector's actual climate impact) as a significant gap that should be integral to the industry's climate commitment.

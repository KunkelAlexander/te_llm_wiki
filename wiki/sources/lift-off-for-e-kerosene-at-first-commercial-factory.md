# Lift off for e-kerosene at first commercial factory

*News · Brussels · 2021-10-28*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/lift-off-for-e-kerosene-at-first-commercial-factory)

> Production of synthetic kerosene will begin early next year in Germany

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Aviation is responsible for around 2.5% of global CO2 emissions.
- The new Atmosfair e-kerosene plant (Werlte, Germany) will initially produce just 8 barrels/day (~1,300 litres) — a tiny amount compared to pre-pandemic international aviation fuel use.
- Lufthansa is at least one confirmed buyer of the plant's initial output (price undisclosed).

- This T&E news piece covers the opening of the world's first commercial synthetic-kerosene (e-kerosene/"power-to-liquid") factory, built by Atmosfair in Werlte, Germany, with production due to begin in early 2022.
- T&E welcomes the plant as an important proof-of-concept — using water and renewable electricity (the site has strong existing solar/wind generation) to produce hydrogen, which is combined with CO2 (partly sourced from food-waste by-products) to make crude oil and then e-kerosene — but stresses its value lies in demonstrating the technology works, not in its (currently minuscule) output volume.
- T&E's Matteo Mirolo frames scale-up as now depending entirely on policy: calling for the EU to mandate a growing e-kerosene share in jet fuel starting in 2027, to give investors and producers the demand certainty needed to scale from litres to millions of litres.
- T&E reiterates its 2018 Aviation Roadmap position that both technological deployment (like this plant) and demand management are needed simultaneously, with demand management playing the larger near-term role given the current urgency of the climate agenda.

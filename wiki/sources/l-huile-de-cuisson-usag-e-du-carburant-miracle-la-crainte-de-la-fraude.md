# Used cooking oil: from miracle fuel to fear of fraud

*Original title (French): "L'huile de cuisson usagée, du carburant miracle à la crainte de la fraude"*

*Briefing · France · 2024-06-18*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/te-france/articles/lhuile-de-cuisson-du-carburant-miracle-a-la-crainte-de-la-fraude) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/202406_UCO_trade_briefing_final.pdf)

> As the aviation sector increasingly uses used cooking oil to decarbonise, a new analysis shows that available quantities will plateau and will be unable to meet future demand. Worse, some exporting countries such as Malaysia show suspicious export levels, raising fears of fraud with virgin palm oil and therefore a catastrophic climate impact.

## Summary

*T&E's own briefing (France office) built on the commissioned data analysis in the companion report,
[UCO (Unknown Cooking Oil): High hopes on limited and suspicious materials](uco-unknown-cooking-oil-high-hopes-on-limited-and-suspicious-materials.md) — see that page for the full Stratas Advisors trade-flow figures; this page adds T&E's framing and policy recommendations.*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- In 2023, European countries consumed close to 7 million tonnes of UCO for biofuels — about 8x the estimated collected volume and 4x the continent's maximum collection potential.
- UCO made up a third of conventional biodiesel feedstock, a quarter of HVO, and 80% of Europe's (still small but 5x-growing since 2021) sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) demand.
- Ryanair alone, to meet its voluntary 12.5%-SAF-by-2030 target (~1 Mt of fuel), would need more than 1.7 Mt of UCO — equivalent to all of Europe's entire maximum UCO collection potential.
- In 2023, roughly half of Chinese UCO previously exported to Europe was redirected to the US (following the US Inflation Reduction Act), while presumably waste-based biodiesel imports from China to Europe rose 40% in the same period.
- China, Indonesia and Malaysia together collected around 4 million tonnes of UCO in 2023 (over three-quarters from China alone); Malaysia already exports three times more UCO than it collects, a strong signal of fraud.
- The US SAF Grand Challenge targets 3 billion gallons (~9 Mt) of SAF by 2030 — almost 3x the EU's mandate ambition; China is expected to announce a 2–5% SAF blending mandate.
- The UK's SAF mandate caps HEFA (UCO-derived) volumes at 71% of mandated SAF in 2030, falling to 33% by 2040 — T&E calculates this cap will still amount to ~0.9 Mt of fuel in 2030, about ten times the UK's own UCO-SAF maximum potential.

- T&E argues Europe's SAF/HVO/biodiesel mandates for planes, ships, cars and trucks are all competing for the same scarce, fraud-prone UCO feedstock, and that reliance on imported "waste" oil risks acting as a laundering channel for virgin palm oil.
- Recommendations: stop counting non-EU UCO imports toward national renewable targets; keep the EU's 1.7% Annex IX-B cap from being raised; create a dedicated EU fraud-investigation unit; overhaul the voluntary certification-scheme system in favour of stronger EU/national regulation; and prioritise direct electrification (and hydrogen-based fuels for aviation/shipping) over waste-biofuel scale-up.

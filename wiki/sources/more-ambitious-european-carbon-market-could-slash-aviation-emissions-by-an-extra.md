# More ambitious European carbon market could slash aviation emissions by an extra 53%

*Press release · Brussels · 2022-06-02*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/more-ambitious-european-carbon-market-could-slash-aviation-emissions-by-an-extra-53) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/20220523-Environmental-and-economic-impacts-of-EU-ETS-and-CORSIA-policy-scenarios-for-European-aviation-Final-report-1.pdf)

> Europe's carbon market - the EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS) - could deliver up to 53% more emission reductions for aviation in Europe if applied to all departing flights compared to the current plan, and 113% more reduction if extended to both incoming and outgoing flights, a new study finds.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Extending the EU ETS to cover all intra-EEA flights plus all EEA-outgoing flights (replacing CORSIA on those routes) would cut aviation emissions 53% more than the Commission's proposal (EU ETS for intra-EEA only, CORSIA for the rest); extending it further to also cover EEA-incoming flights would cut emissions 113% more.
- 2019 verified EU ETS aviation emissions were 68.2 MtCO2 for intra-EEA flights alone (the pre-pandemic baseline), which fell 63% in 2020 (to 24.92 Mt) due to Covid; global international aviation CO2 fell 56% in 2020 per ICAO data.
- The Commission's proposal phases out free ETS allowances (currently 82% of the total) linearly from 2024 to zero by 2027, with a Linear Reduction Factor (annual cap cut) of 4.2% from 2024.
- CORSIA's pilot phase (2021-2026) baselines emissions at 2019 levels; 107 ICAO member states have committed to the voluntary pilot phase, with 5 more (Brazil, China, India, Russia, Vietnam) expected to join once it becomes mandatory in 2027 — though their participation is uncertain.

- This June 2022 press release summarises an April 2022 study by consultancy TAKS (commissioned by T&E and Carbon Market Watch, using EASA's AERO-MS model) modelling the environmental and economic impact of extending EU ETS coverage for aviation beyond the Commission's "Fit for 55" proposal (EU ETS for intra-EEA flights, CORSIA offsetting for the rest).
- Models two alternative scenarios against the Commission's baseline: (1) EU ETS extended to all EEA-outgoing flights, with CORSIA plus monetary compensation covering the remainder; (2) EU ETS extended to both EEA-outgoing and EEA-incoming flights, again with CORSIA/compensation for what's left.
- Both alternative scenarios assume SAF is counted as zero-CO2 under the Commission's proposed blending mandates (2% in 2025, 5% in 2030, 32% in 2040).
- The study also quantifies allowance/credit purchase costs as a share of total airline operating costs, and compensation costs as a share of additional EU Aviation Allowance auctioning revenue, across both scenarios (see the full report for detailed cost tables).

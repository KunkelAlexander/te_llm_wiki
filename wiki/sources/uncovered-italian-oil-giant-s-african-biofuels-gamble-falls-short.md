# Uncovered: Italian oil giant's African biofuels gamble falls short

*Press release · Brussels · 2024-02-24*

> Investigation into Eni's projects in Kenya and the Republic of the Congo shows company is failing to produce 'miracle' drought-resistant biofuels crops at scale

<!-- ENRICHED SUMMARY: hand-written, preserved across regenerations by 1_build_wiki_subset.py -->
## Summary

*This is the press-release announcement of T&E's full investigative report,
[From Farm to Fuel: inside Eni's African biofuels gamble](from-farm-to-fuel-inside-eni-s-african-biofuels-gamble.md) — see that page for the complete figures and analysis; this page covers the same findings at summary length.*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Eni has failed to reach even a quarter of its 2023 Kenya production target.
- In the Republic of Congo, Eni's projects have been stuck at the pilot stage for more than 18 months.
- Eni's 400,000-farmer, 200,000-tonnes/year-by-2027 Kenya target, and its overall commitment to ship up to 700,000 tonnes/year of vegetable oil from Africa by 2026, are the headline goals the investigation tests against actual progress.
- Eni has earmarked €25bn for oil and gas exploration/development versus less than €3.4bn for biofuels.
- The IFC (World Bank) was considering a $210m loan to Eni Kenya to develop more agri-hubs, undecided at time of publication.

- Summarises the same investigation as the full report: Eni is scaling castor, croton and cotton-seed farming across six African countries to supply its refineries and new SAF deals (e.g. with Ryanair), but on-the-ground interviews with Kenyan and Congolese farmers found under-delivery, inadequate broker/cooperative support, and (in Congo) allegations of land expropriation.
- T&E's oil programme lead Agathe Bounfour is quoted arguing the "miracle" drought-resistant-crop narrative doesn't hold up and won't deliver African development or European energy needs.

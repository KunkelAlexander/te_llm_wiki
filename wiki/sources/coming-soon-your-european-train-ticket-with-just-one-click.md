## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- A single-click international rail booking system, once implemented, is estimated to cut intra-European aviation CO2 emissions by only 6–11%, since it would require international rail to carry 50% more passengers to absorb that shift (citing the author's own "Air2Rail" study).

- Op-ed by T&E's President welcoming Transport Commissioner-designate Apostolos Tzitzikostas's 4 November 2024 pledge to propose EU legislation in 2025 requiring a unified, one-click international rail booking system (comparable to buying a plane ticket).
- Argues the "closed strongholds" of national railway companies — reluctant to share transport data, support an independent neutral rail planner, or accept EU-level rail-capacity allocation and cross-carrier delay compensation — have been the main obstacle, and that only EU-level regulation (as with the universal phone charger) can force this open.
- Frames the reform as a major convenience and competition win for rail passengers, but explicitly modest as a climate measure on its own — one piece of a broader push needed to shift flights to rail.

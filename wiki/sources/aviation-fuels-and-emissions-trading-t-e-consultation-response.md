> Calculating the price difference between eligible fuels and kerosene

<!-- ENRICHED SUMMARY: hand-written, preserved across regenerations by 1_build_wiki_subset.py -->
## Summary

*(No quantitative findings — this is a short policy consultation response, not a data-driven report.)*

- T&E's response to a draft EU ETS delegated act setting rules for calculating the yearly price difference between eligible SAF/e-kerosene and fossil kerosene, used to allocate free emissions allowances to airlines that use these fuels.
- Welcomes the proposal's scope (limiting the allowance benefit to operators actually surrendering ETS allowances), its transparency requirements (airlines must acknowledge/disclose the support received, and provide proof of fuel purchase details if reporting actual prices), and its fallback pricing method when EASA market-price data is unavailable.
- Main criticism: the mechanism as designed risks primarily benefiting biofuels (e.g. waste-oil-based HEFA) rather than e-kerosene, since biofuels need less support to reach cost parity — despite e-kerosene being the more sustainable, scalable SAF type that needs targeted help to reach final investment decision.
- Recommends the Commission create a dedicated allowance "basket" specifically for e-kerosene, and complement the mechanism with additional measures (beyond closing the price gap) to foster long-term offtake agreements that de-risk e-kerosene investment.

# La Unión Europea acuerda la adopción del mandato más ambicioso del mundo en materia de combustibles verdes para la aviación

*Press release · Brussels · 2023-04-26*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/la-union-europea-acuerda-la-adopcion-del-mandato-mas-ambicioso-del-mundo-en-materia-de-combustibles-verdes-para-la-aviacion)

> Los negociadores de la Unión Europea (UE) han aprobado un mandato para el uso de combustibles verdes en el sector de la aviación y abre así la puerta a un mercado europeo de este tipo de combustibles.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- ReFuelEU Aviation's final agreed SAF blending mandate: 2% in 2025, rising to 6% in 2030, and 70% by 2050.
- The synthetic-fuel (e-kerosene) sub-mandate was set at 1.2% for 2030-2031 and 2% for 2032-2035 — a marked increase from the Commission's original flat 0.7% (2030-2035) proposal, credited as a European Parliament win.

- This is the Spanish-language T&E press release on the same April 2023 ReFuelEU Aviation trilogue agreement covered in [Aviation: the EU agrees one of the world's most ambitious mandates for sustainable fuels](aviation-l-ue-s-accorde-sur-l-un-des-mandats-les-plus-ambitieux-au-monde-en-mati.md) and [EU adopts world's largest regulation for green fuels in aviation](eu-beschlie-t-weltweit-gr-te-verordnung-f-r-gr-ne-kraftstoffe-in-der-luftfahrt.md) — see those pages for the fuller summary, including T&E's concerns about the lack of a used-cooking-oil cap and the risk of animal-fat feedstocks displacing demand toward palm oil.
- Also notes the final feedstock definition excludes food crops and palm oil by-products (PFAD) but retains animal fats and used cooking oil (UCO), both limited in supply.
- Frames non-CO2 effects' inclusion (via fuel-quality regulation on aromatic/sulphur content) as historic, since two-thirds of aviation's climate impact had previously gone unregulated.

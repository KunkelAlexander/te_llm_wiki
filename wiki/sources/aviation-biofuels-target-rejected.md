# Aviation biofuels target rejected

*News · Brussels · 2017-10-30*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/aviation-biofuels-target-rejected)

> A push by ICAO and some of its members to set quantity and reduction targets for alternative aviation fuels such as biofuels, was rejected at a major conference convened by ICAO in Mexico City in October. 25 countries, many from Europe, refused to back the UN agency's originally proposed '2050 Vision on Sustainable Aviation Fuels' that included volume-based targets for 2025, 2040 and 2050. The International Coalition for Sustainable Aviation, of which T&E is a member, said the targets were selected based on poor analysis, and grossly overestimated the environmental benefits and potential emissions reductions.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- **25 countries**, many from Europe, refused to back ICAO's proposed volume-based fuel targets at the October 2017 Mexico City conference.
- Rejected targets spanned three milestone years: **2025, 2040 and 2050** ("2050 Vision on Sustainable Aviation Fuels").

- Same underlying event as [Countries reject plan for aviation biofuels targets](countries-reject-plan-for-aviation-biofuels-targets.md) (published two weeks earlier) — this is T&E's own news write-up rather than the ICSA statement itself.
- T&E/ICSA's assessment: the rejected targets were selected based on poor analysis and grossly overestimated the environmental benefits and potential emissions reductions achievable from alternative aviation fuels at that scale and timeline.
- Positions the rejection as a win for a more cautious, quality-over-quantity approach to scaling alternative aviation fuels, consistent with T&E's broader stance that fuel volume mandates should not outpace genuinely sustainable feedstock supply (see also [How to incentivise renewable aviation fuels through the Renewable Energy Directive](how-to-incentivise-renewable-aviation-fuels-through-the-renewable-energy-directi.md) for T&E's parallel EU-level position on the same tension).

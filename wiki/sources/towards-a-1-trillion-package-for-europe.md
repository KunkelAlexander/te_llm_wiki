# Towards a €1 trillion package for Europe

*Briefing · Brussels · 2024-04-23*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/towards-a-eur1-trillion-package-for-europe) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/TE_-Investment-Plan_April-2024.pdf)

*(No abstract was published for this document; summary below written from the source text.)*

T&E proposes a Climate and Social Investment Plan to raise €1 trillion between 2025 and 2034, mostly through €850 billion in joint EU borrowing (EU Green Bonds), to close the European Commission's estimated €620 billion annual climate investment gap after the Recovery and Resilience Facility ends in 2026. The plan splits funding between a Green Industry pillar — scaling up batteries, smart grids, renewables and their mineral supply chains — and a Social and Just Transition pillar addressing energy poverty and the socioeconomic impacts of decarbonisation.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The European Commission estimates €620bn in additional annual investment is needed this decade to meet EU climate commitments; energy and transport investment needs alone average €1.5 trillion/year, and social infrastructure needs €192bn (1.3% of EU GDP).
- T&E's proposed Climate and Social Investment Plan would raise €1 trillion from 2025–2034: €850bn in new joint EU borrowing (EU Green Bonds) plus €150bn from existing/anticipated instruments (Social Climate Fund, Innovation Fund, InvestEU).
- Split into a **Green Industry Fund (€400bn)** — €300bn topping up the Innovation Fund, €100bn (tripling) the InvestEU guarantee fund — and a **Social and Just Transition Fund (€620bn)** — raising the Social Climate Fund from €87bn to €150bn, plus a "RRF 2.0" channelling resources to Member States.
- Current total EU climate-investment capacity is estimated at €225.4bn/year (1.4% of EU GDP); the plan would raise this to a 71% increase, from an estimated €131.5bn/year (2023) to €225.4bn/year.
- For comparison: the US Inflation Reduction Act could total $1.2 trillion in subsidies by 2031, and cleantech investment in the US reached $225bn in 2023 alone.
- Cost of inaction: the EU could lose 7% of GDP by 2100 from unmitigated climate change; coastal flood damages alone could exceed €1 trillion/year (European Environment Agency).
- EIB Group climate operations reached €49bn (55% of its €88bn total operations) in 2023, aiming for a minimum 50% climate/environmental-sustainability share by 2025.
- Proposes raising the EU's Multiannual Financial Framework climate earmarking from 30% to 50%, and InvestEU's climate earmarking from 30% today to 60% by 2028 and 80% by 2034.

- Repayment of the new EU debt is proposed via two revenue streams: a larger share of ETS (carbon market) revenues, and new "Own Resources" (e.g. a permanent solidarity levy on fossil fuels, digital taxation, a common EU corporate tax, or a wealth tax).
- Explicitly recommends excluding fossil fuels (including biofuels, biogas and non-renewable/"blue" hydrogen) and new road/airport capacity from eligible funding.
- Recommends the incoming European Commission table a proposal within its first 100 days (by end 2024), with the bond programme starting in 2025 and the full plan rolling out in 2026 alongside the next EU budget cycle (2028–2034).

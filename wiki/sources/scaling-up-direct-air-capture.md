# Scaling up Direct Air Capture

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Estimated European CO2 demand for e-kerosene/e-crude manufacture ranges from 99 to 313 MtCO2/year, depending on scenario (ReFuelEU's proposed mandate vs. T&E's preferred, more ambitious targets).
- Existing (fossil, via steam methane reforming) and growing biogenic CO2 supply are projected to fall short of demand starting in 2030, with the deficit expected to be filled entirely by Direct Air Capture (DAC).
- By 2050, Europe's biogenic CO2 capture potential is estimated at roughly 330 MtCO2/year, but only about half of that (165 Mt) is assumed available for utilisation (fuel production) rather than dedicated to permanent storage for negative emissions.
- The Fischer-Tropsch process used to make e-kerosene only yields e-crude that is about 50% (by energy) usable as jet fuel, with the remainder becoming other products like synthetic diesel.

- This July 2022 T&E report, based on a Ricardo Energy & Environment study, models the EU+UK's CO2 supply and demand balance out to 2050 to establish that Direct Air Capture (DAC) — not point-source or biogenic carbon capture — must become the dominant carbon source for e-kerosene as demand scales up.
- Central argument: existing CO2 supply is dominated by fossil steam methane reforming (SMR), which will be phased out as hydrogen production shifts to low-carbon methods, while biogenic supply is finite and highly uncertain in scale; only DAC can grow to meet the multi-hundred-MtCO2/year demand implied by ReFuelEU's e-kerosene mandate plus emerging demand for other e-fuels, chemicals, plastics, construction materials and horticulture.
- Frames DAC's near-term deployment as urgent and non-negotiable given the 2030 supply-demand crossover point identified in the modelling — reinforcing the DAC mandate schedule T&E proposed in [How can ReFuelEU enable investment in direct air capture?](how-can-refueleu-enable-investment-in-direct-air-capture.md) (10% of e-kerosene carbon feedstock from DAC by 2030, rising to 100% by 2050).
- Also assesses the optimal geographic/economic placement of DAC relative to CO2 utilisation, including co-location of DAC, electrolysers and Fischer-Tropsch synthesis plants (e.g. in industrial clusters) to minimise costs and emissions.

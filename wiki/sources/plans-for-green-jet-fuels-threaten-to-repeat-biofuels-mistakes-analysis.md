# Plans for 'green' jet fuels threaten to repeat biofuels mistakes – analysis

*Press release · Brussels · 2020-12-16*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/plans-green-jet-fuels-threaten-repeat-biofuels-mistakes-analysis)

> Many European governments are getting serious about developing sustainable jet fuels to tackle the climate impact of flying. But a new report shows Spain, the Netherlands, Sweden and Finland risk repeating the biofuels fiasco with plans to promote crop-based fuels that cause more carbon emissions than the fossil fuels they replace. Transport & Environment (T&E), which analysed seven national plans for sustainable jet fuel, said only Germany focuses on developing near zero-carbon electrofuels for aviation.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Same underlying seven-country SAF mandate comparison as [Making aviation fuel mandates sustainable](making-aviation-fuel-mandates-sustainable.md) — no country-specific figures beyond the country ratings are given in this press release's own text (see that source page for the full target/percentage table).

- This is one of T&E's two press releases announcing its "Making aviation fuel mandates sustainable" study, framing the finding that Spain, Sweden and Finland risk repeating the road-biofuels "fiasco" by promoting crop-based fuels, while France and Norway (despite excluding crop biofuels) risk over-relying on limited sustainable advanced-biofuel feedstocks like used cooking oil.
- T&E singles out Germany as the only country on the right path (excluding crop-based fuels and focusing on e-kerosene) but calls on it to commit to 100% renewable-electricity e-fuels sourced via Direct Air Capture rather than waste CO2 from fossil industry.
- T&E's Andrew Murphy calls for the Commission's incoming ReFuelEU mandate to centre on e-fuels made from green hydrogen and direct-air-captured carbon, with a realistic, carbon-intensity-based (not energy-based) target.
- See [Green jet fuel plans risk history repeating itself](green-jet-fuel-plans-risk-history-repeating-itself.md) for T&E's other press release on this same study, and [Making aviation fuel mandates sustainable](making-aviation-fuel-mandates-sustainable.md) for the full briefing with country-by-country target figures and ratings.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Direct flights to East and South East Asia — 45 MtCO2 of emissions, or up to 15.4% of total EU return-flight emissions (92,000 of ~3.7 million annual flights) — were identified as the routes with greatest theoretical carbon-leakage risk via stop-overs in Dubai or Istanbul.
- No direct flight was found to have a financial advantage from evading via Dubai/Istanbul: a direct East Asia return flight costs an airline €212,000 versus €257,000 (Dubai) or €234,000 (Istanbul) with a stop-over, once extra fuel, crew and aircraft costs are included.
- Long-haul flights to North America via a UK stop-over (Heathrow) were also assessed as low-risk, given UK airport capacity constraints and the UK's own planned ETS/SAF-mandate alignment with the EU.

- This is the short article/abstract version of T&E's January 2022 study quantifying whether EU airlines would evade "Fit for 55" climate measures (EU ETS extension and a SAF mandate applied to all departing flights) by routing long-haul flights through non-EU hubs.

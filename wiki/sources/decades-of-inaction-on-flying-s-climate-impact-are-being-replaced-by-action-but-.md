# Decades of inaction on flying's climate impact are being replaced by action. But does it go far enough?

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2021-07-15*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/decades-of-inaction-on-flyings-climate-impact-are-being-replaced-by-action-but-does-it-go-far-enough)

> It took many years of campaigning, and the efforts of people like Greta Thunberg for politicians to finally start paying attention to aviation emissions; a trio of EU laws and reforms is now supposed to make aviation green. Does it go far enough?

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The proposed jet fuel tax reform exempts 63% of fuel sales (extra-EU flights) and gives airlines a 10-year transition at minimal tax rates before full rates apply; cargo-only flights remain excluded entirely.
- Flights departing Europe (over 60% of aviation emissions) remain outside the reformed EU ETS and would instead fall under an industry-crafted offsetting scheme that the Commission's own research found "completely ineffective."
- ReFuelEU's proposed SAF blending share: starting at 2% in 2025, rising to 5% by 2030, increasing further after 2035 — covering all fuel sold in the EU (including long-haul).
- Aviation's non-CO2 climate effects are cited as responsible for two-thirds of the sector's total warming impact, and are not comprehensively addressed by any of the three proposals.

- This T&E opinion piece assesses the aviation-specific elements of the EU's July 2021 "Fit for 55" package — the jet fuel tax exemption phase-out, EU ETS reform, and the ReFuelEU sustainable-fuels mandate — as a mix of genuine progress and significant loopholes.
- T&E's core critique: all three measures are undercut by exemptions for extra-EU/long-haul flights (which cause the majority of aviation emissions) — the fuel tax and ETS reforms apply only intra-EU, leaving long-haul routes to rely on an offsetting scheme the Commission's own analysis found ineffective.
- T&E credits ReFuelEU as potentially the most consequential of the three because it covers all fuel sold in the EU regardless of destination, and welcomes its e-kerosene sub-target as the only genuinely scalable pathway — but flags the accompanying advanced-biofuels mandate as risky given recent findings that even used-cooking-oil-based fuels are often problematic.
- T&E's remaining asks: scale up the e-kerosene target faster (without compromising sustainability, e.g. via genuinely sustainable CO2 sourcing and stronger industrial support to cut costs) and add a comprehensive programme addressing aviation's non-CO2 climate effects, currently missing from the package despite representing two-thirds of the sector's warming impact.

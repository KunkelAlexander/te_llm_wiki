# EU taxonomy for aviation: Will Von der Leyen rubber stamp the biggest act of aviation greenwashing in decades?

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2023-02-20*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/eu-taxonomy-for-aviation-will-von-der-leyen-rubber-stamp-the-biggest-act-of-aviation-greenwashing-in-decades)

> The European Commission is about to stick a green investment label on thousands of highly polluting planes, it must reverse course.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Aviation CO2 emissions grew 129% between 1990 and 2017, despite new-aircraft energy efficiency improving 18% over the same period — evidence, T&E argues, that efficiency-only criteria don't actually constrain sector emissions growth.
- The draft EU taxonomy criteria could greenwash over 7,000 Airbus aircraft (over 90% of Airbus's future order book) and up to 58% of some airlines' fleets, despite these aircraft running exclusively on fossil kerosene.
- Fuel-efficient "best-in-class" aircraft deliver only around 15-20% emissions savings versus older models — modest gains T&E says don't warrant a "green investment" label.

- This February 2023 op-ed argues the European Commission is poised to approve EU taxonomy criteria for aviation that would let green investment funds flow to conventional, fossil-fuel-burning aircraft simply for being "efficient," comparing this to labelling an efficient coal plant as green.
- Recounts that T&E left the Platform on Sustainable Finance (which drafted these criteria) alongside other NGOs, citing political interference by the Commission overriding science-based advice, after T&E had pushed for criteria centred on zero-emission aircraft and high-sustainability-criteria SAF instead.
- Frames the taxonomy inclusion as still reversible: aviation's inclusion in the EU taxonomy as a whole is "back on the table," giving the Commission an opportunity to revise the criteria rather than lock in what T&E calls the industry's "green get-out-of-jail card" for the next decade.
- Calls on Commission President Ursula von der Leyen specifically to reject the current draft and require taxonomy criteria to strictly endorse technologies with genuine emissions-reduction potential (zero-emission aircraft, high-sustainability SAF).

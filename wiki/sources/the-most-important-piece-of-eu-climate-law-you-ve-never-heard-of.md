# The most important piece of EU climate law you've never heard of

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2022-09-28*

> The delegated act that will define renewable hydrogen and e-fuels

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The Commission's grandfathering exemption (permanently excusing pre-2027 electrolysers from proving additional renewable electricity) applies against a doubled 2030 hydrogen target of 10 million tonnes of green hydrogen produced in the EU — requiring more electricity than all EU27+UK onshore and offshore wind power combined, equivalent to France's total electricity consumption.

- This September 2022 T&E op-ed argues the RFNBO (Renewable Fuels of Non-Biological Origin) delegated act — ostensibly a technical implementing measure — is being quietly hollowed out by hydrogen-industry lobbying, in a repeat of what T&E says happened with biofuels sustainability rules a decade earlier.
- Central complaint: the Commission's "last-minute change of heart" to permanently exempt all hydrogen/e-fuel production units starting before 2027 from the RED's "additionality" requirement (that renewable hydrogen come from genuinely new renewable capacity, not existing grid supply) undermines the entire point of the rule — while simultaneously the 2030 hydrogen target was more than doubled following Russia's invasion of Ukraine, raising the stakes of getting the rule wrong.
- Warns that without additionality, diverted wind/solar capacity will be backfilled largely by gas-fired power, at a time of record household/business electricity prices — directly linking weak RFNBO rules to worsening the EU's energy-price crisis.
- T&E's position: some transition flexibility is reasonable for a nascent industry, but a blanket, permanent exemption goes too far — closing with "#notinmytank," echoing T&E's biofuels-era messaging.

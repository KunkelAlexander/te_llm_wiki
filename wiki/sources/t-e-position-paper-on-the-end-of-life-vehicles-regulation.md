# T&E position paper on the End-of-life Vehicles Regulation

*Briefing · Brussels · 2024-10-30*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/t-e-position-paper-on-the-end-of-life-vehicles-regulation)

> The EU proposal to maximise the efficient use of resources in the car sector is welcome, but some improvements are needed.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The automotive sector consumes 17% of EU steel and 10% of EU plastics; the wider transport sector accounts for 40% of all EU aluminium use.
- Materials account for 16–30% of a car's embedded emissions for steel/iron and 27–30% for aluminium (varying by combustion vs. electric powertrain).
- T&E's headline recycled-content/green-material proposal: a mandatory green-steel target (scrap plus green primary) of 40% in new cars by 2030, rising to 75% by 2035 and 100% by 2040, alongside a maximum 0.06% copper-contamination limit on secondary steel from end-of-life vehicles (ELVs) to keep scrap usable for automotive-grade steel.
- Proposes an equivalent green-aluminium mandate, with an impact assessment starting immediately and secondary-legislation targets adopted no later than 12 months after the regulation enters force.
- Recommends a Euro 4 emissions limit on used vehicles exported to third countries from 2028, tightening to a 5-year maximum vehicle age from 2035.
- Wants the proposed Circularity/vehicle passport (covering material composition, recycled content share and carbon footprint) available by 2027 rather than the Commission's proposed ~2033 timeline (7 years after the regulation's entry into force), and merged with the battery passport and vehicle environmental passport.

- T&E's position paper on the revised End-of-Life Vehicles (ELV) Regulation welcomes the Commission's proposal to boost ELV collection, recycled-material quality/quantity and content in new vehicles, and to curb exports of unroadworthy used vehicles, but argues the draft under-delivers on binding green-material targets, recycling-quality standards, and passport timelines.
- Recommends expanding the list of components (e.g. small electric/electronic equipment, mono-material aluminium parts over 5kg) that must be dismantled before shredding, and requiring Member States to run a Deposit Refund Scheme to boost ELV collection rates.
- Builds directly on T&E's [Cleaning up steel in cars: why and how?](cleaning-up-steel-in-cars-why-and-how.md) report and its green-steel target trajectory.

# T&E's take on the EU Transport Commissioner's hearing

*News · Brussels · 2024-11-04*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/t-es-take-on-the-eu-transport-commissioners-hearing)

> EPP candidate Tzitzikostas drew a line under the EU electric cars debate, saying supporting industry – not weakening targets – is the way forward.

## Summary

*(No quantitative findings — this is T&E's reaction to Transport Commissioner-designate Apostolos Tzitzikostas's European Parliament confirmation hearing, not a data-driven report.)*

- Tzitzikostas (Greece, EPP) committed to sticking with the 2035 zero-emission car target and not weakening the 2025 CO2 targets, promised a clean-industrial transition plan for carmakers, and backed green fuels (via a planned Sustainable Transport Investment Plan) as the main aviation and shipping decarbonisation pathway — but repeatedly declined to commit to a specific EU law on greening corporate car fleets, or to detail how clean-fuel investment would be financed or prioritise e-kerosene over biofuels.
- He rejected a "book and claim" system for aviation SAF compliance, citing fraud/double-counting risk, and voiced support for a single cross-border rail ticketing/booking system (to be proposed as 2025 legislation) — but wouldn't commit to requiring rail operators to share data with independent booking platforms.
- T&E (Executive Director William Todts) criticised the hearing for lacking concrete commitments, and specifically flagged the absence of any discussion of aviation's non-CO2 effects (contrails), calling for a decade-long action plan to eliminate warming contrails.

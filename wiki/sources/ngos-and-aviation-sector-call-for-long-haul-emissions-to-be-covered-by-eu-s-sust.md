# NGOs and aviation sector call for long-haul emissions to be covered by EU's Sustainable Aviation Fuels mandate

*Press release · Brussels · 2021-03-11*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/ngos-and-aviation-sector-call-long-haul-emissions-be-covered-eus-sustainable-aviation-fuels)

> A coalition of airlines, aviation sector companies and European NGOs have expressed their concern over policy proposals to limit the scope of the European Union's ReFuel EU Aviation initiative to intra-EEA flights. The legislation, to be proposed by the Commission, is expected to oblige the aviation sector to mix a percentage of Sustainable Aviation Fuels (SAFs) into kerosene- based fuel.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Per Eurocontrol data cited in the coalition's letter, just 6% of flights — those over 4,000km — generate half of aviation's CO2 emissions; Eurocontrol concludes that raising SAF supply to cover just 10% of long-haul needs would cut more net CO2 than anything achievable in short-haul.

- This is a joint letter (sent to Commission VP Frans Timmermans and Transport Commissioner Adina Vălean) from a coalition spanning airlines (easyJet, Ryanair, Wizz Air, Loganair, Jet2), aviation companies (SkyNRG, Pipistrel, Wright Electric) and NGOs (T&E, Bond Beter Leefmilieu, Natuur & Milieu), calling for the EU's incoming ReFuelEU Aviation SAF mandate to cover all flights departing the EU rather than being limited to intra-EEA routes.
- The coalition's rationale: long-haul flights, already excluded from many EU environmental policies like the ETS, are the dominant source of aviation emissions, whereas SAFs are only an interim decarbonisation measure for short-haul routes (which can eventually shift to hydrogen/electric propulsion by the mid-to-late 2030s) but remain the only foreseeable mitigation option for long-haul flying.
- Signatories also warn that SAF mandates shouldn't delay development of zero-emission short-haul propulsion, and call on the Commission to exclude biofuels from dedicated cropland (with an exception for cover crops) and ensure sustainability safeguards for advanced biofuels alongside sub-targets for synthetic (e-)kerosene.
- The letter includes competing airline perspectives: Ryanair and Wizz Air CEOs argue long-haul/connecting carriers should not be allowed to exempt themselves from a mandate they helped advocate for, while easyJet frames SAFs as an interim solution pending its own zero-emission propulsion ambitions.

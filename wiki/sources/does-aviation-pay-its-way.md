# Does aviation pay its way?

*Report · Brussels · 2013-07-24*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/does-aviation-pay-its-way)

> In these times of austerity, deficit budgets of European governments are missing out on almost €40bn a year due to a lack of basic taxes on aviation. This briefing explains a new study that looks at revenue that EU Member States could receive if fuel tax and VAT were imposed on aviation, as on road transport.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- EU Member States forgo an estimated **almost €40 billion a year** in foregone revenue because aviation is not taxed the way road transport is (no fuel tax, no VAT on tickets).

- The underlying study (commissioned by T&E, published July 2013) models the revenue EU governments could raise if kerosene were subject to fuel tax and air tickets to VAT, on the same basis as road transport fuel and services.
- Framed against the austerity/deficit context of the time: T&E argues this untaxed revenue represents an available, low-controversy fiscal lever governments were overlooking while cutting other spending.
- The briefing does not itself set out country-by-country breakdowns in the available text — the full €40bn figure is presented as an EU-wide aggregate.

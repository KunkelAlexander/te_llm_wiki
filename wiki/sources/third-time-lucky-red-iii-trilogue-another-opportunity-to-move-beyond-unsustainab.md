# Third time lucky? RED III trilogue another opportunity to move beyond unsustainable fuels

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- T&E recommends an 8% GHG-savings target for transport by 2030 (translating to roughly a 16% energy-based target) — well below the Council's proposed 13% GHG (29% energy) target and the Parliament's 16% GHG target, arguing higher targets simply drive more crop-based biofuel use.
- T&E advocates a 1.6% RFNBO (e-fuels/renewable hydrogen) transport target — more realistic than the Commission/Parliament's 5.7% (following RePowerEU's doubling) — while strongly supporting the Parliament's proposed 1.2% mandatory RFNBO sub-target specifically for shipping.
- Soy biofuel expansion affects high-carbon-stock land at a rate of 10.5%, above the threshold for "high ILUC-risk" classification (per Cerulogy 2020 research cited), justifying phase-out alongside palm oil.
- More than 70% of all EV recharging happens at home or the workplace — the rationale for extending RED's renewable-electricity credit mechanism to private charging, not just public.

- This October 2022 T&E briefing sets out priorities for the RED III (Renewable Energy Directive revision) trilogue negotiations between Commission, Council and Parliament, focused on preventing renewed reliance on unsustainable crop-based biofuels as transport-sector renewable targets rise.
- Welcomes the Parliament's proposal for an immediate phase-out of palm and soy biofuels (versus the Commission's palm-only, 2030 phase-out), and calls for the overall food/feed biofuel cap to be reduced to zero by 2030 so palm/soy aren't simply replaced by other crop biofuels, while also closing the "intermediate crop" loophole that could let soy re-enter as an unregulated feedstock.
- Opposes a narrow Parliament amendment that would eliminate the legal basis for a delegated act on RFNBOs, warning this would replace a single EU-wide regulatory framework with 27 separate national transpositions, harming investment certainty for the nascent hydrogen industry (see [Getting hydrogen right from the start](getting-hydrogen-right-from-the-start.md) and [The most important piece of EU climate law you've never heard of](the-most-important-piece-of-eu-climate-law-you-ve-never-heard-of.md) for T&E's parallel critique of that same delegated act's substance).
- Recommends applying a "cascading principle" to Annex IX advanced/waste biofuels (limiting double-counted, already-scarce feedstocks like used cooking oil and animal fats) and retaining a Commission-proposed accounting change that would reduce the advantage given to palm-oil-adjacent by-products like PFAD.

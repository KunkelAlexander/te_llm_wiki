# T&E response to the public consultation on the Flight Emissions Label

*Consultation response · Brussels · 2024-10-21*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/te-response-to-the-public-consultation-on-the-flight-emissions-label)

> The proposed Flight Emissions label is a missed opportunity to allow consumers to make truly informed choices.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- T&E estimates the proposed method for apportioning emissions to belly cargo would hide an additional 4 Mt of CO2 from European consumers — equal to 4% of all passenger emissions.

*(Short consultation response; beyond the cargo-apportionment figure above, this is a critique of methodology rather than a quantitative report.)*

- T&E's response to the EU's draft Flight Emissions Label Implementing Act raises four objections: it again omits aviation's non-CO2 warming effects (contrails, NOx, etc.) from what passengers see; its cargo-apportionment method understates passenger-attributable emissions; comparing a flight's CO2-per-km only to other flights on the same route is misleading whenever a train alternative exists (since it never surfaces the option of not flying at all); and passengers should be told more about a fuel's characteristics than just its life-cycle emissions figure (e.g. what type of SAF is used).

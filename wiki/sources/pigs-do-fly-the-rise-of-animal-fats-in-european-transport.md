# Pigs do fly: the rise of animal fats in European transport

*Report · Brussels · 2023-05-31*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/pigs-do-fly-the-rise-of-animal-fats-in-european-transport)

> Growing demand for animal fats biofuels to power Europe's transport system raises concerns over climate impacts and potential fraud

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Animal fats used in biodiesel grew from 30,000 tonnes/year in 2006 to 1.4 million tonnes in 2021 (a 40x increase), with the share of all rendered animal fats going to biodiesel rising from 1% to 46% over the same period; demand is forecast to almost triple to 3.9 million tonnes by 2030 (Stratas Advisors).
- A fully HEFA-fuelled transatlantic flight (Paris-New York) would theoretically require about 8,800 dead pigs each way (4,400 at the current 50% maximum HEFA blend limit).
- Category 3 animal-fat use in biodiesel grew 160% since 2014 (versus only 36% growth for category 1&2 fats, which are already at full potential) — and 2021 EU data show reported category 1&2 biofuel consumption at nearly double what the animal-fat industry (EFPRA) reported actually supplying in those categories, pointing to likely fraudulent mislabelling of category 3 fats as category 1&2 to gain "double counting" credits worth up to €840/tonne (vs. €420/tonne for correctly-labelled category 3 material, in the Dutch HBE-O certificate system).
- If virgin palm oil substitutes displaced animal fats in oleochemicals, the CO2 emissions of animal-fat biodiesel could be up to 1.7x worse than conventional diesel.

- This May 2023 T&E report, based on research commissioned from Cerulogy, examines the rapid growth of animal-fat-based biofuels (increasingly used in "sustainable aviation fuel" as well as road biodiesel) and finds current EU incentive structures cannot be sustained without displacement effects and fraud risk.
- Central mechanism: EU rules classify animal fats into 3 categories by health risk, with categories 1&2 eligible for "double counting" toward renewable-energy targets — creating a strong financial incentive to mislabel higher-value category 3 material (used extensively by pet food, soap and cosmetics industries) as category 1&2, which the report argues is very likely already happening at scale.
- Notes Stratas Advisors project SAF demand for animal fats could rise 80-fold from 2021 to 2030 (6,000 to 460,000 tonnes/year), making animal fats the most-used "waste" SAF feedstock ahead of used cooking oil (projected at 360,000 tonnes by 2030); major oil companies (Neste, TotalEnergies, Shell) are already converting refineries to process animal fats into SAF.
- Recommendations: cap category 1&2 animal-fat use at 2020 levels and hold the existing 1.7% Annex IX Part B cap; exclude category 3 animal fats entirely from eligible biofuel/SAF/shipping-fuel feedstock lists; apply these safeguards consistently across road, aviation (ReFuelEU) and shipping (FuelEU Maritime); and have the European Commission investigate the labelling-fraud risk with independent, robust auditing.

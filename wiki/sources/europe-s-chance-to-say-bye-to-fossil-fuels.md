# Europe's chance to say bye to fossil fuels

*Briefing · Brussels · 2022-05-05*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/bye_fossil_fuels) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/TE-REpowerEU-position-paper.pdf)

> REpowerEU is an opportunity to create an EU level strategy and support mechanisms to lower oil demand in the near term. In this position paper, T&E highlights top priorities.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The EU imports 95.9% of its crude oil (2020: 18.7 Mt produced domestically vs. 440.3 Mt imported), spending around $1 billion/day, a quarter of which flows to Russia — which derives 75% of its EU energy-export revenue from oil (vs. 25% from gas).
- Transport consumes two-thirds of total EU oil demand; within transport, cars use 30%, trucks/buses 19%, ships 12%, and aviation 4.1%.
- Implementing the IEA's 10-point plan (reduced speed limits, car-free days, less corporate travel, etc.) EU-wide could cut transport oil demand by 11.8%; cutting truck speed limits from 90 to 80 km/h alone could save 5% of truck fuel, with aerodynamic retrofits saving a further ~5%.
- Replacing just 6.5% of the EU's Russian crude oil, gasoline and diesel imports with rapeseed/corn/wheat-based biofuels would require doubling the land area the EU's current biofuel consumption already occupies (currently ~4% of all EU land, 7.5% of EU+UK cropland) — illustrating why T&E says biofuels aren't a scalable substitute.

- This May 2022 T&E briefing sets out priorities for the EU's REpowerEU strategy (designed to cut EU dependence on Russian energy after the invasion of Ukraine), arguing it must target oil, not just gas, and use the crisis to structurally cut transport oil demand rather than simply substitute suppliers.
- Short-term asks: EU-wide adoption of the IEA's 10-point oil-demand-reduction plan, mandatory truck speed-limit reductions (90→80km/h), and an aerodynamic-retrofit incentive/mandate for trucks by 2025.
- Medium-term asks: a new EU regulation requiring large corporate fleets (20+ vehicles) to reach 50% zero-emission new registrations by 2025 and 100% by 2030 (for both cars and vans); and tightened truck CO2 standards (30% cut by 2027, 65% by 2030, versus the Commission's -15%/-30% trajectory) with mandatory zero-emission buses from 2027.
- Long-term/fuel asks: a 41% shipping fuel-efficiency improvement by 2030 plus a 6% e-fuels sub-target under FuelEU Maritime; and earlier, higher ReFuelEU e-kerosene targets (0.04% in 2025, 2% in 2030) than the Commission's draft.
- Explicitly opposes including crop-based biofuels in REpowerEU, citing food-security risk (citing the Commission's own Food Security Strategy) and calls instead for immediate suspension of crop-biofuel use by member states, alongside a push to secure sustainably-sourced lithium/nickel supply chains so raw-materials bottlenecks don't slow EV uptake.

# Why "flying less" offers the best path to sustainable aviation

*Report · Brussels · 2022-03-08*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/2050roadmap)

> Transport & Environment (T&E) publishes its "Roadmap to climate neutral aviation".

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- European aviation traffic grew 67% between 2005 and 2019, with CO2 emissions up 24% over the same period; absent action, emissions are projected to grow a further 38% from 2019 to 2050 even accounting for aircraft efficiency gains.
- Cutting corporate/business travel to 50% of pre-Covid levels could cut CO2 emissions by up to 32.6 MtCO2 by 2030 — equivalent to taking 16 million polluting cars off the road.
- Demand management (an end to airport expansion plus reduced business travel) is identified as the single most effective lever for cutting emissions this decade, though insufficient alone to reach climate neutrality by 2050.

- This March 2022 T&E report ("Roadmap to climate neutral aviation") sets out T&E's preferred pathway to decarbonise European aviation, prioritising demand reduction as the near-term lever while ambitious fuel and technology policy scale up.
- Twin near-term demand pillars: end airport expansion in Europe (identified as a major driver of emissions growth) and halve corporate/business travel versus pre-pandemic levels.
- Beyond demand management, the roadmap calls for: ending aviation's tax exemptions (fossil jet fuel tax, ticket taxes, full ETS coverage) so the sector pays its true climate cost; ambitious SAF mandates favouring e-kerosene (produced from green hydrogen, additional renewable electricity and direct-air-captured CO2) over lower-quality biofuels; a requirement that the industry sell/operate zero-emission (electric/hydrogen) aircraft by 2030; and mitigation of non-CO2 effects (e.g. contrails) via cleaner fossil jet fuel with fewer contrail-forming particles and flight rerouting.
- Frames non-CO2 warming effects (contrails etc.) as often exceeding the warming impact of aviation's CO2 emissions themselves, arguing these are comparatively easy, low-cost near-term fixes.
- See [Why direct air capture holds one of the keys to sustainable aviation](why-direct-air-capture-holds-one-of-the-keys-to-sustainable-aviation.md) and [Can we count on zero-emissions planes and ships?](can-we-count-on-zero-emissions-planes-and-ships.md) for T&E's related work on e-kerosene/DAC and zero-emission aircraft technology referenced in this roadmap.

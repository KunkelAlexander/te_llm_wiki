# Over €200 to fill up a car – the cost of Germany's bid to keep combustion engines

*Press release · Brussels · 2023-03-23*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/over-e200-to-fill-up-a-car-the-cost-of-germanys-bid-to-keep-combustion-engines)

> Chancellor Scholz's support for e-fuel will hit motorists in the pocket while driving up carbon emissions, oil consumption and air pollution.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- E-petrol could cost over €2.80/litre in Germany by 2030 — 50% more than regular petrol today — costing the average driver at least €2,300/year to fill up, versus current fossil-petrol costs.
- Allowing e-fuel-powered combustion cars past the 2035 phase-out could displace sales of up to 46 million zero-emission electric cars by 2050; if e-petrol were used in new cars instead of the existing fleet, the existing fleet would burn an extra 135 billion litres of fossil petrol and emit an extra 320 MtCO2e by 2050.
- Cars running on e-fuels could emit up to 160,000 tonnes of additional NOx pollution across the EU by 2050 — more than Italy's entire car fleet emits in a year.
- The political uncertainty over the e-fuel loophole puts at risk up to €30 billion of battery-plant investment in Germany alone.

- This March 2023 T&E press release (Alex Keynes) is the English-language press release summarising T&E's e-fuels analysis rebutting Chancellor Scholz's push for an e-fuel exemption to the EU's 2035 combustion-engine phase-out — see [E-fuels: a full tank of petrol around 50% more expensive if Germany gets its way](e-fuels-un-plein-d-essence-environ-50-plus-cher-si-l-allemagne-obtient-gain-de-c.md) for the fuller underlying briefing with detailed methodology and additional findings on hydrogen import dependency and carbon-neutrality certification gaps.
- Central argument: exorbitant e-fuel costs mean this would become a "niche solution for Porsche drivers" only, while undermining investment certainty in the EU's broader electrification transition.

# EU governments approve extension of polluter pays principle

*Press release · Brussels · 2022-06-29*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/eu-governments-approve-exension-of-polluters-pay-principle)

> Environment ministers of the EU27 approved a carbon market to regulate key sources of transport emissions, but important exemptions remain.

<!-- ENRICHED SUMMARY: hand-written, preserved across regenerations by 1_build_wiki_subset.py -->

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- EU Environment ministers refused to extend the ETS to extra-EEA flights, leaving over 60% of aviation's pollution without effective carbon pricing.
- Non-CO2 effects — two-thirds of aviation's climate impact — went unaddressed in the Council's position.
- The Council's weakened Effort Sharing Regulation amendments allow member states to emit emissions equivalent to 20 million cars more than the Commission's original 2030 budget.
- Ministers agreed to a new road transport/heating carbon market from 2027, and endorsed extending the ETS to international shipping, though with new exemptions (e.g. ferries serving islands under 200,000 inhabitants).

- This June 2022 T&E press release reports the EU Environment Council's agreement on several "Fit for 55" files (EU ETS, Effort Sharing Regulation, Social Climate Fund), welcoming the shipping ETS breakthrough while criticising the Council for continuing to shield most aviation emissions from carbon pricing.
- Criticises ministers for weakening the Social Climate Fund by dropping its co-financing requirement (halving the revenue guaranteed for low-income households) and removing the requirement that all remaining ETS revenue go to climate measures (opening the door to using it for debt relief or general industry support).
- Also flags that member states missed the opportunity (proposed by Parliament) to split the new road-transport/heating carbon price between fuel suppliers and citizens — which T&E argues would have made oil majors, then posting record profits amid the Ukraine war, share more of the cost.
- Notes methane and nitrous oxide monitoring for ships was agreed but not yet folded into ETS carbon pricing, and that coverage of smaller cargo ships (400-5,000 GT) remains only partial pending further Parliament negotiations.
- See [EU set for major expansion of carbon market](eu-set-for-major-expansion-of-carbon-market.md) for T&E's report on the Council's later, more definitive vote on the same package.

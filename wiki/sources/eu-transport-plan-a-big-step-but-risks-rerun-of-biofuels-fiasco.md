# EU transport plan a big step but risks rerun of biofuels fiasco

*Press release · Brussels · 2020-12-09*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/eu-transport-plan-big-step-risks-rerun-biofuels-fiasco)

> The EU today charted a course for aviation and shipping out of the Covid crisis without fossil fuels. Transport & Environment (T&E) welcomed the European Commission's mobility strategy but said the heavy reliance on biofuels instead of electrofuels for planes and ships risks an ecological disaster similar to the bloc's last biofuels misadventure. On road transport, the strategy is a major step towards the electrification of cars and trucks although the EU executive continues to duck the question of when the last combustion-engine cars will be sold in Europe.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The Commission's Sustainable and Smart Mobility Strategy staff working document projects e-fuels will represent only 0.7–2% of aviation's energy use by 2030 (1% in its "MIX" scenario), with the largest share of renewable/low-carbon aviation fuel coming from liquid biofuels.
- For shipping, the same document projects by 2050: liquid biofuels at 39–40% of the fuel mix, e-liquids adding another 19–20%, low-carbon gases (bio-LNG/e-gas) at 20–22%, and hydrogen at 7–8% — with shipping CO2 intensity (per tonne-km) projected to fall 13–15% by 2030 (MIX/MIX-50 scenarios) and 89–91% by 2050 (ALLBNK scenario) relative to 2015.
- The strategy implies at least 30 million electric cars on European roads by 2030, which T&E says is consistent with raising carmakers' 2030 CO2 reduction target to 50%.
- A cited T&E-commissioned study found Europe could scale up e-fuels production to power all of its planes and ships by 2050.

- This December 2020 T&E press release reacts to the European Commission's Sustainable and Smart Mobility Strategy, welcoming its ambition for aviation/shipping decarbonisation and road electrification but warning its aviation/shipping fuel mix relies too heavily on biofuels rather than e-fuels.
- T&E's William Todts argues this echoes the EU's earlier road-biofuels experience ("Europe's last biofuels adventure was a fiasco causing deforestation worldwide"), and calls renewable hydrogen-based fuels a cleaner alternative that also represents an industrial opportunity, rather than a repeat mistake.
- On cars/trucks, T&E welcomes the strategy's committed electrification direction (including, for the first time, action on corporate/company-fleet decarbonisation and a 2022 revision of truck CO2 standards) but criticises the Commission for not setting a combustion-engine sales phase-out date, contrasting this with the UK, California, and carmakers like Volvo committing to 2035.
- See [Making aviation fuel mandates sustainable](making-aviation-fuel-mandates-sustainable.md) and [How to ensure the sustainability of electrofuels](how-to-ensure-the-sustainability-of-electrofuels.md) for T&E's detailed critique of over-reliance on biofuels versus e-fuels in aviation policy referenced here.

# Two euros are enough to "green" a flight to New York under the UN's aviation programme

*Original title (French): "Deux euros suffisent à « verdir » un vol pour New York avec le programme de l'ONU pour l'aviation"*

*Press release · France · 2022-09-23*

> According to new data from Transport & Environment (T&E), ICAO, the UN's aviation agency, is relying on an offsetting mechanism to decarbonise the sector, adding a modest 2.40 euros to the price of a long-haul flight (1).

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- CORSIA international-aviation emissions were 606.48 MtCO2 in 2019 and 265.24 MtCO2 in 2020 (average 435.86 Mt, i.e. 71.9% of 2019 levels), per ICAO's CORSIA Central Registry.
- Under the analysis, offset obligations start at a 100% "sectoral growth rate" (0% individual) through the pilot/early second phase, shifting to 20% individual growth rate for 2030-2032 and 70% individual for 2033-2035.
- EU ETS allowance prices are modelled rising from ~€54/tCO2 (2021 average) and €80-90/tCO2 (2022) to €100/tCO2 by 2030 and €200/tCO2 by 2050 (with €125/tCO2 assumed for 2035); 6 CORSIA baseline/participation policy scenarios were modelled against an EU ETS full-departing-flights option.
- The modelling assumes an EU ETS aviation cap reflecting ~52% of 2019 verified emissions (mirroring the intra-EEA-only cap ratio: 35.7 Mt cap vs. 68.2 Mt verified 2019 emissions), with a 4.2% Linear Reduction Factor from 2024 and free-allowance phase-out from 82% (2024) to 0% (2027).

- This September 2022 T&E France publication is the methodology/assumptions annex (produced by consultancy TAKS) behind T&E's analysis of CORSIA's weak offsetting ambition ahead of the October 2022 ICAO General Assembly — see [UN body ICAO hails empty goal and cheap offsetting scheme to 'green' aviation](un-body-icao-hails-empty-goal-and-cheap-offsetting-scheme-to-green-aviation.md) for the resulting findings and headline figures (only 22% of international aviation emissions offset by 2030 under the adopted 85%-of-2019 baseline; ~€1.70-€2.40 added to a Europe-New York ticket).
- Documents the underlying AERO-MS model (EASA-owned), data sources (ICAO CORSIA Central Registry, ICAO/IATA recovery forecasts), and the 5 route groups (EEA-US, EEA-Middle East, EEA-China, EEA-Brazil, UK-US) and 6 CORSIA policy variants (3 baseline options × whether 5 major non-signatory countries join the mandatory phase) used to generate the headline results.

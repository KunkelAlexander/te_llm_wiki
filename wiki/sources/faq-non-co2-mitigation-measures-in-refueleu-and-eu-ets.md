# FAQ: Non-CO2 mitigation measures in ReFuelEU and EU ETS

> Why MRVs are key to tackling aviation non-CO2 effects

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Non-CO2 effects (contrails, NOx, soot etc.) account for roughly two-thirds of aviation's total climate impact — a net warming effect about twice as large as aviation's CO2 emissions alone.
- An estimated 80% of contrail-cirrus warming is caused by fewer than 15% of flights, and reducing soot emissions by 80% can cut contrail-cirrus warming by 50%.
- Fossil jet fuel's aromatic content typically ranges from 12-25%, naphthalene 1-3%, and sulphur 300-600 ppm — yet current aircraft can already safely fly on Jet A fuel with just 8% aromatics and no naphthalene or sulphur.
- Optimising jet fuel composition (via hydrotreatment) is estimated to cost less than €0.05/litre, while cutting fuel consumption by roughly 1% thanks to hydrotreated fuel's higher energy density.

- This October 2022 T&E FAQ explains why Monitoring, Reporting and Verification (MRV) schemes in both the EU ETS revision and ReFuelEU Aviation are the necessary first step to finally regulate aviation's non-CO2 climate effects, which have escaped policy attention for decades despite being scientifically well-understood.
- Proposes a non-CO2 MRV pilot scheme under the EU ETS (tracking aircraft trajectory, fuel flow, ambient temperature/humidity per flight) followed by a 2026 Commission report with binding policy proposals, including possible per-flight pricing of non-CO2 effects — noting the required data is already collected by existing aircraft systems, so operator burden is minimal.
- Proposes a parallel MRV under ReFuelEU tracking aromatic/naphthalene/sulphur content of jet fuel supplied by each fuel supplier, to build the evidence base for future binding jet-fuel-quality legislation.
- Frames non-CO2 mitigation as unusually fast-acting compared to CO2 measures, since these effects are short-lived in the atmosphere — meaning fuel-quality/operational fixes could reduce aviation's warming impact quickly once adopted.
- See [Mitigating aviation's non-CO2 effects in the EU's Fit for 55 package](mitigating-aviation-s-non-co2-effects-in-the-eu-s-fit-for-55-package.md) for T&E's earlier, closely related briefing making the same MRV-first case in more detail.

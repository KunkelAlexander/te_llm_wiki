# National Energy and Climate Plans: how to deliver zero emission transport

*Briefing · Brussels · 2023-05-16*

> How can member states deliver a zero emissions transport system in the NECPs?

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- EU transport emissions rose steadily between 2013 and 2019 and, without additional action, are projected to still be 9% above 1990 levels in 2030 — while overall EU emissions target a steep decline; road transport alone represents three-quarters of all EU transport GHG emissions in 2019.
- Only domestic shipping and rail reduced their emissions versus 1990 levels among national transport modes; without prioritising aviation and shipping decarbonisation, these two modes are set to be the largest drivers of transport emissions growth to 2030.
- 93% of EU citizens consider climate change a serious problem, per the 2021 Eurobarometer survey cited to justify ambitious NECP planning.

- This May 2023 T&E Italy briefing (published in Italian) sets out recommendations for what EU member states should include in their National Energy and Climate Plans (NECPs) — 10-year plans due for a first draft update by end-June 2023 — to align national transport policy with the EU's raised "Fit for 55" climate ambition.
- Sector-by-sector recommendations include: national combustion-engine phase-out dates ahead of the EU's 2035 target, bonus-malus CO2 taxation and social leasing for low-income households (road); zero-emission-only public fleet procurement by 2030 and CO2-based highway tolling from 2024 with 50-75% discounts for zero-emission trucks (road freight); prioritising green hydrogen/e-ammonia bunkering infrastructure and banning new LNG bunkering investment (shipping); including all departing flight emissions in national climate targets, mandatory 50% flying-reduction targets for top corporate frequent flyers, and higher ticket taxes for long-haul/premium/private-jet flights (aviation).
- On renewables, recommends capping the overall transport renewables target at 16% energy share (or 8% GHG-intensity), eliminating palm oil biofuels before 2030 and removing soy/other dedicated-crop biofuels from the RED III target entirely, while avoiding overly ambitious advanced-biofuel targets that risk unsustainable feedstock use.
- Emphasises governance reforms: transparent, publicly accessible NECP data and modelling; meaningful and timely public consultation; whole-of-government involvement (including national parliaments); and mandatory national scientific advisory bodies to ground plans in independent evidence — flagging that prior NECP rounds suffered from weak civil-society engagement and inadequate monitoring/review structures.

# Timmermans must make concrete proposals to deliver a European Green Deal

*Press release · Brussels · 2019-10-08*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/timmermans-must-make-concrete-proposals-deliver-european-green-deal)

> Concrete proposals to clean up transport, the biggest emitter in Europe, will be needed from Frans Timmermans if he is to live up to the positive rhetoric of today's parliamentary hearing, Transport & Environment (T&E) has said. The nominee Vice-President's commitment to enforcing and strengthening the car and truck CO2 standards is good news. So is his intention to add shipping to the EU ETS and take action on aviation. However, Mr Timmermans failed to rule out putting road transport emissions in the EU ETS, which would be a distraction and would undermine the Climate Action Regulation – the main driver for climate action at national level.

- Reacting to Frans Timmermans' European Parliament confirmation hearing as incoming Commission Vice-President for the European Green Deal, T&E's executive director William Todts welcomed his rhetoric but said concrete proposals were still needed.
- T&E welcomed Timmermans' commitment to enforcing and strengthening car/truck CO2 standards and his intention to bring shipping into the EU ETS and take action on aviation emissions.
- T&E called for the Commission to go further: accelerating the shift to e-mobility, phasing out combustion-engine vehicles, cleaning up taxi/bus fleets, making batteries greener, and taking action to enable zero-emission ships and e-kerosene-powered planes — while flagging that Timmermans failed to rule out including road transport emissions in the EU ETS, which T&E argues would distract from and undermine the Effort Sharing/Climate Action Regulation, the main national-level climate driver for road transport.

# How the EU's car scrapyard law can bring clean steel and aluminium to Europe

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2023-07-06*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/how-the-eus-car-scrapyard-law-can-bring-clean-steel-and-aluminium-to-europe)

> A revised EU law on used vehicles should be as much about what goes into making a new car as about how it's recycled at the end of its life.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Materials (non-tailpipe lifecycle) emissions are projected to account for 60% of a car's total lifecycle emissions by 2040 as tailpipe emissions fall toward zero; steel alone accounts for 30-40% of that materials footprint.
- The automotive industry consumes 12% of global steel demand and 18% of global aluminium demand, giving it significant leverage to drive green production processes for both metals.
- Around a third of all end-of-life vehicles in Europe (3-4 million per year) go "missing" — exported (legally or illegally) or diverted into illegal dismantling — resulting in lost recyclable materials and unmanaged hazardous liquids.

- This July 2023 T&E op-ed argues the EU's revision of the End-of-Life Vehicles (ELV) Directive (proposal due 11 July 2023) is an underrated opportunity to decarbonise car manufacturing's material footprint, not just improve end-of-life recycling.
- Recommends a 20% mandatory target for low-emission steel and aluminium content in new cars sold in the EU (achievable via recycled content, low-carbon virgin material, or a mix), with proposed thresholds of 4-5 tCO2/tonne for "low-carbon" aluminium and below 0.4 tCO2/tonne for near-zero steel (per IEA/ResponsibleSteel standards) until 2030, after which green-hydrogen DRI steel (as low as 0.05 tCO2/tonne) should become the priority.
- Cites Denmark's €300 "scrappage premium" (funded by an annual driver tax) as a best-practice model to reduce vehicle exports/illegal dismantling, alongside proposed minimum standards (Euro 5, valid roadworthiness certificate) for used-vehicle exports to prevent dumping pollution on developing countries.
- Recommends shifting from weight-based recycling targets to per-material, quality-based targets (e.g. limiting copper contamination in recycled steel) to prevent "downcycling" of automotive materials into lower-value uses like construction.

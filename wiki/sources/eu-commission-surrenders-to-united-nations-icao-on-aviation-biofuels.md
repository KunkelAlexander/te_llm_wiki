# EU Commission surrenders to United Nations' ICAO on aviation biofuels

*Press release · Brussels · 2017-11-10*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/eu-commission-surrenders-united-nations-icao-aviation-biofuels)

> The European Commission and EU member states look set to agree to almost entirely remove sustainability criteria for bio jet fuel at the UN's aviation agency (ICAO) Council meeting today in Montreal. The countries gathered at the ICAO meeting will trash ten sustainability points out of 12, which will mean that highly unsustainable biofuels would qualify for the aviation's global carbon offsetting scheme dubbed CORSIA.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- ICAO's CAEP (Committee on Aviation Environmental Protection) had approved **12** environmental/social sustainability safeguards for CORSIA-eligible biofuels in September 2017.
- At the November 2017 ICAO Council meeting in Montreal, **10 of those 12** safeguards were dropped, leaving only **2** surviving rules.
- The two surviving rules: a minimum **-10%** greenhouse-gas-reduction threshold for biofuels versus regular jet fuel, and a ban on using crops grown on land deforested after **2009**.

- Safeguards removed covered areas including land rights, food security, labour rights and biodiversity protection — T&E frames this as ICAO setting what will become the de facto global standard for aviation biofuel sustainability, given CORSIA's scope.
- T&E holds EU member states responsible for the outcome: it says the European Commission's transport department gave in to pressure from Brazil and the US (under the Trump administration) to weaken the CAEP-approved safeguards, with France, Sweden, Italy, Spain, Ireland and Germany supporting that capitulation and neutralising opposition from the Netherlands and the UK.
- Quoted T&E reaction (Carlos Calvo Ambel, analysis and climate manager): calls the episode a "surrender" to ICAO and evidence CORSIA — ICAO's flagship aviation-decarbonisation mechanism — is "a complete shambles," urging the EU to either fix it or withdraw.

# Ready or not: Who are the frontrunners in the global race to clean up trucks?

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Scania, Mercedes-Benz Trucks and MAN lead T&E's zero-emission readiness ranking (all above 70/100 points), each targeting 100% zero-emission truck (ZET) sales by 2040 or earlier; DAF ranks last among European legacy makers with no public 2030 ZET target.
- China accounted for 90% of global ZET registrations in 2022, versus just 7% for the EU+EFTA+UK and 2% for the US; trucks/buses cause 6% of EU GHG emissions and 25% of EU emissions come from transport overall (2019).
- Two-thirds (68%, or 1.2 of 1.8 TWh) of Europe's planned battery cell production capacity is at risk of delay, downsizing or cancellation without stronger industrial policy support; the US is projected to nearly triple its share of global battery cell production from 5% to 14% by 2030 thanks to the Inflation Reduction Act, while China holds 69%.
- The Commission's proposed -45% CO2 target for 2030 would deliver only 28% ZET sales that year — well below the 47% OEMs' own voluntary targets already imply, or the 63% German manufacturers privately indicated to authorities; T&E recommends a -65% 2030 target (53% ZET sales) and a -100% target by 2035, aligned with California's binding 100%-ZET-by-2036 mandate.

- This June 2023 T&E report scores major truckmakers (Europe, US, China) on a 100-point "zero-emission readiness index" combining voluntary ZET sales ambition (with penalties for promoting gas, biofuels or e-fuels as long-term solutions) and industrial strategy (model line-up, battery/component value chain investment, charging/hydrogen infrastructure, financing).
- Tesla and China's BYD outrank most legacy truckmakers globally, having already proven rapid zero-emission manufacturing scale-up in the car segment and secured strong battery supply chains — a competitive threat T&E says established truckmakers must respond to quickly.
- Highlights an ambition-strategy gap for several OEMs: Mercedes-Benz Trucks scores highest on stated ambition (100%) but only 74% on industrial strategy, the largest gap among European makers; Volvo Trucks' 81% ambition score drops to 59% once penalised for continuing to promote gas trucks and biofuels as long-term solutions.
- IVECO is flagged as the only major European truckmaker supporting inclusion of a fuel-crediting mechanism (biofuels/e-fuels) in the EU's HDV CO2 standards — opposed by OEMs representing over 90% of the EU market — which T&E argues would let truckmakers offload their decarbonisation responsibility onto fuel suppliers.
- Warns that California's newly adopted zero-emission mandate (2036) plus IRA subsidies could pull group-level battery/EV investment away from Europe toward the US, given many "US" legacy truck brands (Freightliner, Mack, Volvo Trucks NA) are subsidiaries of European parent groups.

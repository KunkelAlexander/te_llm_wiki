# Carbon market revenues can fund green fuels for shipping and aviation

*Report · Brussels · 2024-12-16*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/carbon-market-revenues-can-fund-green-fuels-for-shipping-and-aviation) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/Briefing_-Hydrogen-support-schemes-in-aviation-and-shipping.docx.pdf)

> Dedicating a quarter of the carbon market revenues from the shipping and aviation industries can help to bridge the price gap between fossil fuels and green fuels

## Summary

*This source bundles T&E's briefing with the underlying commissioned Ricardo technical study ("Study on support schemes for RFNBOs in shipping and aviation") behind it — both summarised together below, since they cover the same modelling.*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Modelling assumes 25% of EU ETS revenue from aviation/shipping funds a Contract for Difference (CfD) covering RFNBOs (renewable fuels of non-biological origin, e.g. e-kerosene, e-ammonia).
- **Aviation**: using 25% of current-scope ETS revenue for a CfD covering 20% of the e-kerosene/fossil-kerosene price gap (~€1,000/tonne) would cover 87% of the mandated e-kerosene volume in the first auction (2025-2034: 2.5 Mt, €2.5bn) and 48% in the second (2030-2039: 9.1 Mt, €9.3bn) — 55% of the total mandate to 2040. Extending the ETS to all departing flights (scenario "FF55+") would let the same 25%-revenue CfD cover ~100% of the mandate.
- Under a scenario using all available extended-ETS funds to close the full price gap, a CfD could make e-kerosene fully cost-competitive with fossil kerosene for the first auction period.
- **Shipping**: 25% of maritime ETS revenue could subsidise up to 115 million GJ (~6 Mt) of e-ammonia by 2030 (~7% of the sector's energy, exceeding the ~0.4 Mt otherwise expected); by 2036 expected uptake would exceed what the same revenue share can support. Annual ETS revenue available for the scheme peaks around 2035 at just over €3 billion.
- e-ammonia emerges as the dominant RFNBO supported in shipping scenarios (60-77 Mt subsidised across both auction rounds, depending on scenario), with e-methanol/e-LNG/e-diesel playing only marginal roles.
- The EU's first Hydrogen Bank auction (fixed-premium, €800m budget) saw at least 2 of 7 successful projects with shipping offtakers, but none with aviation offtakers or e-kerosene production — evidence, per the report, that fixed premiums may suit shipping but not aviation's higher capital risk.
- The UK's CfD scheme for renewable electricity (since 2014) is cited as a precedent: it helped underpin 30 GW of mostly offshore wind capacity and saved nearly 6 MtCO2/year over its first decade.

- T&E (drawing on the commissioned Ricardo study) recommends supply-side CfDs (subsidy paid to fuel producers, not buyers) as the most effective public-support design for both sectors — appropriate for aviation given weak private investment appetite, and for shipping given that bunkering concentrates in a handful of major ports.
- Recommends earmarking a fixed share of aviation/shipping ETS revenue for RFNBOs, extending the ETS to close current exemptions (international flights, smaller vessels), strengthening and front-loading the RFNBO sub-targets in ReFuelEU Aviation and FuelEU Maritime, and pairing subsidies with demand-management policies (traffic growth limits in aviation, mandated efficiency gains in shipping) to avoid open-ended dependence on state aid.

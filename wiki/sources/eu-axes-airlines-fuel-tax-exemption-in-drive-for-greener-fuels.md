# EU axes airlines' fuel tax exemption in drive for greener fuels

*Press release · Brussels · 2021-07-14*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/eu-axes-airlines-fuel-tax-exemption-in-drive-for-greener-fuels)

> The EU will end jet fuel's tax exemption which has let flying go untaxed for decades, and instead will drive airlines to use cleaner, low-carbon fuels. The tax reforms announced as part of the EU's Fit for 55 plan will, however, only apply to fuel used on private and commercial flights within Europe, exempting 60% of fuel sales.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The proposed jet fuel tax exemption phase-out covers only intra-EU flights, exempting 60% of fuel sales (i.e. extra-EU/long-haul flights); flights departing Europe are responsible for over 60% of aviation emissions and remain outside the tax's scope.
- From 2030, jet fuel sold in Europe must include a growing blend of e-kerosene alongside advanced biofuels under the proposed ReFuelEU regulation.

- This T&E news piece covers the European Commission's July 2021 "Fit for 55" proposal to end jet fuel's EU tax exemption, alongside parallel reforms to the EU ETS and the new ReFuelEU sustainable-fuels mandate.
- T&E's Andrew Murphy welcomes ending the fuel tax exemption (which previously covered even private jet fuel) as ending "decades of subsidised pollution," but criticises the fact it applies only to intra-EU flights, letting the majority of emissions (extra-EU/long-haul) "off the hook."
- On the EU ETS reform, T&E notes it should raise credit prices and bring forward the net-zero deadline, but flags that extra-EU flights would instead be covered by an industry-crafted offsetting scheme that the Commission's own research found ineffective.
- T&E frames ReFuelEU — requiring an increasing e-kerosene/advanced-biofuel blend covering all jet fuel sold in Europe, including long-haul routes — as potentially the most impactful of the three proposals precisely because of its broader scope, while still arguing the e-kerosene sub-target should be set higher.

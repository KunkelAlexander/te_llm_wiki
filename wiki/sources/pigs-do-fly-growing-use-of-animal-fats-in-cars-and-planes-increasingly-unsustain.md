# 'Pigs do fly': Growing use of animal fats in cars and planes increasingly unsustainable

*Press release · Brussels · 2023-05-31*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/pigs-do-fly-growing-use-of-animal-fats-in-cars-and-planes-increasingly-unsustainable)

> 8,800 dead pigs needed to fuel a flight from Paris to New York raising concerns over the availability of 'waste' biofuels like animal fats, seen as panacea for cleaning up Europe's transport system

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Animal fat use in biodiesel has doubled in the past decade and is 40x higher than in 2006; a Paris-New York flight would need around 8,800 dead pigs' worth of fat if fully fuelled by animal-fat-derived SAF.
- Nearly half of all European animal fats already go into biodiesel, despite extensive competing use in pet food, soap and cosmetics; use of biofuels from these fats is set to triple by 2030.
- Category 1&2 animal-fat biofuel use grew just 36% since 2014 versus 160% growth for category 3 fats (used heavily by other industries) — while reported EU consumption of category 1&2 biofuels in 2021 was roughly double what the animal-fat industry actually reported supplying, pointing to likely fraud via mislabelling.

- This May 2023 T&E press release (English-language original) summarises the Cerulogy-authored study on Europe's growing and increasingly unsustainable reliance on animal-fat biofuels — see [Pigs do fly: the rise of animal fats in European transport](pigs-do-fly-the-rise-of-animal-fats-in-european-transport.md) for the full report with detailed data and policy recommendations.
- Highlights that if animal fats are diverted from pet food/oleochemical uses, those industries will likely substitute with palm oil, potentially making animal-fat biodiesel's true climate impact worse than conventional diesel.
- T&E calls for greater transparency on SAF feedstocks and for policymakers to exclude category 3 animal fats from eligible biofuel feedstock lists.

# Aviation: the EU agrees one of the world's most ambitious mandates for sustainable fuels

*Original title (French): "Aviation: L'UE s'accorde sur l'un des mandats les plus ambitieux au monde en matière de carburants durables"*

*Press release · France · 2023-04-26*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/te-france/articles/aviation-lue-saccorde-sur-lun-des-mandats-les-plus-ambitieux-au-monde-en-matiere-de-carburants-durables)

> Meeting in trilogue, EU negotiators reached an agreement on a gradual mandate for sustainable fuels in aviation. This will finally allow the market for these fuels to take off.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- ReFuelEU Aviation's final agreed SAF blending mandate starts at 2% in 2025, rises to 6% in 2030, and reaches 70% by 2050.
- The synthetic-fuel (e-kerosene) sub-mandate was set at 1.2% for 2030-2031 and 2% for 2032-2035 — up sharply from the Commission's original flat 0.7% proposal for 2030-2035, a win credited to the European Parliament.
- Non-CO2 effects (two-thirds of aviation's climate impact) were included in the final deal for the first time via jet-fuel-quality regulation (aromatic/sulphur content).

- This April 2023 T&E France press release reports the final ReFuelEU Aviation trilogue agreement, welcoming the near-tripling of the e-kerosene sub-mandate as a major win while flagging the final SAF feedstock definition still allows animal fats and used cooking oil (UCO) — both limited-availability by-products — with no cap set on UCO specifically.
- Warns the absence of a UCO cap risks EU aviation demand exceeding sustainable European supply, increasing import reliance and fraud risk; also flags that broader animal-fat inclusion risks squeezing supply in competing industries (e.g. pet food), likely pushing those industries toward palm oil substitutes with negative land-use impacts.
- Notes the deal was reached alongside separate agreement on "SAF allowances" under the aviation carbon market, together intended to give airlines cost certainty that green fuels (especially e-kerosene) will become progressively cheaper and more available.

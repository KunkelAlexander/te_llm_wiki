# Airlines, NGOs unite behind push for clean jet fuels in Europe

*Press release · Brussels · 2022-02-01*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/airlines-ngos-unite-behind-push-for-clean-jet-fuels-in-europe)

> Airlines and environmental groups are teaming up for the first time to support a European Union plan for big increases in climate-friendly jet fuels.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Aviation accounted for around 4% of European greenhouse-gas emissions before the pandemic and was a rapidly growing source of EU emissions.
- The "Fit for 55" package targets at least a 55% cut in EU greenhouse-gas emissions by 2030 (vs. 1990) and EU climate neutrality by mid-century.

- This February 2022 press release announces the "Fuelling Flight Initiative," a coalition of major airlines (Air France-KLM, easyJet, Ryanair, DPDHL) and NGOs (including T&E), convened by the European Climate Foundation and ClimateWorks with technical input from the ICCT, to jointly push EU legislators to strengthen the ReFuelEU Aviation proposal.
- The coalition's four asks: earlier and more ambitious e-kerosene sub-targets; financial support for new (sustainability-vetted) biofuel feedstocks; a European SAF industrial alliance focused on R&D; and a comprehensive SAF supplier registry.
- The group commends the Commission's proposal for already excluding food/feed crop-based biofuels, framing the initiative as building on rather than opposing the draft law.
- Signatories: AIREG, Air France-KLM, Bauhaus Luftfahrt, Deutsche Post DHL Group, easyJet, the European Cockpit Association, Natuur & Milieu, Ryanair, SkyNRG and Transport & Environment.

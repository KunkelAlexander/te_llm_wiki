# UCO (Unknown Cooking Oil): High hopes on limited and suspicious materials

*Report · Brussels · 2024-06-18*

> Europe's increased consumption of used cooking oil biofuels has resulted in a heavy reliance on imports from Asian countries. T&E assesses recent trade flows, economic dynamics and explores current and future limitations of UCO biofuels

## Summary

*This is the underlying Stratas Advisors trade-and-pricing study ("UCO Imports: Unfair Competition with EU UCO Industry?") that T&E commissioned; see the companion T&E briefing built on this data,
[Used cooking oil: from miracle fuel to fear of fraud](l-huile-de-cuisson-usag-e-du-carburant-miracle-la-crainte-de-la-fraude.md), for T&E's own framing, recommendations and additional figures.*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- European biomass-based diesel (BBD) demand grew just 3.7% (2021–2023) to 21.4 billion litres/year (bly); FAME (biodiesel) demand was 16.2 bly in 2023 versus 5.2 bly for HVO. European SAF demand grew nearly 5-fold to ~300 million litres/year (mmly) over the same period.
- UCO-based FAME (UCOME) grew to 32.8% of total European FAME consumption; UCO's share of European HEFA (SAF feedstock) rose from 10.7% (2021) to 81.8% (2023).
- The EU+UK's combined UCO collection rate is ~55% of the estimated 1,669 kt total collectible potential (798 kt collected in the EU, 126 kt in the UK); collection is ~75% of potential in the commercial sector but only ~12% in the household sector.
- China, Indonesia and Malaysia's combined UCO potential is estimated at 8,409 kt (2023); China's own supply is estimated at 3,377 kt. Malaysia showed an estimated 558 kt supply deficit in 2023 (versus reported exports) — a red flag for fraud, since it implies exporting more than it collects or imports.
- EU+UK average monthly inter-regional imports: 180 kt of UCO and 272 kt of biodiesel, of which Asia supplies ~80% of UCO and ~72% of biodiesel imports (2021–2023). Malaysia exported 925 kt of UCO globally in 2023 (599 kt to EU+UK) despite only ~100 kt of domestic UCO supply.
- China's biodiesel exports to EU+UK reached 2,053 kt in 2023 (~1,067 kt estimated UCO-based); Indonesian biodiesel exports to China rose 233% year-on-year (May 2022–May 2023, to 361 kt), raising suspicion of transshipment to evade EU anti-dumping duties.
- Supply-chain cost comparison: UCO collection+pretreatment costs are ~130 EUR/t in Asia vs. ~185 EUR/t in Europe (excluding transport/tariffs), but transport/storage costs (up to 330 EUR/t from Asia) can offset Asia's cost advantage; European labour costs for collection were 82.3% higher than in China/Indonesia/Malaysia in 2022.
- European UCO prices peaked near €1,850/tonne in June 2022, then fell steadily to below €900/tonne by December 2023; UCOME (biodiesel) prices fell 45% year-on-year to below €1,200/tonne by June 2023.
- The EU launched a formal anti-dumping investigation into Chinese biodiesel imports (Dec 2023) and had separately probed Indonesian biodiesel allegedly transshipped via China/UK to dodge an existing 8–18% countervailing duty.

- Prepared by Stratas Advisors, an energy/transport consultancy, this report assesses European biofuel/UCO trade flows and pricing to test industry allegations that mislabeled virgin oils (e.g. palm oil) are entering Europe disguised as waste-based UCO, undercutting EU producers.
- Concludes there is only limited direct evidence tying unprocessed UCO trade specifically to the price collapse, but flags substantial circumstantial evidence of potential fraud in finished biodiesel trade, given implausible export-to-collection ratios (especially Malaysia) and weak certification-scheme verification (self-declaration, limited physical/lab spot-checks, "double claiming" across certification bodies).

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Non-CO2 aviation effects (contrails, NOx etc.) cause additional warming at least as large as, and up to three times worse than, aviation's CO2 emissions.
- Less than 5% of flights are responsible for 80% of contrails' warming effect, meaning targeted flight-path changes for a small subset of "worst offender" flights could substantially cut contrail formation with minimal fuel-burn penalty.

- This April 2023 T&E op-ed (first published in Aviation Week) accuses the aviation industry of replicating the fossil-fuel industry's historic climate-denial "playbook" — failing to acknowledge non-CO2 effects, deflecting attention toward CO2-only decarbonisation narratives, and delaying action by overstating scientific uncertainty.
- Proposes two low-cost near-term fixes requiring no fleet or major operational overhaul: targeted rerouting of the small number of contrail-heavy flights, and hydrotreating jet fuel (a decades-old refinery technology already used for road/shipping fuels) to cut particle emissions and improve air quality even before SAF scales up.
- Argues binding regulation, not voluntary industry action, is necessary given aviation's track record of missing its own climate targets — specifically calling for non-CO2 effects to be priced under the EU ETS in the second half of the decade (building on the ETS's forthcoming non-CO2 monitoring/reporting system) and for binding jet-fuel-quality legislation by 2025.

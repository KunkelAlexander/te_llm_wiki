# Low cost airlines pollute more than ever, latest emissions data shows

*Press release · Brussels · 2024-04-19*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/low-cost-airlines-pollute-more-than-ever-latest-emissions-data-shows)

> More than three quarters of European aviation emissions aren't subject to a carbon price.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Ryanair was Europe's top-polluting airline for the third year running in 2023, emitting 15 Mt CO2 — 23% above pre-Covid (2019) levels, equivalent to the annual emissions of seven million petrol cars. Wizz Air's emissions grew 40% versus 2019.
- Lufthansa and British Airways ranked second and third by emissions, but both remained below pre-Covid flying levels.
- One in four European flights in 2023 was operated by a low-cost carrier (easyJet, Ryanair, Wizz Air), up from one in five in 2019; legacy carriers (Air France, Lufthansa, KLM, British Airways) lost 2.8 percentage points of market share since Covid, but still account for 42.2% of European aviation emissions because they fly long-haul.
- 20 airlines (European legacy carriers plus the largest third-country carriers) emit more, combined, than over 400 other airlines flying from Europe combined.
- Lufthansa paid for only 16% of its 2023 emissions and Air France just 7%, due to free EU ETS allowances and the carbon market's limited scope; without these exemptions Lufthansa would have paid over €800m for its CO2, but actually paid about €130m (€13/tonne, versus Air France's €5/tonne).
- Overall, 78% of aviation's CO2 emissions were not priced in 2023, either falling outside carbon-market scope or covered by free allowances.

- T&E's aviation director Jo Dardenne argues the low-cost carrier growth model is driving unsustainable emissions growth that clean technologies like SAF cannot keep pace with.
- Non-CO2 warming effects (e.g. contrails), which the piece notes warm the planet at least as much as CO2, remain entirely unpriced.

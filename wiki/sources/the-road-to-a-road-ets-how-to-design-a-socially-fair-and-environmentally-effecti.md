# The road to a road ETS: How to design a socially fair and environmentally effective system

*Briefing · Brussels · 2021-12-15*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/the-road-to-a-road-ets-how-to-design-a-socially-fair-and-environmentally-effective-system) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/Road-to-road-ETS-10-ETS2_SCF-criteria-1.pdf)

> The decarbonisation of road transport will have an impact on the whole of society and could become an economic burden on the most vulnerable. In a new briefing, Transport and Environment (T&E) proposes a set of criteria to ensure ETS2 and the SCF are socially fair and environmentally integral.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Big Oil is cited as having made nearly $2 trillion in profits over the last 30 years, cited by T&E to argue oil companies — not just citizens — should absorb part of the ETS2 carbon cost.

*(Largely a qualitative policy-design briefing — a 10-point criteria checklist rather than a modelling exercise; see [Response to the Emissions Trading System (ETS) inception impact assessment](response-to-the-emissions-trading-system-ets-inception-impact-assessment.md) and [Advanced renewable fuels in EU transport](advanced-renewable-fuels-in-eu-transport.md) for T&E's quantitative modelling on related road-transport carbon-pricing questions.)*

- This December 2021 T&E briefing sets out 10 criteria for making the EU's newly proposed second Emissions Trading System (ETS2, covering road transport and buildings) and its companion Social Climate Fund (SCF) both environmentally effective and socially fair, published after T&E's earlier fears — that ETS2 might replace national climate targets (the Effort Sharing Regulation) or vehicle CO2 standards — didn't materialise in the final Fit for 55 package.
- T&E's framing: ETS2 should be a secondary tool (managing demand and raising revenue for a just transition) rather than the primary decarbonisation lever, which should remain sectoral regulation (car/van/truck CO2 standards, building energy performance rules) since large companies, not individual citizens, have the greatest capacity to drive the transition.
- Key ETS2/SCF design recommendations: no free allowances for industry under the parallel ETS1; oil companies should absorb part of the ETS2 cost rather than passing it entirely to consumers; a "shock prevention" mechanism to buffer against fossil-fuel price spikes (like the concurrent 2021 energy crisis) compounding the carbon price; the bulk of ETS2 revenue should flow back directly to citizens (not the Innovation Fund) via visible mechanisms like lump-sum "climate dividend" payments; and the Social Climate Fund should include a formal EU definition of "mobility poverty," be funded partly by the existing ETS1, and come into force before ETS2 itself takes effect.
- T&E stresses the SCF cannot solve pre-existing social problems (low wages, lack of social housing, weak public transport) whose root causes lie outside climate policy, and calls for strong EU-level communication to prevent member states from blaming Brussels for higher fuel prices while claiming credit for the compensation.
- See [Response to the Emissions Trading System (ETS) inception impact assessment](response-to-the-emissions-trading-system-ets-inception-impact-assessment.md) for T&E's earlier, more skeptical position on folding road transport into the ETS, and [What the EU's climate plan means for Europe](what-the-eu-s-climate-plan-means-for-europe.md) for T&E's assessment of how ETS2 emerged (deliberately "toned down") in the final Fit for 55 package.

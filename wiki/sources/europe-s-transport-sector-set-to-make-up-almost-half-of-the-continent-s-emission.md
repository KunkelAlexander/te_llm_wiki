# Europe's transport sector set to make up almost half of the continent's emissions in 2030

*Press release · Brussels · 2024-03-20*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/europes-transport-sector-set-to-make-up-almost-half-of-the-continents-emissions-in-2030)

> Since a peak in 2007, the transport sector has decarbonised three times slower than the rest of the economy, making it the 'problem child' of Europe's climate efforts

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Transport's share of EU greenhouse gas emissions is projected to rise from 29% today to 44% by 2030, under current policies.
- EU transport emissions have grown more than 25% since 1990, and now exceed 1,000 MtCO2e — equivalent to the combined total emissions of Germany and the Netherlands.
- Since transport emissions peaked in 2007, the sector has decarbonised more than three times slower than the rest of the economy.
- Existing EU climate regulations are projected to cut transport emissions by only 25% versus 1990 levels by 2040, and 62% by 2050.
- Cars burning petrol/diesel account for more than 40% of transport emissions; aviation emissions have doubled over the past 30 years, faster than any other transport sub-sector, and contrails potentially triple aviation's total climate impact.
- Preliminary data: road transport emissions fell 8 MtCO2e and shipping 5 MtCO2e in the latest year, but this was outweighed by a 15 MtCO2e increase in aviation emissions.
- T&E's proposed additional measures could cut a further 213 MtCO2e by 2040 (ambitious zero-emission fleet targets for corporate vehicle owners) and 93 MtCO2e in 2030 (shipping efficiency gains).
- Direct electrification of road transport is more than twice as efficient as hydrogen and four times more efficient than e-fuels.

- Summarises T&E's "State of European Transport 2024" analysis: transport is on track to become nearly half of Europe's emissions by 2030 even as other sectors decarbonise faster, making it "the problem child of Europe's climate efforts" (Executive Director William Todts).
- T&E argues cars/trucks/vans are the "lowest hanging fruit" (cheap battery-electric decarbonisation), while planes and ships need a bigger push on green fuels (e-kerosene, ammonia) plus a plan to address aviation contrails.
- Recommends halting new airport and motorway capacity expansion, setting binding EV sales targets for corporate/large vehicle fleets, and improving shipping operational efficiency.

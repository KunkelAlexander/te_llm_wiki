# E-fuel would be wasted on cars while it's badly needed to decarbonise planes and ships – study

*Press release · Brussels · 2020-12-07*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/e-fuel-would-be-wasted-cars-while-its-badly-needed-decarbonise-planes-and-ships-study)

> Running just a fraction of vehicles on e-fuels would require offshore wind turbines covering all of Denmark, new research shows.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Powering just 10% of cars/vans/small trucks with hydrogen and 10% with e-diesel would require 41% more renewable electricity by 2050 than running the same vehicles as battery EVs.
- If half of heavy-duty trucks ran on hydrogen and half on e-diesel, they would consume 151% more renewables by 2050 than if directly electrified.
- Running the modelled fraction of road vehicles on synthetic hydrocarbons would consume 936 TWh more renewable energy by 2050 than the base-case (electrified) scenario — equivalent to needing offshore wind farms covering roughly 44,430 km² (using 2GW turbines each covering 375 km² and producing 7.9 TWh), which exceeds Denmark's total land area of 42,394 km².
- Powering Europe's ships (ammonia/hydrogen) and planes (e-kerosene) would consume 1,275 TWh of renewables by 2050 — more than the amount needed to directly electrify all of road transport.
- The EU aims to deliver 330 TWh of hydrogen to market over the coming decade (per its hydrogen strategy).

- This is a T&E press release (accompanying the Ricardo Energy & Environment study referenced across several 2020-21 T&E aviation/e-fuels papers) arguing that hydrogen and e-fuels (electrofuels) should be prioritised for shipping and aviation — where batteries aren't a viable decarbonisation option — rather than for road vehicles, which can be more efficiently decarbonised via direct electrification.
- T&E's Geert De Cock frames the choice as consequential: using e-fuels broadly in road transport would require an area of new offshore wind capacity the size of Denmark just to cover a fraction of vehicles, which T&E argues "doesn't make any sense" given batteries are a more efficient alternative for cars/vans/most trucks.
- T&E argues aviation and shipping instead represent the ideal "lead markets" to scale green hydrogen production, given the EU's hydrogen strategy target and the absence of a direct-electrification alternative for these sectors.
- Policy calls: CO2 standards requiring ships to operate more efficiently and adopt clean technology (ammonia, hydrogen), and mandates requiring aviation fuel suppliers to deliver zero-emission fuels to airlines.
- See [EU strategy for Smart Sector Integration](eu-strategy-for-smart-sector-integration.md) and [How to ensure the sustainability of electrofuels](how-to-ensure-the-sustainability-of-electrofuels.md) for T&E's fuller treatment of the same "electrofuels for hard-to-abate sectors, direct electrification everywhere else" argument.

# Industrial policy is not a relic of the past but key to winning the green energy race

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2022-09-30*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/industrial-policy-is-not-a-relic-of-the-past-but-key-to-winning-the-green-energy-race)

> There was a time when 'industrial policy' was a swear word in Brussels. Is that still the case?

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The US Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) combines a $7,500 EV tax credit, a $4,000 used-EV credit, and a $35-45/kWh battery-cell/module production tax credit (worth roughly $3,000 more), plus $300 million in grants for SAF production and low-emission aviation technology — available only to US-made vehicles/batteries with raw materials sourced from allied countries.
- The tax credit on a company-car Porsche 911 in Germany is worth €15,564/year, illustrating the scale of existing ICE-favouring tax subsidies the author argues could be redirected to EVs.
- The author proposes funding an EU-wide social leasing scheme (e.g. entry-level EVs from ~€200/month) using the EU's existing €17 billion in annual fuel-duty tax cuts.
- EU battery electric vehicle (BEV) sales were close to 10% as of writing, with sales growth reportedly stagnating in Europe in H1 2022 even as they continued surging in China and the US.

- This September 2022 op-ed by T&E's executive director argues the EU must respond aggressively to China's state-driven EV/battery dominance and the US Inflation Reduction Act's generous, US-content-conditioned clean-tech subsidies, or risk losing the global clean-energy manufacturing race.
- Frames China's industrial policy as net-positive for global decarbonisation (cheaper solar/batteries) but a competitive threat given Chinese EV startups already eating into European OEMs' market share domestically and increasingly in Europe.
- Recommends: an EU "energy sovereignty fund" (modelled on the post-Covid Recovery Fund) to finance home insulation, heat pumps, renewables and industrial electrification; policies to secure/reshore critical raw materials supply chains (lithium, etc.) and promote recycling; converting existing large company-car ICE tax subsidies into EV incentives via an EU fleets regulation; and an EU-wide social leasing scheme for affordable EVs.
- Cautions that Europe should not respond to foreign competition with protectionism that excludes non-European investment (e.g. CATL), arguing competition and new entrants drive down prices and bring innovation — while still needing defensive measures against the IRA's function as a de facto export subsidy.

# A carbon market that works for the climate and industry

*Letter · Brussels · 2022-06-03*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/an-carbon-market-that-works-for-the-climate-and-industry) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/FINAL_Open-letter_Free-allowances_06062022-2.pdf)

> A coalition of NGOs sends an open letter to the European Parliament, calling for them to vote for an EU Emissions Trading System (ETS) that works for climate and industrial transformation

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- In 2021 alone, the EU ETS's Modernisation and Innovation Funds received 69 million and 40 million allowances respectively, while free allocation to industry cost 676 million allowances — nearly 5x the two funds combined.
- Free allocation of ETS allowances generated an estimated €50 billion in windfall profits for resource- and energy-intensive industries between 2013 and 2019, per a CE Delft study cited in the letter, with almost no emissions reductions in return.
- The letter calls for the ETS's minimum annual benchmark-tightening rate to rise to 1% (from a proposed 0.2%), and for the benchmark review to move up from the Commission's proposed 2026 date.
- Removing free allowances alongside introducing the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) would deliver 15% additional emissions reductions in CBAM-covered sectors compared to the baseline, per the Commission's own CBAM impact assessment.

- This June 2022 open letter — signed by T&E alongside CAN Europe, Carbon Market Watch, WWF EPO, EEB and roughly 20 other NGOs — urges MEPs to vote for an ambitious ETS reform ahead of the 8 June 2022 European Parliament vote, prioritising a full and rapid phase-out of free allowances plus a stronger emissions cap.
- Central argument: free allocation is a "substantial market failure" that has let emissions from industries like steel, cement and basic chemicals stagnate since 2013 (per the European Court of Auditors), while doing nothing to reward genuine decarbonisation.
- Calls for auctioned-allowance revenue to be reinvested in the green transition (efficiency, innovation, socially just transformation) rather than debt relief or general industry support, with green conditionality (e.g. excluding "grey"/"blue" hydrogen investments) attached to any funding.
- Opposes the proposed "Carbon Leakage Protection Reserve," which would bank CBAM-freed allowances for two years rather than channel them directly to the Innovation Fund, arguing this would undermine both decarbonisation and the EU's international credibility on CBAM.
- Frames ETS reform as one part of a wider needed industrial policy package (binding pollution performance standards, green product requirements, green public procurement), not a standalone fix.

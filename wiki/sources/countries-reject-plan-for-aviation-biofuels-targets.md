# Countries reject plan for aviation biofuels targets

*Press release · Brussels · 2017-10-14*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/countries-reject-plan-aviation-biofuels-targets)

> Today, 25 countries convened by the UN International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO) rightly rejected the 2050 Vision on Sustainable Aviation Fuels that included volume-based targets as originally proposed by the ICAO Secretariat. Brad Schallert, Deputy Director at World Wildlife Fund and a spokesperson for the International Coalition for Sustainable Aviation, a network of nonprofit organizations representing millions of members, released the following statement in response.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- **25 countries**, convened by ICAO (meeting held in Mexico City, October 2017), rejected the ICAO Secretariat's proposed volume-based fuel-use targets.
- The rejected targets covered three milestone years: **2025, 2040 and 2050** ("2050 Vision on Sustainable Aviation Fuels").

- The rejection is welcomed by T&E/ICSA (International Coalition for Sustainable Aviation) as the right outcome: the proposed volume targets were, in ICSA's assessment, based on poor analysis that grossly overestimated the achievable environmental benefit and emissions reductions of scaling up alternative aviation fuels that fast.
- ICSA's position (voiced here by Brad Schallert of WWF): in these early stages of alternative-fuel development, policy should prioritize the sustainability and genuine emissions-reduction quality of fuels over sheer production volume — scaling unsustainable fuel production quickly is itself a risk.
- Broader framing: aviation is characterized as a top-ten global polluter with emissions still rising, so ICSA argues the sector's fuel strategy should be judged on real decarbonisation impact, not headline volume commitments.

# The small price to pay to clean up shipping

*Briefing · Brussels · 2022-06-28*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/the-small-price-to-pay-to-clean-up-shipping) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/Cost-of-clean-shipping-is-negligible-_-Case-study-for-6-green-e-fuels-and-stringent-ETS_Final_Corrected.pdf)

> Analysis of how consumer goods prices would be impacted by shipping decarbonisation measures

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Strengthening FuelEU Maritime's 2030 GHG-intensity target from -6% to -14%, adding a 6% e-fuels (RFNBO) sub-quota, and pricing well-to-wake (not just tank-to-wake) emissions under the maritime ETS would add between €8.2 and €40.5 per shipping container (TEU) on a China–Belgium voyage — just 1.04% to 5.15% of the €786/TEU historical average freight cost (under €79.1/TEU, or under 0.8%, versus 2022's spiked freight rates of €4,200-6,200/TEU).
- Passed fully through to consumers, the maximum per-item cost increase would be €0.0081 (0.81 cents) for a pair of shoes, €0.0008 for a banana, €0.1011 for a TV, and €0.8091 for a fridge — around 0.03% of a typical TV or shoe price.
- Modelled using a real 14,354-TEU container ship (the "Taurus") on an actual 2019 China–Belgium route, assuming an €80/tonne CO2e carbon price.

- This June 2022 T&E briefing quantifies the real-world consumer cost of much more ambitious shipping decarbonisation rules than the Commission's "Fit for 55" proposal, directly rebutting shipping-industry claims that stronger FuelEU Maritime/ETS rules would meaningfully raise the price of imported goods.
- Tests six fuel-mix compliance scenarios (fossil LNG, biodiesel blend, e-ammonia, e-LNG, e-methanol) against the stricter targets, finding e-fuel-compliant pathways (e-LNG, e-ammonia, e-methanol) cost only slightly more than fossil-LNG or biodiesel-blend compliance pathways.
- Notes fossil LNG is the cheapest way to hit GHG-intensity targets alone (but not the RFNBO e-fuels sub-quota) under historical prices — reinforcing T&E's broader warning (see [How to drive the uptake of sustainable fuels in European shipping](how-to-drive-the-uptake-of-sustainable-fuels-in-european-shipping.md)) that a technology-neutral target without a dedicated e-fuels sub-quota risks locking in fossil LNG rather than driving genuine e-fuel uptake.
- Concludes that given container-shipping freight rate volatility (multiple thousands of euros per TEU during 2021-22), even the higher-ambition scenario's cost increase would be within noise for cargo owners and consumers — undercutting the case for weakening EU shipping climate ambition on cost grounds.

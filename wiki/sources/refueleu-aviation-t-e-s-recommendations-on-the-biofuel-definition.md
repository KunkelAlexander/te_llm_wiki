## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The scraped fulltext for this page is only the short introductory abstract; the full quantitative analysis (PFAD emissions >2.5x worse than fossil fuel, projected 2030 PFAD-HEFA volumes able to meet the entire proposed SAF mandate, etc.) is captured in full in the companion source below.

- This July 2022 T&E briefing flags that the European Parliament's Transport (TRAN) committee widened the ReFuelEU Aviation definition of "sustainable aviation fuels" to include recycled carbon fuels and a broader biofuel feedstock base than the Commission's original proposal.
- T&E's position: revert to the Commission's original definition (Annex IX feedstocks only), since the widened definition would open the door to unsustainable feedstocks like Palm Fatty Acid Distillate (PFAD), category 3 animal fats, and "intermediate crop" biofuels — all of which have competing uses elsewhere in the economy and would cause displacement (indirect land-use change) emissions.
- See [Airline and green NGO urge MEPs to exclude palm oil by-products from European aviation](airline-and-green-ngo-urge-meps-to-exclude-palm-oil-by-products-from-european-av.md) for the fuller, jointly-signed (with easyJet) version of this same argument, including the detailed PFAD/animal-fat/intermediate-crop quantitative analysis referenced above.

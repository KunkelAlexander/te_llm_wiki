# Agenda for climate-neutral aviation

*Original title (German): "Agenda klimaneutraler Luftverkehr"*

> Environmental organisations' demands on aviation ahead of Germany's 2025-29 legislative term.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Aviation accounts for at least 7.5% of global climate-warming emissions, two-thirds of which are non-CO2 effects.
- Demands include: a general night-flight ban from 10pm to 6am; a 30% reduction in aircraft noise by 2040; a 10 ppm sulphur limit for kerosene (matching the existing limit for petrol/diesel) and capping aromatics near the lower end of ASTM standards (8%); phasing out climate-harmful federal aviation subsidies to zero, halved by 2030 at the latest; and adding a new distance class to Germany's air passenger tax for flights over 13,000 km while abolishing its revenue cap.

- Joint policy demands from German environmental/aviation-noise groups (Bundesvereinigung gegen Fluglärm, Germanwatch, T&E Germany, VCD) addressed to the incoming German government ahead of the 2025 legislative term, organised around three pillars: a "roadmap to climate-neutral aviation" by 2045, a noise-reduction strategy, and measures to cut air pollution around airports.
- Calls for Germany to push at EU level for a stronger ETS and Energy Taxation Directive to end kerosene's tax exemption, build out power-to-liquid (e-kerosene) infrastructure under strict sustainability standards, and abolish free slot allocation/"grandfather rights" for airlines.
- Recommends demand-management levers (slot reductions, a declining CO2 cap, cutting short-haul routes with viable rail alternatives) and expanding night-train and daytime rail connections as substitutes for domestic/European flights.
- Cites ultrafine particles (UFPs) from kerosene combustion, especially at takeoff, as a major airport-vicinity health risk, recommending a stricter sulphur/aromatics fuel standard and EU-mandated UFP monitoring infrastructure — see [Ultrafine particles from aircraft pose a health risk to 11 million French people](les-particules-ultrafines-des-avions-font-peser-un-risque-sur-la-sant-de-11-mill.md) for T&E's related research on this topic.

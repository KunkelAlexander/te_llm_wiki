# The UK's green jet fuel mandate needs robust sustainability criteria to set aviation on a net-zero flightpath

*Letter · Brussels · 2021-06-15*

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Cites the UK government's aim for 5GW of hydrogen production capacity by 2030 as a foundation for UK e-kerosene production leadership.

*(Largely a qualitative policy-design letter — its substance is a checklist of criteria and process recommendations rather than quantitative modelling; see [Making aviation fuel mandates sustainable](making-aviation-fuel-mandates-sustainable.md) and [E-kerosene mandate: key steps for ReFuelEU success](e-kerosene-mandate-key-steps-for-refueleu-success.md) for the figures behind the same underlying T&E positions applied here to the UK.)*

- This June 2021 open letter to the UK's Transport Secretary and Aviation Minister — co-signed by T&E, the Aviation Environment Federation, Friends of the Earth, Green Alliance and Greenpeace — urges the UK government to define sustainability criteria for its forthcoming Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) mandate before setting target percentages, via a two-stage consultation (criteria/scope first, mandate levels second).
- Core recommendations mirror T&E's EU ReFuelEU positions: exclude conventional crop-based biofuels (palm, soy); apply strict waste-hierarchy/cascading-principle scrutiny to waste-based advanced biofuels; require e-kerosene hydrogen to come from additional renewable electricity and require a significant (rising to 100%) Direct Air Capture carbon share; and apply the mandate to all fuel suppliers covering all fuel uplifted in the UK regardless of flight destination (domestic, short-haul, or long-haul).
- The letter recommends dissuasive non-compliance penalties set above the price gap between fossil kerosene and the most expensive eligible SAF, and stresses that SAF alone is "not a panacea" — it still emits CO2 and non-CO2 warming effects — so a wider net-zero-aviation framework (including true zero-emission aircraft/fuels) is still needed.
- The letter welcomes the UK's decision to include international aviation and shipping emissions in its sixth carbon budget, and calls for the UK to treat 2019 as its aviation emissions peak year.
- See [Making aviation fuel mandates sustainable](making-aviation-fuel-mandates-sustainable.md) and [E-kerosene mandate: key steps for ReFuelEU success](e-kerosene-mandate-key-steps-for-refueleu-success.md) for the EU-focused T&E analysis whose recommendations (crop-biofuel exclusion, DAC requirements, penalty design, full-scope mandates) are mirrored here for the UK context.

# Rising use of private jets sends CO2 emissions soaring

*Press release · Brussels · 2021-05-27*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/rising-use-of-private-jets-sends-co2-emissions-soaring)

> Environmental impact triggers call for ban on fossil-fuel private jets in Europe by 2030 CO2 emissions from private jets in Europe increased by nearly a third (31%) between 2005 and 2019, rising faster than commercial aviation emissions, finds a new report from campaign group Transport & Environment (T&E).

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Same underlying figures as [Private jets: can the super-rich supercharge zero-emission aviation?](private-jets-can-the-super-rich-supercharge-zero-emission-aviation.md) — 31% growth in European private-jet CO2 emissions 2005–2019 (vs. commercial aviation), 10x higher per-passenger carbon intensity than commercial flights and 50x higher than trains, a 4-hour private flight emitting roughly a year's worth of an average person's emissions, and an average private jet owner net worth of €1.3 billion.
- Seven of the ten most polluting European private-jet routes lie on the UK-France-Switzerland-Italy axis; private jets departing the UK and France together account for 36% of European private-flight emissions; 1 in 10 flights departing France are private jets, half under 500km.
- A distance-proportionate jet fuel tax is estimated to raise €325 million if applied to all flights departing the EU and UK.

- This is T&E's press release announcing its "Private jets: can the super-rich supercharge zero-emission aviation?" report, framing private jets as "probably the worst thing you can do for the environment" while noting private jet traffic returned to pre-COVID levels by August 2020 even as commercial flights remained down 60% year-on-year.
- T&E's Andrew Murphy frames the private jet market's small scale and wealth as an opportunity to accelerate hydrogen/electric aviation ("aviation's Tesla moment"), calling for regulators to urgently tax fossil-fuel private jets and ban their use by 2030.
- Recommendations mirror the full report: hydrogen/electric-only mandate for private flights under 1,000km from 2030; a polluter-pays carbon/fuel/flight tax scaled by distance and aircraft weight in the interim; and voluntary reduction commitments from companies/individuals, with flights prohibited where a non-flying alternative exists that doesn't add more than 2h30 travel time.
- See [Private jets: can the super-rich supercharge zero-emission aviation?](private-jets-can-the-super-rich-supercharge-zero-emission-aviation.md) for the full report with detailed methodology and country/route-level breakdowns.

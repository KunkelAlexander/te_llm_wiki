# Seulement 10 compagnies aériennes, dont Air France-KLM, investissent sérieusement dans les carburants alternatifs

*Briefing · France · 2024-12-03*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/te-france/articles/seulement-10-compagnies-aeriennes-dont-air-france-klm-investissent-serieusement-dans-les-carburants-alternatifs)

> Sur les 77 compagnies aériennes classées par T&E, la moitié obtiennent la pire note car elles ne font pas d'effort pour incorporer des carburants alternatifs au kérosène fossile. Air France-KLM, au contraire, se classe en tête. Les compagnies pétrolières, qui n'investissent pas assez dans cette transition, sont en grande partie responsables de cette situation.

## Summary

*French-office coverage of T&E's global airline SAF ranking; see the companion German-office press release,
[Ölkonzerne und Fluggesellschaften verschleppen Investitionen in E-Kerosin](lkonzerne-und-fluggesellschaften-verschleppen-investitionen-in-e-kerosin.md), for the same ranking's findings on Lufthansa and oil-company behaviour.*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- T&E ranked 77 global airlines on their sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) transition: 67 (87%) buy too little SAF, the wrong type of SAF, or don't consider SAF in their decarbonisation plans at all; only 10 airlines showed meaningful effort.
- Air France-KLM tops the ranking with 61/100 points (grade B) — the only airline to score a B; no airline achieved the top grade (A). United Airlines and Norwegian rank second and third.
- 67 of 77 ranked airlines (the majority) fall into the lowest grade (D), reflecting little/no SAF adoption and heavy reliance on unsustainable food/feed-crop-based biofuels.
- E-kerosene — made from renewable electricity, the only SAF type considered durably scalable — accounts for less than 10% of airlines' signed SAF agreements, while unsustainable biofuels from corn and soybean oil make up more than 30%.

- Ranking criteria: SAF/e-kerosene incorporation targets, types of fuel purchased, and e-kerosene commitments via offtake agreements, letters of intent and MOUs.
- T&E argues most airlines are betting on the wrong (least sustainable, least scalable) type of SAF, and calls on airlines to pressure fuel producers to prioritise e-kerosene over crop-based biofuels.

# How is e-kerosene developing in Europe?

*News · Brussels · 2024-02-22*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/how-is-e-kerosene-developing-in-europe)

> As the most sustainable alternative to traditional jet fuels, e-kerosene is generated from hydrogen and CO₂. Unlike many other sustainable aviation fuels, the combustion of e-kerosene can be close to CO2 neutral.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- ReFuelEU Aviation mandates e-kerosene blending starting at 1.2% in 2030, rising to 35% in 2050.
- T&E's tracker had identified 56 e-kerosene projects in the EEA as of its latest count: 36 large-scale industrial projects and 20 smaller pilot projects.
- None of the major projects had reached final investment decision (FID) yet at time of writing; many were still at the feasibility-study stage.

*(Note: the article text says the tracker map "was last updated on 24th of June 2024" — later than the article's own 2024-02-22 publication date, suggesting this page's live content was updated in place after publication; the 56/36/20 project counts above are from the scraped fulltext as captured.)*

- e-kerosene (synthetic/e-fuel jet fuel) is made from captured CO2 and hydrogen produced with additional renewable electricity, making its combustion close to CO2-neutral, unlike biomass-based biofuels which face feedstock scalability limits.
- The piece frames e-kerosene as the more scalable long-term decarbonisation pathway for aviation compared to biofuels, since its feedstock (renewable electricity) isn't constrained the way biomass supply is.

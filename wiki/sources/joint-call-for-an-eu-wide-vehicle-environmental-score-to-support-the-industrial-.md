# Joint call for an EU-wide vehicle environmental score to support the industrial transition

*Letter · Brussels · 2024-09-25*

> Four organizations ask the European Commission to put in place a methodology that will help achieve climate neutrality by 2050 and boost 'Made in EU' electric cars

## Summary

*Follow-up joint letter to the incoming European Commission, building on the April 2024 briefing [Making EVs fit for the future](making-evs-fit-for-the-future.md) — see that page for the underlying eco-score methodology and worked examples.*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Production/materials account for 50–60% of a battery electric vehicle's lifecycle emissions, versus only ~10% for combustion cars, given the latter's use-phase emissions dominate.
- The car CO2 regulation requires the European Commission to develop a full lifecycle-CO2 (LCA) reporting methodology for passenger cars by end of 2025, with voluntary manufacturer reporting from June 2026, and to assess minimum EV energy-efficiency thresholds by 2026.

- Signed by BEUC, Deutsche Umwelthilfe (DUH), the Mobility in Transition Institute (IMT) and T&E, addressed to Commissioner Wopke Hoekstra and three Commission Director-Generals, urging that a harmonised EU "eco-score" (combining vehicle energy efficiency with the carbon footprint of batteries, steel and aluminium) replace the more complex full-LCA approach the Commission is otherwise required to develop.
- Recommends the Commission adopt delegated acts establishing the eco-score methodology by 31 December 2025, with carmaker reporting starting June 2026 — the same regulatory vehicle (and timeline) already earmarked for the LCA requirement.
- Frames the eco-score as simultaneously a consumer-information tool, a Single Market harmonisation measure (preventing fragmented national rules like France's eco-bonus), a fiscal-policy tool, and an industrial-policy tool rewarding low-carbon "Made in EU" production.

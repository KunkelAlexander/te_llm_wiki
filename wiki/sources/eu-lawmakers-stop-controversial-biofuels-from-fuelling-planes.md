# EU lawmakers stop controversial biofuels from fuelling planes

*Press release · Brussels · 2022-07-07*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/eu-lawmakers-stop-controversial-biofuels-from-fuelling-planes)

> In a narrow vote, the European Parliament paved the way for the uptake of green fuels in planes.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The European Parliament tripled the synthetic-fuel (e-kerosene) volumes the Commission had proposed for 2030, and set a target for synthetic fuels to make up half of all EU jet fuel use by 2050.
- Non-CO2 effects — two-thirds of aviation's climate impact — were addressed for the first time via a provision to regulate jet fuel quality (lower aromatic/sulfur content).

- This July 2022 T&E press release reports the European Parliament's narrow vote to exclude palm oil by-products (PFADs) and "intermediate crops" from ReFuelEU Aviation's definition of sustainable aviation fuels, following the joint T&E/easyJet lobbying letter (see [Airline and green NGO urge MEPs to exclude palm oil by-products from European aviation](airline-and-green-ngo-urge-meps-to-exclude-palm-oil-by-products-from-european-av.md)).
- Notes category-3 animal fats (also a by-product with competing uses, whose likely substitute is palm oil) remain included in the SAF definition, and T&E calls on the Council, Commission and Parliament to exclude them too during the upcoming September trilogue negotiations.
- The Parliament also expanded the synthetic-fuel definition to include renewable electricity and green hydrogen, explicitly to accelerate development of electric/hydrogen zero-emission aircraft.
- See [Europe gets one step closer to a green flight](europe-gets-one-step-closer-to-a-green-flight.md) for T&E's op-ed take on the same vote.

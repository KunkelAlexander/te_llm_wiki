# Global Powerfuels Alliance and Transport & Environment call for an ambitious e-kerosene target

*Letter · Brussels · 2022-11-29*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/gglobal-powerfuels-alliance-and-te-call-for-an-ambitious-e-kerosene-target)

> An ambitious European e-kerosene target would lead to minimal added cost to passenger tickets but maximum investor certainty

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Raising the ReFuelEU 2030 e-kerosene blending sub-target from the Commission/Council's proposed 0.7% to the Parliament's 2% would add only €0.6 per ticket per 1,000km flown — roughly the length of an average intra-EU flight.

- This November 2022 joint letter from the Global Powerfuels Alliance and T&E urges EU member states and the Commission to adopt the European Parliament's 2% e-kerosene sub-target for 2030 in ReFuelEU Aviation, rather than the Commission/Council's 0.7% General Approach position.
- Central argument: 0.7% is too low to send a meaningful market signal to e-kerosene producers, while the cost of tripling it to 2% is negligible for passengers — framing the choice as a near-costless way to accelerate synthetic aviation fuel investment.
- See [Green aviation fuels could save 5 million tonnes of CO2 in 2030](green-aviation-fuels-could-save-5-million-tonnes-of-co2-in-2030.md) and [Analysis of green jet fuel production in Europe](analysis-of-green-jet-fuel-production-in-europe.md) for T&E's underlying e-kerosene production-capacity analysis supporting the case for a 2% (rather than 0.7%) mandate.

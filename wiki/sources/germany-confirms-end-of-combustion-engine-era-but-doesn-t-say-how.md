# Germany confirms end of combustion engine era – but doesn't say how

> What the country's coalition agreement really means for transport

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The new SPD-Greens-FDP coalition targets 15 million electric cars on German roads by 2030 — short of the 21 million T&E and Prognos project is needed for Germany to meet its transport decarbonisation targets.
- The coalition maintains its goal of 1 million charging points by 2030 (reliant on private investment) and plans to raise company-car tax on EVs from 0.25% to 0.5% from 2025, while keeping existing tax breaks for diesel/petrol company cars — significant given company cars make up 63% of German car sales.
- From 2023, truck tolls will vary by CO2 emissions for all trucks over 3.5 tonnes (down from the current 7.5-tonne threshold), alongside a new CO2 charge for trucks — a first in Europe.
- Coal phase-out brought forward to "ideally" 2030 (eight years earlier than previously planned); renewable electricity target raised to 80% of the mix by 2030; rail freight share targeted to reach 25% by 2030.

- This T&E news piece analyses Germany's November 2021 coalition agreement (SPD, Greens, FDP), finding a mixed picture for transport decarbonisation.
- On cars, T&E welcomes the coalition's support for the EU's 2035 combustion-engine sales phase-out but flags an ambiguity and cause for concern: the agreement allows continued use of synthetic ("e-")fuels in combustion engines, which T&E's own research shows emit consistently more CO2 than equivalent battery-electric cars and require five times more energy to produce than direct electrification — creating uncertainty about how the coalition's professed 100%-zero-emission-sales-by-2035 support would coexist with e-fuel allowances outside CO2 standards.
- T&E's biggest disappointment is on company car taxation: the coalition maintains generous tax breaks for diesel/petrol company cars, which T&E argues is the single highest-leverage lever available to Germany given company cars' 63% share of car sales, and calls for reform to be a top priority in the 2022 Climate Action Plan (Klimaschutz Sofortprogramm).
- On aviation and shipping, T&E welcomes the coalition's ambition to become a leader in CO2-neutral aviation via Power-to-Liquid quotas, but flags that a domestic-flight ticket-tax increase won't happen before 2023 and that shipping receives only limited attention in the agreement.

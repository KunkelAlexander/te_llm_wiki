# Analysis of green jet fuel production in Europe

*Briefing · Brussels · 2022-06-09*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/analysis-of-green-jet-fuel-production-in-europe) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/220915_E-kerosene-tracker-briefing_SB_2nd-release-4.pdf)

> Why the EU can and should increase e-kerosene targets in ReFuelEU

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- As of this November 2022 update, 12 companies had planned e-kerosene production capacity of 0.025 Mt for 2025 — matching the European Parliament's proposed 0.04% (0.02 Mt) 2025 sub-target — while 28 companies had plans totalling 1.85 Mt for 2030 (up from 1.83 Mt in the original June 2022 study), enough to cover 3.69% of EU jet fuel demand and save around 5 MtCO2/year (equivalent to ~30,000 transatlantic flights).
- The Commission's proposed e-kerosene sub-target remains just 0.7% by 2030 (0.35 Mt) — well below planned industry capacity — while the Parliament's position calls for 0.04% in 2025 and 2% (1.00 Mt) in 2030, which T&E endorses as achievable given announced capacity.
- Of the 28 producers tracked, the majority (19) use biogenic carbon sources rather than direct air capture (DAC) — a shift T&E flags as concerning since only DAC can scale sustainably in the long run.
- E-kerosene production could create 202,100 additional jobs by 2050 per European Commission estimates, and costs are projected (per ATAG) to fall 61% by 2050 versus 2025 as green hydrogen and renewable electricity costs decline.

- This is the November 2022 updated version of T&E's e-kerosene production tracker (first published June 2022 — see [Green aviation fuels could save 5 million tonnes of CO2 in 2030](green-aviation-fuels-could-save-5-million-tonnes-of-co2-in-2030.md) for the original), quantifying real-world e-kerosene manufacturer commitments to test whether ReFuelEU Aviation's proposed blending sub-targets are set at the right level.
- Notes that between the two study versions, one 80,000t project was cancelled and another 60,000t project delayed a year, while several new projects were announced — illustrating T&E's core argument that stronger, earlier mandates are needed to prevent investment hesitancy and further delays.
- Reiterates that e-kerosene, unlike advanced biofuels (limited by finite waste/residue feedstocks), is the only genuinely scalable sustainable aviation fuel pathway, and that DAC-sourced carbon should become mandatory for large-scale production in the medium term rather than relying on biogenic sources.
- Policy recommendations: raise the ReFuelEU e-kerosene sub-target to at least 0.04% (0.02 Mt) in 2025 and 2% (1.00 Mt) in 2030 (with flexibility for member states to exceed this); require additional renewable electricity and green hydrogen; give DAC carbon a dedicated scale-up sub-target; include direct-use renewable hydrogen/electricity (for electric/hydrogen aircraft) in the synthetic-fuel definition; and strengthen carbon pricing (jet fuel tax, EU ETS) to close the cost gap with fossil kerosene.

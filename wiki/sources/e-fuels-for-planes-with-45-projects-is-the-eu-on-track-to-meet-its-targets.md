# E-fuels for planes: with 45 projects, is the EU on track to meet its targets?

*Press release · Brussels · 2024-01-24*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/e-fuels-for-planes-with-45-projects-is-the-eu-on-track-to-meet-its-targets) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/2024_01_E-kerosene_Tracker_TE_2024-04-29-155012_cevi.pdf)

> Norway, Germany and France surge ahead to produce e-fuels for planes, but uncertainties in the market persist, a new study by green group Transport & Environment shows.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- T&E has identified 45 e-kerosene projects across the EEA (25 industrial-scale, 20 demonstration), up from 28 companies in its November 2022 tracker; combined potential 2030 production capacity is 1.7 Mt — above ReFuelEU's 2030 mandate (1.2%, ~0.6 Mt) and 2032 mandate (2%, ~1.0 Mt), but not yet the 5% (2035) level.
- No major industrial e-kerosene project has reached Final Investment Decision (FID) yet; the three most advanced projects (Arcadia eFuels, Norsk e-Fuel, Nordic Electrofuel) together represent only 105,000 tonnes of capacity — far below the 600,000-tonne 2030 target.
- Norway, France and Germany account for up to 80% of Europe's projected e-kerosene production; all 45 projects rely on biogenic carbon (none currently plan to use Direct Air Capture), a shift T&E flags as a looming bottleneck given competing biogenic-CO2 demand from other sectors.
- Meeting ReFuelEU's full 35%-by-2050 e-kerosene mandate would require 534 TWh of renewable electricity (with demand management: 290 TWh) — 19% (or 10-11%) of the EU's 2022 total electricity demand.

- This January 2024 T&E study (updating its June/November 2022 e-kerosene tracker) maps European e-kerosene production plans against ReFuelEU Aviation's now-finalised blending mandate, finding the pipeline of announced projects is theoretically sufficient near-term but faces major hurdles before projects convert to real capacity.
- Key barriers identified: no ReFuelEU incentive to supply e-kerosene before 2030; a "regulatory loophole" risk of e-fuels being diverted to road transport (following Germany's 2035 combustion-engine e-fuel carve-out fight); e-kerosene's exclusion from the Net Zero Industrial Act's strategic-technology list (unlike electrolysers); high production costs (ICCT estimates e-kerosene cost 10x fossil kerosene in 2020, narrowing to 3.5x by 2030 and 2.5x by 2050) deterring airline offtake agreements; and oil majors' potential gatekeeping of downstream refining/blending infrastructure.
- Recommends: prioritising e-fuels for aviation/shipping over road transport; adding e-kerosene to the NZIA's strategic technology list; earmarking 25% of national aviation ETS revenue for green hydrogen/e-fuel production (an estimated €10.25bn 2024-2030, rising to €18bn if ETS scope extends to all departing flights); more EU Innovation Fund/Hydrogen Bank support specifically for hard-to-electrify sectors; and future DAC-specific mandates/funding given DAC firms currently favour carbon storage over utilisation.

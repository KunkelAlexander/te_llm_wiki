## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Germany's Federal Climate Protection Act (tightened in 2021) requires a 65% GHG cut vs. 1990 by 2030 (up from 55%) and net-zero by 2045; the transport sector's 2030 target was correspondingly raised, requiring a cut from 164 Mt CO2eq (1990 baseline, roughly equal to 2019's actual level) to 85 Mt CO2eq — a 48% reduction, or nearly 80 million tonnes, in under a decade.
- Transport was the only German sector whose emissions had not fallen below 1990 levels by 2019.
- An earlier Prognos assessment (for the economy ministry) of the prior, less strict Climate Protection Programme found transport would miss its then-target by roughly 30 Mt CO2eq (against a 95 Mt CO2eq goal) — the largest shortfall of any sector assessed.
- Two policy pathways modelled: a "CO2 path" relying primarily on carbon pricing (CO2 price rising to €210/tonne by 2025 and €450/tonne by 2030) to suppress demand via higher fuel prices; and a "policy mix" path combining a more moderate carbon price with tighter vehicle CO2 standards (above the CO2-path level), higher company car and vehicle taxes, a 130 km/h motorway speed limit, zero-emission zones in the five largest German cities, and a shift from environmental/innovation purchase premiums to a bonus-malus scheme from 2023.

- This February 2022 study, commissioned by T&E Germany from consultancy Prognos, models two distinct policy pathways for Germany's transport sector to meet its tightened 2030 climate target (85 Mt CO2eq, a 48% cut from 1990 levels) following the 2021 constitutional court ruling and subsequent legal tightening.
- Central finding: both a pure carbon-pricing approach and a broader "policy mix" of regulatory and fiscal measures can in principle deliver the required emissions cut, but the carbon-pricing-only path requires a very steep CO2 price rise (to €450/tonne by 2030) to hit the target, whereas the policy-mix path spreads the burden across regulation (stricter vehicle standards, speed limits, urban zero-emission zones) and a more moderate carbon price.
- The study frames rapid electrification of road transport, enabled by "boldly designed policy instruments," as the central lever making the 2030 target achievable under the policy-mix scenario.
- See [Germany raises climate target following 'epoch-making' supreme court decision](germany-raises-climate-target-following-epoch-making-supreme-court-decision.md) for the constitutional court ruling and legal tightening this study responds to, and [Germany confirms end of combustion engine era – but doesn't say how](germany-confirms-end-of-combustion-engine-era-but-doesn-t-say-how.md) for T&E's assessment of whether Germany's actual coalition policy matches the ambition modelled here.

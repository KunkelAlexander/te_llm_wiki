# The EU's green fuels law: A clean shift for EU transport fuels?

*Briefing · Brussels · 2021-11-26*

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- 10 years of EU biofuels policy required ~8.4 million hectares of land for crop-based biofuels (rapeseed, palm, soy, sunflower) — twice the size of the Netherlands; since 2011, EU drivers burned ~39 Mt of palm and soy biodiesel combined, emitting ~381 Mt CO2eq — more than if fossil diesel had been used instead.
- The EEA found the average GHG intensity of EU transport fuels was only 2.6% lower in 2019 than in 2010 including ILUC impacts (4.3% excluding ILUC) — evidence the EU was "not on track" to meet its 2020 target.
- The Commission's proposed RFNBO (renewable hydrogen/e-fuels) target is 2.6% of transport demand by 2030; combined with its industrial target of replacing 50% of grey hydrogen, this would require ~500 TWh of additional renewable electricity generation — equivalent to France's current total electricity consumption or all EU wind power generated.
- T&E's recommended, lower RFNBO target: 1.6% (44 PJ e-kerosene for aviation ≈ a 2% e-kerosene mandate, plus 85 PJ e-fuels ≈ 6% of shipping energy demand) — versus the Commission's 0.7% aviation e-kerosene sub-target and no dedicated shipping RFNBO sub-target.
- T&E recommends the RED's proposed 13% carbon-intensity reduction target be lowered; ICCT modelling cited finds a scenario excluding crop biofuels would be equivalent to only an 8% target.
- A Cerulogy study (cited) finds Europe's soy biodiesel use could grow 2–4x by 2030 versus current levels if palm oil is phased out, causing deforestation over an area larger than London.

- This November 2021 T&E briefing sets detailed recommendations on the European Commission's proposed Renewable Energy Directive (RED) revision (part of Fit for 55), arguing the Commission's proposal creates useful new incentives for electricity and RFNBOs but fails to fix the RED's decade-long biofuels problems.
- Core recommendations: support the Commission's proposed renewable-electricity credit-trading mechanism (but expand it to cover private/home charging, which represents ~80% of all EV charging); lower the RFNBO target from 2.6% to 1.6% (matched to realistic 2030 aviation/shipping demand, plus a dedicated shipping sub-target) rather than risk diverting scarce green hydrogen to road transport; phase out high-ILUC-risk biofuels (palm, soy and their co-products) by 2022; phase out all crop biofuels (including intermediate crops like Brazilian "safrinha" corn) by 2030; and remove unsustainable feedstocks (e.g. crude tall oil, PFAD) from the Annex IX advanced-biofuels list.
- T&E also recommends lowering the RED's proposed carbon-intensity target and keeping the GHG-savings threshold at 70% (rising to 80% if crop biofuels aren't fully excluded, to compensate for unaccounted ILUC emissions), and calls for RED sustainability criteria to extend to the newly proposed ETS2 covering road transport fuels.

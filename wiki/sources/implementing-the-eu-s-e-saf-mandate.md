# Implementing the EU's e-SAF mandate

*Briefing · Brussels · 2024-10-01*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/implementing-the-eus-e-saf-mandate) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/202410_ReFuelEU_Penalties.pdf)

*(No abstract was published for this document; summary below written from the source text.)*

This briefing analyses the penalty system that will apply to jet fuel suppliers under the EU's ReFuelEU Aviation regulation from 2025, finding that paying penalties for missing sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) quotas would always be costlier than complying with the mandate, provided penalties are based on accurate price references. It warns that legacy jet fuel suppliers are not yet involved in major e-kerosene projects in Europe despite this, and estimates that collective non-compliance penalties for e-kerosene could reach up to 292 billion euros between 2030 and 2035, compared with an estimated 37 billion euros to produce enough e-kerosene to meet the mandate over the same period.

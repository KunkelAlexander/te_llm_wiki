# Four positives for sustainable aviation in this annus horribilis

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2020-12-21*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/four-positives-sustainable-aviation-annus-horribilis)

> By Matteo Mirolo and Andrew MurphyThe Covid-19 pandemic inflicted a public health catastrophe on the world together with an unprecedented economic crisis. In the midst of all this sorrow, however, it is a relief that a third item was not added to this list: climatic back-pedaling. Indeed, with most of the aviation sector on its knees, there were fears that long overdue action to rein in its growing emissions would be delayed, once again, out of so-called "economic pragmatism". On the contrary, 2020 was a year of opportunity to build a green transformation for aviation. Here are four positives in this annus horribilis:

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Aviation's non-CO2 climate impact is estimated (per a European Commission report on the topic, cited here) to be roughly three times the effect of its CO2 emissions alone.

*(Otherwise a largely qualitative year-in-review opinion piece; the underlying figures for each of the four developments are covered in T&E's dedicated papers on each topic, cross-linked below.)*

- This December 2020 T&E opinion piece (Matteo Mirolo and Andrew Murphy) lists four positive 2020 developments for sustainable aviation policy despite the COVID-19 crisis: (1) the Commission's announced ReFuelEU initiative (due March 2021) to mandate sustainable aviation fuel uptake, which T&E urges should prioritise e-fuels over unsustainably-sourced alternatives; (2) Airbus's September 2020 publication of three hydrogen-powered zero-emission aircraft concepts, which the authors read as a signal (though not yet proof) that the industry accepts fossil jet fuel has an end date; (3) the European Investment Bank's November 2020 Climate Roadmap decision to withdraw support for airport expansion; and (4) the Commission's November 2020 report acknowledging aviation's non-CO2 climate effects are roughly three times its CO2-only impact, which the authors frame as opening the door to future non-CO2 legislation.
- The piece frames 2021 as the year these initiatives (ReFuelEU, non-CO2 policy, investment shifts) would need to actually materialise to confirm COVID-19 acted as an "unintended ally" of aviation's green transformation.

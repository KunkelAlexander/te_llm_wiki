# Airline beggars can't be choosers

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2020-12-03*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/airline-beggars-cant-be-choosers)

> Let's call a spade a spade: much of the EU airline industry is bankrupt. Since March airlines have needed a €37 billion cash injection to stay afloat. And there's more to come. Airports are begging for help. Mighty Airbus too has come up with its own backdoor bailout. But does one of the world's most polluting industries really need saving?

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- EU airlines had needed a €37 billion cash injection since March 2020 to stay afloat, with more bailout demands expected.
- T&E's memorandum with the aviation industry commits signatories to 2019 as the peak year for aviation emissions, with "substantial" cuts by 2030 and net-zero by 2050, to be codified in a European sustainability pact agreed in 2021.
- T&E's target: fossil-fuel-powered short-haul and intra-EU flights should be progressively phased out, with planes flying within Europe running on hydrogen, electric power, or (more likely) e-fuels by 2040.

- This is a T&E opinion piece (William Todts) arguing that, while support for aviation *workers* through job-retention schemes is justified, bailing out airlines/airports/shareholders is not economically necessary — the sector's physical infrastructure survives the crisis regardless, and struggling carriers would be replaced by others (citing Ryanair/Wizz Air's own expansion plans).
- T&E frames government bailout leverage as a rare opportunity for "green conditionality," citing a memorandum T&E signed with Europe's aviation industry (announced that month) in which the industry accepts a mandate for clean aviation fuels (particularly e-fuels) and confirms the EU ETS — not ICAO's CORSIA — should remain the aviation carbon-pricing tool.
- T&E calls on the Commission's state-aid enforcer (then Margrethe Vestager) to reject future bailouts that don't align with this sustainability pact, and argues the crisis is a unique opportunity to address pre-pandemic distortions like loss-making regional airports kept alive by rolling bailouts and ultra-cheap tickets enabled by the jet fuel tax exemption and a weak ETS.
- See [Making aviation fuel mandates sustainable](making-aviation-fuel-mandates-sustainable.md) for T&E's detailed critique of how national SAF mandates (flagged here only briefly) were developing at the time, and [One year of airline bailouts: what have we learned?](one-year-of-airline-bailouts-what-have-we-learned.md) for T&E's later assessment of how conditional (or not) the actual bailouts turned out to be.

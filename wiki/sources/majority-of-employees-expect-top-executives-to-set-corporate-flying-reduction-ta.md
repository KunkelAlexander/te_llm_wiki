# Majority of employees expect top executives to set corporate flying reduction targets – new survey

*Press release · Brussels · 2022-11-03*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/majority-of-employees-expect-top-executives-to-set-corporate-flying-reduction-targets-new-survey) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/Poll_Travel-Smart-briefing_2024-04-29-152720_dkwb.pdf)

> Three in five business travelers report having curbed their flying habits as a result of the pandemic in a new survey by the Travel Smart Campaign conducted by Ipsos.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- 62% of business travelers surveyed fly at least somewhat less than before the pandemic, including 23% who have stopped flying for work entirely; more than half of all full-time employees surveyed (55%) say they currently never fly for work.
- 77% of respondents at least somewhat agree businesses have an important role in fighting climate change; 74% agree that reducing corporate flying is an important factor in cutting a business's carbon footprint, and 74% agree businesses must set targets/travel policies to reduce corporate flying levels.
- 60% of business travelers say they play an extremely/very important role in shaping their company's travel policy; a majority report being willing to take steps like taking fewer flights for internal meetings (90% at least somewhat willing) or using more environmentally friendly transport (86%).
- Poll covered 2,506 adults aged 18-74 (including 1,279 business travelers) across the US, UK, France, Germany and Spain, conducted 6-10 October 2022 by Ipsos, with a credibility interval of ±2.4 percentage points (±3.4 for business travelers).

- This November 2022 T&E press release presents results of a five-country Ipsos poll (commissioned as part of T&E's Travel Smart Campaign) finding strong employee support for corporate flying-reduction policies, and documents how the pandemic already reshaped business travel habits.
- Half or more of business travelers report the reduction in travel had a "great or good amount" of impact on their work-life balance (55%), productivity (50%), health (51%) and motivation (50%) — used to argue reduced corporate flying need not harm employee wellbeing or output.
- 75% of respondents believe cost savings will meaningfully influence a company's decision to cut business flights — more than cite employee work-life balance or health/wellbeing (62% each) — suggesting a financial, not just environmental, case for flying-reduction policies.
- Frames the findings as evidence that corporate net-zero commitments should translate into concrete travel-reduction targets and policies, since employees themselves expect and are largely willing to support this shift.

# Now is the time for an ambitious European e-kerosene target

*Letter · Brussels · 2021-07-02*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/now-is-the-time-for-an-ambitious-european-e-kerosene-target) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/2021_06_Letter_accelerate_e-kerosene_final.pdf)

> T&E, Lufthansa, the Global Alliance Powerfuels, pilots representatives, and other aviation industry and energy players have called on the European Commission to set ambitious targets for e-kerosene, a green synthetic fuel, when it proposes the world's first sustainable jet fuel law this month.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Pre-COVID, aviation accounted for 3.7% of EU emissions, up from 1.4% in 1990.
- The coalition's proposed e-kerosene mandate: binding, starting at 0.5–1% of EU jet fuel supply in 2027 (rather than waiting until 2030), potentially rising to 2.5% by 2030 subject to sustainability assurance.
- The letter calls for a non-compliance penalty at least equal to the expected cost differential between e-kerosene and fossil kerosene.

- This is a June 2021 open letter to Commission Vice-President Timmermans and Commissioners Vălean and Simson — co-signed by T&E, Lufthansa, the Global Alliance Powerfuels (chaired by the German Energy Agency), pilots' representatives and other aviation/energy industry players — calling for the incoming ReFuelEU proposal to start a binding e-kerosene mandate in 2027 rather than 2030, to avoid delaying planned e-kerosene facility investments already under development for the second half of the decade.
- Signatories argue accompanying measures (to prevent competitive distortion for European airlines) must be introduced simultaneously with the mandate, and that policy support for Direct Air Capture must begin this decade to reduce reliance on biogenic or fossil point-source CO2 and long-distance CO2 transport, with ReFuelEU potentially guaranteeing DAC market demand directly.
- Notably, this letter — unlike most T&E aviation publications — is co-signed by an airline (Lufthansa) and an energy-industry alliance, signalling broader industry buy-in for an early, binding e-kerosene mandate ahead of the Commission's ReFuelEU proposal.
- See [E-kerosene mandate: key steps for ReFuelEU success](e-kerosene-mandate-key-steps-for-refueleu-success.md) for T&E's own detailed technical recommendations (including its 1% 2030 volumetric sub-target proposal) that this joint letter's earlier 2027 start date builds on, and [Decades of inaction on flying's climate impact are being replaced by action](decades-of-inaction-on-flying-s-climate-impact-are-being-replaced-by-action-but-.md) for T&E's assessment of the ReFuelEU proposal once actually published.

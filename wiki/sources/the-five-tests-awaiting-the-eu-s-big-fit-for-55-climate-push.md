# The five tests awaiting the EU's big 'Fit for 55' climate push

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2021-06-29*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/the-five-tests-awaiting-the-eus-big-fit-for-55-climate-push)

> On the 14th July the European Commission will propose what may well be the most important climate legislation anywhere, ever. On the line in this bumper package is Europe's ability to decarbonise transport. Failure on transport, would mean failure for the entire package. Success, on the other hand, would supercharge action in an area that affects the everyday lives of Europeans.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- As of writing, one in seven vehicles sold in Europe had a plug (i.e. was electric or plug-in hybrid), following the 2020 car CO2 standards taking effect.
- T&E's recommended 2030 car CO2 target: two-thirds of new cars sold emissions-free, rising to 100% a few years later.
- Volkswagen announced its VW brand would go all-electric by 2035.

- This T&E opinion piece (published just ahead of the European Commission's July 2021 "Fit for 55" package) sets out five tests T&E argues the package must pass to genuinely decarbonise European transport.
- Test 1 — Ambitious CO2 standards and a combustion-engine end date: T&E calls for tougher car CO2 standards from 2025 to trigger a "Henry Ford moment," with two-thirds zero-emission car sales by 2030 rising to 100% shortly after.
- Test 2 — An ambitious charging infrastructure law covering both cars AND trucks: T&E calls for an EU charging regulation (rather than a directive) guaranteeing coverage for both passenger EVs and electric trucks (citing Daimler's electric Actros and Scania's electrification targets).
- Test 3 — Moving "beyond biofuels": T&E calls for the Commission to Europeanise the approach already taken by the Netherlands, Germany and France (crediting/multiplying electricity in national green-fuel laws) to break "the biofuel monopoly" on the Renewable Energy Directive.
- Test 4 — Green hydrogen fuel mandates for aviation and shipping: T&E calls for ambitious e-fuel mandates (e-kerosene, e-ammonia) rather than biofuel promotion, citing recent "used cooking oil" fraud scandals as evidence even advanced biofuels have major sustainability problems.
- Test 5 — Pricing shipping and aviation pollution (plus a kerosene tax): T&E calls for all European shipping to be brought into the EU ETS, the aviation ETS to be strengthened/expanded, and a kerosene tax finally introduced — while explicitly deprioritising the idea of including road transport in a carbon market, which T&E argues would do little to cut emissions and would be politically very hard to agree.
- See [What the EU's climate plan means for Europe](what-the-eu-s-climate-plan-means-for-europe.md) for T&E's assessment of how the actual Fit for 55 package scored against these five tests once published.

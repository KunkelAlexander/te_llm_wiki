# T&E, shipping and aviation industry jointly call for urgent action to ramp up clean fuel production

*Press release · Brussels · 2025-02-13*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/t-e-shipping-and-aviation-industry-jointly-call-for-urgent-action-to-ramp-up-clean-fuel-production) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/FINAL-ECSA-A4E-TE-joint-statement-13022025.pdf)

> European Shipowners (ECSA), Airlines for Europe (A4E), which represent the shipping and aviation sector at European level, along with the green transport group Transport & Environment (T&E) issue today a joint statement calling on the Commission and the EU member states to ramp up the production of clean shipping and aviation fuels in Europe.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Per the Draghi report (cited by ECSA), European shipping alone needs about €40 billion/year in investment to decarbonise; the joint statement cites ~€100 billion/year needed for European shipping and aviation combined.
- Recommends the upcoming Sustainable Transport Investment Plan (STIP) require at least 40% of the scalable renewable fuels needed to comply with FuelEU Maritime and ReFuelEU Aviation to be produced within the EU.

- Joint public statement by T&E, European shipowners' association ECSA, and airline association A4E (Airlines for Europe) — a rare alignment between an environmental NGO and the industries it typically pushes against — addressed to the European Commission ahead of the Clean Industrial Deal.
- Five asks: include scalable renewable fuels for shipping/aviation within the Clean Industrial Deal's scope; use the Sustainable Transport Investment Plan to de-risk investment with a 40% EU-production requirement; establish a European industrial base for these fuels via public financial support (EU/national ETS revenue, contracts for difference, revenue-certainty mechanisms); create a one-stop-shop "matchmaking platform" upgrading the Hydrogen Bank to simplify access to financing; and support ports/airports becoming renewable-fuel energy hubs through infrastructure mandates and international cooperation.
- Frames current EU Hydrogen Bank/Innovation Fund support as insufficient given first-mover technology risk, high capital costs, and a business-model mismatch between fuel producers (seeking long-term high-price contracts) and shipping/airline buyers (preferring shorter-term, smaller-volume commitments).
- See [E-fuels financing and infrastructure](../topics/e-fuels-financing.md) and [How to take the risk out of hydrogen investments](how-to-take-the-risk-out-of-hydrogen-investments.md) for T&E's related proposals on de-risking fuel investment.

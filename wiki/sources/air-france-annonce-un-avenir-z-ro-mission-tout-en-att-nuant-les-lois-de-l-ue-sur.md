# Air France announces a zero-emission future while lobbying to weaken EU climate laws

*Original title (French): "Air France annonce un avenir zéro émission tout en atténuant les lois de l'UE sur le climat"*

*Briefing · France · 2022-04-07*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/te-france/articles/air-france-annonce-un-avenir-zero-emission-tout-en-menant-des-actions-de-lobbying-pour-attenuer-les-lois-de-lue-sur-le-climat) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/Legacy-airlines-take-aim-at-EU-climate-policies.pdf)

> A new analysis by Transport & Environment (T&E) reveals that Air France is working to weaken the EU's climate plans for aviation, omitting up to 80% of its emissions [1].

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Under IATA's amendment restricting ReFuelEU's SAF mandate to intra-EU flights, Air France's SAF-mandate emissions coverage would fall from over 90% to just 20% — letting over 80% of its emissions "off the hook," as this page's headline states.
- The IATA/BDL scope-restriction proposal would cover only 28% of total EU departing aviation emissions overall, cutting the Commission's expected CO2 savings by 69%; IAG's alternative (intra-EU scope with a higher 10% 2030 sub-target) would still cut savings by 38% in 2030.
- Air France-KLM currently pays for only 8% of its emissions under the EU ETS and receives roughly half its allowances for free, while lobbying to delay free-allowance phase-out by 3 years (to 2030) and to exempt hub "feeder" flights from ETS obligations.

- This is the French-office (T&E France) publication of T&E's April 2022 briefing exposing legacy-airline lobbying against "Fit for 55" aviation climate measures, using Air France's specific lobbying record as the headline example.
- Same underlying analysis as [Europe's largest airlines claim net zero future whilst lobbying to weaken EU's climate laws](europe-s-largest-airlines-claim-net-zero-future-whilst-lobbying-to-weaken-eu-s-c.md) — see that page for the full briefing content, including per-airline coverage figures for Iberia, Aer Lingus and Lufthansa, and details of specific amendments tabled by MEPs following industry lobbying.
- Also documents Air France-KLM's push to weaken CORSIA (delaying its baseline reset, opposing offset-quality safeguards) despite CORSIA already being considered ineffective by T&E.

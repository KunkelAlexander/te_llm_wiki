# Getting hydrogen right from the start

> T&E submission to Commission consultation on Renewable Fuels of Non-Biological Origin (RNFBOs)

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- RePowerEU's target of 20 million tonnes of renewable hydrogen and ~120 GW of electrolyser capacity by 2030 (to produce 10 Mt of it within the EU) is double the original "Fit for 55"/RED revision target, and would require about 500 TWh of additional renewable electricity — more than all installed EU27+UK wind power generated in 2021, or France's total annual electricity demand.
- The RED revision's RFNBO transport target rose from 2.6% to 5.7%, and its target for replacing "grey" hydrogen in industry with renewable hydrogen rose from 50% to 75%.
- T&E's proposed phased DAC carbon-feedstock mandate for e-kerosene: 10% (2030) → 20% (2035) → 40% (2040) → 80% (2045) → 100% (2050) — versus the Commission's proposed cut-off of simply excluding fossil carbon from 2036 with no phased ramp-up.
- Modelling cited (Aurora Energy Research) finds a hybrid wind+solar electrolyser setup can reach 4.1 €/kg H2 by 2025 at an optimal ~57% load factor — pushing for maximum operating hours alone does not minimise hydrogen production cost once hourly power-price variation is factored in.

- This June 2022 T&E consultation response addresses two EU delegated acts defining "additionality" rules and a GHG-savings methodology for Renewable Fuels of Non-Biological Origin (RFNBOs, e.g. green hydrogen, e-kerosene, e-ammonia) under the Renewable Energy Directive.
- Central objection: a "grandfathering" clause exempting electrolysers built before 2027 from ever having to prove their electricity is "additional" (i.e., genuinely new renewable capacity, not diverted from the existing grid) risks adding hundreds of TWh of demand serviced by fossil generation, undermining RFNBOs' claimed zero-emission status — T&E argues, backed by commissioned legal advice, that the Commission actually lacks legal authority under Article 27 RED to grant such a blanket exemption and the clause must be removed.
- If grandfathering is retained regardless, T&E's fallback asks: time-limit the exemption, require pre-2027 electrolysers to eventually sign renewable power-purchase agreements or pay into an EU renewable-financing mechanism, and never extend grandfathering to temporal/geographic correlation requirements.
- Separately pushes for hourly (not monthly) temporal correlation from the start, arguing this drives genuinely flexible, renewables-synced electrolyser operation rather than allowing electrolysers to draw grid power that is often fossil-generated.
- On the GHG-methodology delegated act, argues for a slower, ReFuelEU-aligned phased ramp-up of DAC-sourced carbon (rather than the Commission's abrupt 2036 fossil-carbon cutoff) and against allowing new biogenic-CO2 facilities given land and feedstock constraints — see [How can ReFuelEU enable investment in direct air capture?](how-can-refueleu-enable-investment-in-direct-air-capture.md) for the DAC mandate schedule this recommendation is based on.

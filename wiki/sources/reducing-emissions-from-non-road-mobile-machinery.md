# Reducing emissions from non-road mobile machinery

*Briefing · Brussels · 2024-12-19*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/reducing-emissions-from-non-road-mobile-machinery) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/202412_NRMM_briefing_final.pdf)

> How new CO2 rules, public procurement and infrastructure provisions can bring clean, affordable off-road machines to market and establish Europe's industry as a zero-emission leader

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Non-road mobile machinery (NRMM — excavators, tractors, forklifts, inland vessels, diesel trains, etc.) emits 108 Mt CO2e/year, 3.1% of EU climate emissions — equivalent to ~55 million cars, or more than half of heavy-duty vehicles' annual emissions. Without new policy, NRMM is projected to overtake trucks/buses' emissions by the 2040s.
- NRMM makes up 5% of total EU Effort Sharing Regulation (ESR) emissions, but far more in some countries: Finland (13.5%), Sweden (12.7%), Denmark and Latvia (11.5% each), Croatia and Spain (8% each).
- Two-thirds of NRMM emissions come from industry/construction and agriculture/forestry, both of which have grown (+5% and +4% respectively) since 1990, while rail (-71%), military (-54%), fishing (-49%) and inland waterways (-27%) emissions fell.
- NRMM consumed 30 million tonnes of oil-equivalent (Mtoe) of diesel in 2022, versus 1.2 Mtoe gasoline and 1.9 Mtoe LPG — making it the largest still-unregulated oil-consuming sector in Europe.
- Electric machinery is already ~50% cleaner per operating hour than diesel equivalents on the EU's current grid (Volvo CE lifecycle analysis); EU renewable electricity share is projected to rise from 45% today to 62% (2030), 83% (2040) and 90% (2050).
- Volvo Construction Equipment targets 35% of its construction-equipment sales being electric by 2030, expecting upfront price parity with diesel machines within the 2020s; electric machines can have 50% longer service lifetimes than diesel equivalents.
- Air pollution linked to (all) NRMM and other sources causes an estimated 327,000 premature deaths/year in the EU; over 100 million Europeans are exposed to harmful noise levels, a portion attributable to machinery.
- Europe's rail network is 60% electrified, carrying 80% of traffic; a German case study found battery-electric trains optimal for 80% of use cases, hydrogen for the remaining 20%.
- Over 20% of engines in European-manufactured machinery are already imported, mainly from Asia, even though final assembly mostly stays in Europe — flagged as an early warning sign of the same import dependency seen in EVs.

- T&E recommends: introducing EU supply-side CO2 or zero-emission standards regulated at the engine level (not whole-machine, unlike the decade-long VECTO process for trucks); designating mobile machinery a strategic sector under the EU's revised public procurement framework with a "buy green and European" clause; and integrating NRMM charging/refuelling needs into the revised Alternative Fuels Infrastructure Regulation (AFIR).
- Argues a unified CO2 regulation could also fold in NRMM's separate air-pollutant (Stage V/VI) and noise-directive reviews, reducing regulatory overlap while delivering co-benefits.

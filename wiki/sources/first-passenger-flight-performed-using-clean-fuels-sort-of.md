# First passenger flight performed using clean fuels. Sort of.

*News · Brussels · 2021-02-25*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/first-passenger-flight-performed-using-clean-fuels-sort)

> On a freezing February morning, Europe's aviation 'bubble' came together, virtually, to discuss the potential of sustainable advanced fuels (SAFs) for aircraft. The meeting of policymakers, industry executives and NGOs discussed the challenge of making clean fuels available at a competitive price. But one announcement grabbed the headlines. For the first time worldwide, a passenger flight was partly fueled by synthetic kerosene - a fuel that T&E says has the most potential to significantly reduce aviation's climate impact.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- KLM's Boeing 737-800 flew from Amsterdam to Madrid on 500 litres of synthetic kerosene (produced by Shell from CO2, water and renewable energy sourced in the Netherlands) — enough to fuel roughly the first 100km of the flight, blended with fossil jet fuel for the rest.

- This is a T&E news piece covering KLM's February 2021 announcement of the world's first passenger flight partly fuelled by synthetic (e-)kerosene, made at a virtual EU aviation-sector conference on scaling sustainable aviation fuels (SAFs).
- T&E (Matteo Mirolo) frames the flight as a symbolically important but very early first step, noting the fuel used point-source CO2 (captured from an industrial emitter) rather than atmospheric CO2 via Direct Air Capture, meaning it was not fully zero-carbon by T&E's own standard.
- T&E emphasizes e-kerosene's practical advantage — as a drop-in fuel blendable with existing jet fuel, it doesn't require refuelling infrastructure changes — but cautions it "won't arrive in significant amounts until after 2030," meaning flying will remain the most carbon-intensive transport mode in the interim.
- The piece notes several EU member states (Netherlands, France, Sweden, Germany, Finland, Luxembourg, Spain) issued a joint statement the same day calling on the European Commission to require fuel suppliers to blend e-kerosene into aviation fuel, and records that T&E published its own e-kerosene factsheet the same day.
- T&E calls on the Commission's incoming ReFuelEU initiative to support e-kerosene specifically, warning that otherwise "the KLM announcement will be yet another false dawn for greening aviation."

# What the EU climate plan means for aviation fuel

*Briefing · Brussels · 2022-05-31*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/what-the-eu-climate-plan-means-for-aviation-fuel) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/Updated-ReFuelEU-TE-fit-for-55-briefing.pdf)

> The EU Commission has mandated the use of advanced biofuels and e-kerosene (SAFs) for aircraft as part of its Fit for 55 package.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- ReFuelEU's proposed SAF blending obligation starts at 2% in 2025, rising to 5% by 2030 (further increasing beyond that); of the 2030 5% target, only 0.7% is reserved for e-kerosene (synthetic fuel), with the remaining 4.3% left to advanced biofuels.
- The Regulation covers all fuel sold at EU airports — including flights departing the bloc — versus other climate measures (e.g. the EU ETS at the time) that cover only intra-EU flights, representing under 40% of aviation emissions.
- T&E recommends raising the 2025 e-kerosene sub-target to 0.1% (from effectively 0%) and the 2030 e-kerosene sub-target to 2% (from 0.7%), while correspondingly lowering the 4.3% advanced-biofuel target.

- This May 2022 T&E briefing (Matteo Mirolo) is T&E's position paper on the ReFuelEU Aviation proposal as it entered EU Parliament committee votes, summarising what's good, what's flawed, and what needs improvement.
- Praises the exclusion of crop-based (first-generation) biofuels and the proposed anti-tankering rule (requiring airlines to uplift at least 90% of needed fuel from EU airports) as important safeguards against airlines evading the mandate.
- Central critique: the proposal over-weights advanced biofuels (limited, competing-use feedstocks) relative to e-kerosene, which T&E considers the only fuel type genuinely scalable to meet aviation's long-term fuel demand; it also calls for renewable hydrogen/electricity to be brought into ReFuelEU's scope to support zero-emission aircraft development.
- Flags non-CO2 effects — said to represent two-thirds of aviation's climate impact — as an issue ReFuelEU could partly address via fuel-quality (aromatic/sulfur content) regulation, but doesn't yet.
- Notes the legislative timeline as of writing: TRAN (lead), ITRE and ENVI committees in Parliament had already adopted more ambitious opinions (including a higher synthetic-fuel sub-target); a TRAN vote was scheduled for 15/16 June 2022, a Council general approach for 2 June, and trilogues expected to start summer 2022 with entry into force targeted for 2023.
- See [How can ReFuelEU enable investment in direct air capture?](how-can-refueleu-enable-investment-in-direct-air-capture.md) for T&E's parallel push on the DAC carbon-feedstock share within the e-kerosene sub-target.

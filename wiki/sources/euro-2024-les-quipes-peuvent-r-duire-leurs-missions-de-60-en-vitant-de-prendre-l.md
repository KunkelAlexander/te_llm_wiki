# Euro 2024: teams could cut their emissions by 60% by avoiding flying

*Original title (French): "Euro 2024 : Les équipes peuvent réduire leurs émissions de 60% en évitant de prendre l'avion"*

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- National football teams competing in Euro 2024 could cut their travel emissions by nearly 60% overall if they avoided flying; teams from countries near Germany could cut travel-to-basecamp emissions by 94–98% by using rail/road instead.
- If all 24 teams avoided flying during the group stage, they would save more than 840 tonnes of CO2e (95% of group-stage travel emissions) — equivalent to over 150 times the annual carbon footprint of an average European, or producing 750,000 beers (per the briefing's own comparison metric).
- Switching from air to rail/coach to reach Germany (for teams where this is feasible, e.g. Austria, Belgium, France, Switzerland) would save 270 tonnes of CO2e (25% of relevant emissions).
- Full-competition case study: the German team's commitment to avoid flying during the group stage saves an estimated 97 tonnes CO2e (98%); if extended through the knockout stage, savings could reach 164 tonnes (97% of total travel emissions).
- Elite players like Harry Kane and Kylian Mbappé would, if flying for the whole tournament, generate transport emissions seven to nine times the average person's total monthly emissions.
- For the six teams most likely to reach the final (Spain, France, England, Belgium, Italy, Germany), switching from air to rail/road for the final would save an average 96% of emissions.
- Aviation's non-CO2 climate impact was included via a 1.7x multiplier (GWP100) applied to CO2 emissions to estimate total CO2e.
- Team-level plans found: Switzerland travelled entirely by bus/rail avoiding flights; Germany avoided flying for the group stage; Portugal used coach for 2 of 3 group games.

- Joint briefing by T&E, the Travel Smart Campaign and 18 national partner organisations, which wrote to 13 national football federations urging them to shift from air to rail travel during the tournament.
- Notes UEFA's own mitigation steps: a discounted rail-ticket partnership with Deutsche Bahn and geographically clustering group-stage matches to reduce travel distances.
- Frames the tournament as a missed opportunity for national teams to model low-carbon travel choices to fans, in line with T&E's broader "flying less"/Travel Smart corporate-travel-emissions campaign.

# Mitigating aviation's non-CO2 effects in the EU's Fit for 55 package

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Non-CO2 effects (contrail cirrus, NOx, soot, water vapour) make up two-thirds of aviation's total climate impact, per a 2020 EASA report commissioned by the European Commission — larger than the warming effect of aviation's CO2 emissions alone.
- The EU ETS currently gives no carbon price at all to non-CO2 effects, despite the 2017 ETS reform explicitly mandating the Commission study how to address them.

- This April 2022 T&E briefing (Matteo Mirolo, Carlos López de la Osa) argues the "Fit for 55" package (ReFuelEU Aviation and EU ETS) is missing a critical opportunity to legislate on aviation's non-CO2 climate effects, which have been scientifically understood since the 1990s but left unregulated.
- Two concrete ReFuelEU asks: require a Commission impact assessment (by 31 December 2023) on reducing aromatics, naphthalene and sulphur content in fossil jet fuel to cut contrail formation and improve air quality, followed by legislation on fuel-quality limits; and mandate fuel suppliers to publicly report the aromatic/naphthalene/sulphur content of jet fuel supplied in the EU.
- Two concrete EU ETS asks: create a pilot Monitoring, Reporting and Verification (MRV) scheme for non-CO2 effects (fuel flow, flight position, humidity/temperature, emission indices) within 6 months of the ETS revision entering into force, with a 2025 Commission report on results and follow-up pricing legislation; and, until that MRV scheme is operational, apply a non-CO2 multiplier requiring airlines to surrender two ETS allowances per tonne of CO2 (a measure the European Parliament first called for in 2007), exempting operators already participating in the MRV pilot.
- See [Aviation: reducing the climate impact of contrails would cost less than 4 euros per flight](aviation-r-duire-l-impact-climatique-des-tra-n-es-de-condensation-co-terait-moin.md) for T&E's later, more granular costing of contrail-mitigation measures.

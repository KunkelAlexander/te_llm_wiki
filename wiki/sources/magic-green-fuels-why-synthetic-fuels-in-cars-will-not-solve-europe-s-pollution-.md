# Magic green fuels: Why synthetic fuels in cars will not solve Europe's pollution problems

*Report · Brussels · 2021-12-06*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/magic-green-fuels-why-synthetic-fuels-in-cars-will-not-solve-europes-pollution-problems) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/2021_12_IFPEN_test_results.pdf)

> T&E commissioned lab tests to measure the air pollutant emissions of e-petrol in cars.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- IFP Energies Nouvelles lab/road tests (commissioned by T&E) of e-petrol blends in a Mercedes A180 vs. standard E10 petrol: no difference in NOx emissions; particle number (PN >10nm) fell 97% (lab) and 81–86% (real-driving/RDE) — a large relative cut, but still at least 2.2 billion particles emitted per km even with 100% e-petrol; no difference in particle mass.
- Toxic carbon monoxide emissions were up to ~3x higher (lab test) and 1.2–1.5x higher (RDE) with e-petrol than fossil petrol, with the largest increase at cold engine start (common in urban driving).
- Hydrocarbon emissions fell 23–40% on the lab test but showed no difference on the real-driving test; ammonia emissions (a PM2.5 precursor) roughly doubled on the RDE test for two of the three e-petrol blends, especially at cold start.
- E-petrol combustion was found to emit methane and nitrous oxide (not counted in "climate neutral" claims); T&E calculates this adds ~7–9 kg CO2-equivalent per car per year (at 12,000 km/year) — small per car, but equivalent to ~50,000 extra fossil-fuel cars on EU roads if applied to all 2020 new diesel/petrol car sales.
- T&E cites its own separate analysis that an e-fuel-running petrol car would leave a driver about €10,000 worse off over 5 years compared to a BEV owner (new or second-hand), and that e-fuel CO2 credits would cost carmakers roughly 3x more than producing battery-electric cars in 2030.
- Cited context: over 400,000 Europeans die prematurely from air pollution each year; a YouGov poll of 21 major European cities found 2 in 3 residents did not want to return to pre-lockdown pollution levels.

- This December 2021 T&E study (lab/road testing by IFP Energies Nouvelles) tests industry claims (from Bosch, Porsche, and oil lobby group FuelsEurope) that synthetic "e-petrol"/"e-diesel" could be a clean alternative to electrification for combustion-engine cars.
- Central finding: e-petrol is not a clean fuel — while it substantially cuts particle-number emissions, it leaves NOx unchanged, increases toxic carbon monoxide and (in some blends) ammonia emissions, and is not truly "climate neutral" once methane and N2O combustion by-products are accounted for.
- T&E frames this as directly rebutting the "decarbonise the fuel, not the engine" argument some auto suppliers and oil companies use to argue against battery-electric vehicles, concluding e-fuels are also far more expensive for consumers than battery-electric alternatives.
- T&E's recommendation: policymakers should focus on affordable mass-market electric vehicles (via stricter EU CO2 standards) and better public/shared transport, rather than treating e-fuels as a pollution or climate solution for cars.

# EU aviation deal will see another lost decade in tackling emissions

*Press release · Brussels · 2022-12-07*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/eu-aviation-deal-will-see-another-lost-decade-in-tackling-emissions)

> EU negotiators have come to an agreement on the carbon market for aviation and failed to include long-haul flights in the pricing scheme. But in an unprecedented move, non-CO2 effects of the sector will be addressed under the agreement.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The final EU ETS aviation deal keeps the carbon market limited to intra-EEA flights, leaving 58% of Europe's aviation CO2 emissions unpriced.
- T&E's earlier study found CORSIA (the alternative offered for long-haul flights) would cover only 22% of total international aviation emissions by 2030 — the deal nonetheless leaves long-haul flights to rely on it rather than the ETS.
- Free ETS allowances to airlines will be phased out by 2026 — one year earlier than originally proposed — and the ETS scope can be revisited by 2027 if CORSIA isn't strengthened by then.

- This December 2022 T&E press release reports EU negotiators' final trilogue agreement on the aviation carbon market, criticising the decision to exclude long-haul (extra-EEA) flights from ETS pricing in favor of the "cheap and dubious" CORSIA offsetting scheme, while welcoming a world-first requirement for airlines to report non-CO2 effects (two-thirds of aviation's climate impact).
- Frames the outcome as socially unfair: European families flying intra-EU on holiday will pay for their CO2 emissions while frequent long-haul flyers largely will not, since only intra-EEA flights are ETS-covered.
- Notes the deal also approved a "SAF allowances" pricing mechanism intended to bridge the cost gap between conventional jet fuel and sustainable aviation fuels, which T&E says needs to be reserved for the most sustainable fuel types to be effective.
- Quotes T&E's Jo Dardenne: the deal represents "another decade of climate inaction because of EU governments' cowardice towards ICAO" — see [UN body ICAO hails empty goal and cheap offsetting scheme to 'green' aviation](un-body-icao-hails-empty-goal-and-cheap-offsetting-scheme-to-green-aviation.md) for the CORSIA critique this argument draws on.

# Europe's massive dependence on used cooking oil imports raises fears of fraud

*Original title (French): "La gigantesque dépendance de l'Europe aux importations d'huile de cuisson usagée fait craindre des fraudes"*

*Press release · France · 2023-12-14*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/te-france/articles/la-gigantesque-dependance-de-leurope-aux-importations-dhuile-de-cuisson-usagee-fait-craindre-des-fraudes)

> Analysis of the most recent biofuel data reveals a worrying increase in dubious imports of used cooking oils, as airlines increasingly promote biofuels as a solution for flying cleaner.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Europe now imports 80% of the used cooking oil (UCO) it burns as car, truck and plane fuel; 60% of all UCO imports (and 40% of Europe's total UCO supply) come from China alone — rising to 96% of Bulgaria's UCO imports.
- European UCO consumption more than doubled between 2015 and 2022.
- Palm-oil-derived biodiesel fell nearly 30% in 2022 versus the prior year, but palm oil derivatives (POME, PFAD) rose enough to offset almost half of that decline between 2020-2022.
- Biodiesel's average carbon footprint remains about 20% worse than conventional diesel, per T&E's analysis, despite its "renewable" status under the EU's RED.

- This December 2023 T&E France press release, based on updated biofuels-market data analysis, warns that Europe's aviation industry's push to use UCO as a "sustainable aviation fuel" (SAF) feedstock — following Virgin Atlantic's "first 100% SAF transatlantic flight" the prior month — is deepening reliance on a feedstock highly vulnerable to fraud.
- Central concern: virgin oils (e.g. palm oil) can be falsely labelled as "used" cooking oil to be counted as a sustainable/waste feedstock; Germany and Ireland have launched official investigations, and the European Commission has pledged to investigate fraudulent Indonesian biodiesel potentially routed through China and the UK to dodge tariffs.
- Notes crop-based bioethanol (maize, wheat, sugar beet) has barely declined as a share of biofuel feedstock over the past decade (95% in 2010 to 85% in 2022), continuing to put pressure on global food prices and land use.
- T&E calls for greater transparency and import limits on UCO to prevent it becoming a "backdoor" for deforestation-linked palm oil, arguing biofuels broadly should not be treated as a climate panacea.

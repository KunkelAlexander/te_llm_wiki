# Response to the Emissions Trading System (ETS) inception impact assessment

*Consultation response · Brussels · 2020-11-30*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/response-emissions-trading-system-ets-inception-impact-assessment) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/ETS20IIA20-20T.pdf)

> European shipping is a large source of greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions and air pollution. Analysing the Commission's ETS consultation, T&E expresses concerns.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- EU-related maritime CO2 emissions reached 144 Mt in 2019 (full MRV scope) — 3.7% of total EU CO2 emissions and 13% of EU transport emissions, comparable to the climate impact of Belgium; global shipping emissions are projected to grow up to 50% by 2050.
- Limiting a maritime ETS to intra-EEA scope would cover only 55 Mt of CO2 (38% of EU trade-related maritime emissions) versus 144 Mt under full MRV scope.
- Shipping benefits from an estimated €24 billion/year in fossil fuel tax subsidies for international journeys, plus VAT/ticket-tax/corporate-tax exemptions; a cited ITF report found at least €3 billion/year spent on just three maritime subsidies (tonnage taxes, domestic fuel tax exemptions, seafarer wage-cost measures) in OECD countries.
- Aviation: European aviation CO2 emissions rose 1.5% in 2019 while other ETS sectors fell 8.9%; since 2013 aviation emissions rose 27.6% versus a 19.7% fall elsewhere in the ETS; airlines received ~32.3 million tonnes of free allowances in 2019 (worth ~€900m at ~€25/tonne) against an estimated €27 billion/year jet fuel tax exemption; governments had committed at least €32 billion in aviation bailouts.
- Road transport: modelling (Cambridge Econometrics) cited by T&E finds an extended ETS covering road transport would deliver close to zero additional emissions reductions by 2030 and less than 10% by 2050, due to inelastic fuel demand.
- T&E calls for car CO2 standards to become annual and reach 65% by 2030 as an alternative/complementary lever to carbon pricing for road transport decarbonisation.

- This November 2020 T&E response to the Commission's Emissions Trading System Inception Impact Assessment covers three separate proposals in one submission: (A) extending the ETS to full MRV-scope shipping emissions, (B) strengthening the aviation ETS, and (C) opposing the possible inclusion of road transport (and buildings) in the ETS.
- On shipping (section A): T&E calls for the ETS to cover the full MRV scope (not just intra-EEA), no pilot phase or free allowances (given the sector's already-long grace period), and a dedicated "Ocean Fund" (as proposed by the European Parliament) using ETS revenue to fund zero-carbon fuels/technology uptake via Contracts for Difference, alongside a possible multiplier to strengthen the price signal.
- On aviation (section B): this section closely mirrors T&E's contemporaneous papers — see [Revision of the EU ETS for aviation](revision-of-the-eu-ets-for-aviation.md) and [Making the aviation ETS fit for purpose](making-the-aviation-ets-fit-for-purpose.md) for the detailed reform recommendations (removing free allowances, discounting factors, cap reductions, minimum price, rejecting CORSIA substitution) repeated here.
- On road transport/buildings (section C): T&E strongly opposes repealing national Effort Sharing Regulation (ESR) targets in favour of an extended ETS, arguing this is a "carbon pricing is not a holy grail" scenario — modelling shows minimal near-term emissions impact, regressive effects on lower-income households, and political risk of undermining support for the vehicle CO2 standards that have actually driven the EV transition. T&E's preferred "missing Option Zero" is to keep and strengthen both the ETS and the ESR in parallel, rather than restructure the architecture; it flags Germany's national road-transport carbon pricing scheme (from 2021) as a cautionary precedent given ministry-announced compensation for hauliers undermining the price signal.
- See [The costs of EU ETS and Corsia for European aviation](the-costs-of-eu-ets-and-corsia-for-european-aviation.md) for the detailed cost modelling behind the aviation-specific figures cited in section B.

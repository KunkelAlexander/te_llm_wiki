# Who could finance e-kerosene production in Germany?

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- T&E calculates the German government lost €4 billion in 2022 due to aviation tax exemptions (fuel tax, ETS, VAT exemptions).
- Germany's air passenger tax (Luftverkehrsteuer) is capped at €2.33 billion/year in total revenue — T&E calls for scrapping this cap entirely.
- Taxing business-class tickets by their CO2 footprint (3.7x a narrowbody economy seat, 4.3x a widebody economy seat) would raise an extra €303 million/year while affecting only 4% of passengers; proposed rates range from €15.53 to €70.83 by distance class, rising to €57.46–€304.57 under the CO2-weighted approach.
- Private jets are 5–14 times more polluting per passenger than commercial aircraft, yet are largely exempt from kerosene tax (via a loophole where operators register as commercial passenger services — e.g. Volkswagen, which flies 8 private-jet flights per day, does this) and from EU ETS carbon pricing.
- Proposed private-jet kerosene tax of €0.65/litre (matching car drivers' fuel tax) on Germany's estimated private-jet kerosene consumption would raise about €38 million/year (exact single-digit figure garbled in the scraped text).
- Proposed alternative: a per-flight private-jet levy differentiated by EU vs. non-EU destination, based on 2022 departure counts.
- Private jets caused 248,322.3 tonnes of CO2 in Germany in 2022; at the German Environment Agency's recommended cost of carbon (€809/tonne), this represents foregone revenue, equivalent to an average €2,555 per private-jet flight if levied as a per-departure charge.
- Nearly 85% of Germans surveyed support measures against private jets; only 5% opposed any action on private-jet emissions (More in Common survey).
- Private jet owners have an average net worth of €1.3 billion; a Berlin–Frankfurt private jet charter costs roughly €7,000–15,000, and extra-EU flights (e.g. to Bali) can cost €100,000+.
- 14 of 20 planned European e-kerosene research facilities are located in Germany, giving it potential research/innovation leadership — T&E's e-kerosene tracker finds enough planned German production capacity to exceed the 2032 ReFuelEU target domestically, if funding materialises.
- T&E's modelling finds e-kerosene must supply 20% of German aviation's CO2 abatement by 2050, with no comparably scalable sustainable alternative fuel.

- T&E (Germany office) argues the government broke its coalition-agreement promise to reinvest air-tax revenue into e-kerosene production, and proposes reforming the Luftverkehrsteuer (higher business-class rates, uncapped revenue, a new private-jet levy) to fund domestic e-kerosene ramp-up instead.
- Also calls for a ban on fossil-fuelled private jets by 2030.

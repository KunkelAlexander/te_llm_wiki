## Summary

*Press release announcing T&E's report [Down to earth](down-to-earth.md) — see that page for the full quantitative findings (traffic-growth scenarios, fuel-burn projections, carbon-budget depletion dates, e-kerosene electricity demand) drawn from this same underlying document.*

- Summarises the report's core warning: if Airbus/Boeing's own passenger-growth forecasts materialise, European aviation's emissions will barely fall by 2049 despite meeting ReFuelEU's SAF mandates, and the sector will exhaust its 1.5°C-aligned carbon budget as soon as 2026.
- Reiterates T&E's four recommendations: end airport infrastructure expansion, remove aviation's fossil-fuel subsidies, prioritise scaling e-kerosene over unsustainable biofuels, and maximise rail's potential to substitute short-haul flights.

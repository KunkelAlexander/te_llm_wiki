# How long will flying less remain the best way to reduce aviation's climate impact?

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2022-03-14*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/how-long-will-flying-less-remain-the-best-way-to-reduce-aviations-climate-impact)

> Aviation emissions need to fall now, but the technical solutions to achieve this take time. Thankfully, the Covid crisis has shown that while technology is scaled up, we have ways to immediately reduce the climate impact of flying.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- 1% of the global population is responsible for 50% of aviation emissions; corporate flyers account for around 30% of Europe's flying emissions.
- Cutting corporate travel by 50% versus pre-Covid levels could cut CO2 emissions by up to 32.6 MtCO2 by 2030 — equivalent to taking 16 million polluting cars off the road.
- T&E's updated modelling finds demand management (flying less) is the only way to substantially cut aviation emissions for the rest of this decade, before sustainable fuels and zero-emission aircraft can scale.

- This March 2022 op-ed previews the findings of T&E's revised "Roadmap to climate neutral aviation," arguing the EU's "Fit for 55" fuel and carbon-pricing proposals rightly target the root causes of aviation emissions but will take years to bite, leaving demand reduction as the only lever with near-term impact.
- Argues the Covid-19 pandemic proved not every flight is essential — physical connectivity isn't always necessary for economic growth, and remote work can substitute for many corporate trips — making a 50% cut in business travel a realistic near-term target.
- Frames demand management explicitly as a *transitional*, not permanent, solution: as SAF production, e-kerosene and zero-emission aircraft scale up later this decade, technology is expected to overtake demand reduction as the primary lever.
- Criticises the aviation industry for spending more effort opposing climate measures and promoting offsetting/ICAO's CORSIA than investing in the innovations needed to decarbonise, previewing further T&E work "exposing more of these anti-climate tactics" (see [Europe's largest airlines claim net zero future whilst lobbying to weaken EU's climate laws](europe-s-largest-airlines-claim-net-zero-future-whilst-lobbying-to-weaken-eu-s-c.md)).

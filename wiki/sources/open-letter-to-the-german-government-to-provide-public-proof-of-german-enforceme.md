# Open letter to the German government to provide public proof of German enforcement of the ETS

*Letter · Brussels · 2014-04-29*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/open-letter-german-government-provide-public-proof-german-enforcement-ets)

> With this open letter, Transport & Environment calls on the German government and the German enforcement agency (DEHSt) to provide public proof that German enforcement of the 2012 Aviation Emissions Trading System legislation is fully applied to non compliant carriers by the 26 April 2014 deadline.

## Summary

**Key dates/figures:**
- Enforcement deadline cited: **26 April 2014**.
- Underlying legislation: Germany's **2012** Aviation Emissions Trading System (ETS) law.

- T&E's open letter targets two addressees: the German federal government and DEHSt (the German Emissions Trading Authority), the body responsible for enforcing aviation ETS compliance domestically.
- The demand is narrow and procedural: public proof that Germany is actually enforcing the 2012 aviation ETS legislation against non-compliant carriers, not a request for new policy.
- This fits a broader pattern (seen elsewhere in T&E's early ETS-era advocacy) of pressing individual member states on enforcement gaps rather than only lobbying at EU/ICAO level — weak national enforcement was a recurring T&E concern for aviation's inclusion in the ETS around this period.

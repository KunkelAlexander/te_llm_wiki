# Oil companies and airlines are delaying investments in e-kerosene

*Original title (German): "Ölkonzerne und Fluggesellschaften verschleppen Investitionen in E-Kerosin"*

*Press release · Germany · 2024-12-03*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/te-deutschland/articles/oelkonzerne-und-fluggesellschaften-verschleppen-investitionen-in-e-kerosin)

> Most airlines show no serious ambition to switch to e-kerosene. Oil companies are also investing too little in sustainable aviation fuels. This not only slows the shift to climate-neutral flying but is also a missed opportunity for the German economy.

## Summary

*German-office coverage of T&E's global airline SAF ranking, with a specific Lufthansa Group focus; see the companion French-office piece,
[Seulement 10 compagnies aériennes...](seulement-10-compagnies-a-riennes-dont-air-france-klm-investissent-s-rieusement-.md), for the full ranking's top performers (Air France-KLM, United, Norwegian).*

**Key quantitative findings:**
- 87% of 77 ranked airlines (67 airlines, including the Lufthansa Group) buy too little SAF, the wrong type of SAF, or don't consider SAF at all in decarbonisation plans; only 10 airlines (including DHL) showed meaningful effort.
- E-kerosene makes up less than 10% of airlines' SAF agreements overall, while unsustainable crop-based biofuels account for more than 30%.
- The Lufthansa Group spent about €7.9 billion on fuel alone in 2023, against which its e-kerosene investment (a stake in Synhelion) and offtake agreement (with Atmosfair) are described as disproportionately small.

- T&E's ranking scores airlines on SAF/e-kerosene targets and commitment (offtake agreements, letters of intent, MOUs), weighting e-kerosene — made from renewable electricity — as the most sustainable and scalable SAF type versus food/feed-crop biofuels, which are not considered sustainable at all.
- Frames Lufthansa Group's SAF strategy as overwhelmingly focused on unsustainable biofuels rather than e-kerosene, calling this a missed opportunity for German industry given the country's e-kerosene research/production potential (see [Who could finance e-kerosene production in Germany?](wer-k-nnte-die-e-kerosin-produktion-in-deutschland-finanzieren.md)).

# Rail and clean industry players join forces with NGOs to call for the introduction of a fuel tax for planes and ships

*Letter · Brussels · 2024-10-18*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/rail-and-clean-industry-players-join-forces-with-ngos-to-call-for-the-introduction-of-a-fuel-tax-for-planes-and-ships) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/Joint-letter-on-ETD-Unfair-tax-exemption-FINAL-3.pdf)

*(No abstract was published for this document; summary below written from the source text.)*

In this joint letter to EU finance ministers and the incoming Climate Commissioner, Transport & Environment and a coalition of rail, sustainable aviation fuel and civil society organisations call for an end to tax exemptions on aviation and marine fuels under the Energy Taxation Directive, warning that a proposed extension of the exemption to 2049 would undermine rail's competitiveness against flying and cost EU governments billions in lost revenue. The letter cites a 10.7 billion euro tax gap from untaxed jet fuel on flights departing Europe in 2022 alone.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Untaxed jet fuel on flights departing the EU-27 represented a €10.7 billion tax gap for governments in 2022 alone.
- Greenpeace found rail travel in Europe costs on average twice as much as flying — a key barrier to shifting passengers from air to rail.
- An estimated €80 billion is needed to deploy ERTMS rail signalling across the entire TEN-T network.
- 500–800 SAF production facilities are estimated to be needed globally by 2050 (SkyNRG); at ~€1.8 billion per facility, that implies roughly €36 billion/year in capital expenditure between 2025 and 2050.
- Trains are, on average, at least 12 times more energy-efficient per passenger than planes (IEA).
- The proposed Energy Taxation Directive (ETD) revision under negotiation would extend the tax exemption on fossil jet and marine fuel until 2049 — a year before the EU's 2050 climate-neutrality deadline.

- Signatories: T&E plus rail-sector bodies (ALLRAIL, AERRL, Back on Track, trainline), SAF/e-fuel industry players (SkyNRG, H2V, European Energy, Nordic Electrofuel, Norsk e-fuel) and environmental NGOs (BLOOM, Carbon Market Watch, Seas At Risk, and others), addressed to EU finance ministers and the incoming Climate Commissioner.
- Argues continuing the aviation/marine fuel tax exemption unfairly undermines rail and clean-fuel competitiveness, contradicts COP28 fossil-subsidy-phaseout commitments and the EU's own Sustainable and Smart Mobility Strategy (carbon-neutral travel under 500km by 2030, doubling high-speed rail by 2030), and represents a social-fairness failure given ordinary citizens pay tax on heating, driving and rail travel while jet fuel remains untaxed.
- Calls for aviation and marine fuels to finally be taxed under the Energy Taxation Directive, both to fund the transition (ERTMS rollout, SAF scale-up) and to close the price gap that currently favours flying over rail.

# Tax exemptions for aviation: German government forewent four billion euros in 2022

*Original title (German): "Steuerausnahmen für den Luftverkehr: Bundesregierung hat sich 2022 vier Milliarden Euro entgehen lassen"*

*Press release · Germany · 2023-07-12*

> The European aviation sector benefits from unjustified tax exemptions worth billions - and these are set to increase further in the coming years. Transport & Environment and Germanwatch call on the German government to close the climate-damaging tax gap now.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Across the study's full scope (EU27, UK, Norway, Switzerland, Iceland), European governments lost €34.2 billion in 2022 due to aviation tax/carbon-pricing exemptions ("the tax gap") — projected to rise to €47.1 billion by 2025 absent reform; Germany's own 2022 share of this foregone revenue was around €4 billion, per this press release's headline.
- Current EU aviation pricing policies capture on average only 16% of the total revenue that could be raised by properly taxing the sector; the top 15 most-polluting airlines account for 56% of the total European tax gap.
- Ending the exemptions in 2022 would have cut CO2 emissions by 34.8 Mt (equal to the combined emissions of Ryanair, Air France and Lufthansa) — or up to ~104.4 Mt CO2-equivalent once non-CO2 effects are counted, comparable to taking all German cars off the road for a year.
- If national governments must act unilaterally absent EU-wide reform, T&E calculates the ticket taxes needed to close the gap average €23 (domestic), €51 (intra-European) and €259 (extra-European) per journey.

- This July 2023 T&E Germany press release, based on T&E's Europe-wide "Aviation Tax Gap" study (with Germanwatch), quantifies how much revenue European governments forgo through jet fuel tax exemptions, VAT exemptions on tickets, and weak/absent carbon pricing (ETS, CORSIA) — using Germany's ~€4 billion 2022 shortfall as the headline figure to press the German government specifically to act.
- T&E's core recommendations: end jet fuel tax exemptions, extend carbon markets to cover all airline emissions (including long-haul), apply VAT to all plane tickets; in the interim, national ticket taxes calibrated to the tax-gap benchmarks above; and reinvest a share of any new revenue into clean technologies (renewable energy, e-fuels) and rail alternatives.

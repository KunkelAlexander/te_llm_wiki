# Green jet fuel plans risk history repeating itself

*News · Brussels · 2021-01-21*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/green-jet-fuel-plans-risk-history-repeating-itself)

> Back in 2009 EU leaders proudly adopted the Renewable Energy Directive, setting flawed targets for the use of renewable fuels in the road transport sector. The resulting use of unsustainable crop-based biofuels, such as palm and rapeseed oil, drove deforestation, loss of habitats and increased emissions. Now, a new T&E study shows history risks repeating itself with plans for airline sustainable advanced fuel (SAF) targets.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Same underlying seven-country SAF mandate comparison as [Making aviation fuel mandates sustainable](making-aviation-fuel-mandates-sustainable.md) — no additional figures beyond the country ratings are given in this press release's own text.

- This is T&E's other press release (aviation director Andrew Murphy) announcing the "Making aviation fuel mandates sustainable" study ahead of the Commission's planned 2021 ReFuelEU proposal, framing the choice of alternative jet fuel as determining "whether we repeat the mistakes of the past" (the road-transport RED biofuels experience) "or embrace fuels that can actually decarbonise aviation."
- T&E flags Spain, the Netherlands, Sweden and Finland for unrealistically high targets risking unsustainable biofuel uptake, and France/Norway for a strong advanced-biofuels focus that could still strain limited sustainably-sourceable feedstocks like used cooking oil.
- T&E recommends countries properly assess the impact of the targets they set for advanced biofuels, and again singles out Germany's e-fuels-focused, crop-biofuel-free approach as the most promising (while flagging its sustainability criteria as still unclear) — calling for e-fuels to be produced with 100% renewable electricity and Direct Air Capture rather than fossil waste CO2.

# Can we count on zero-emissions planes and ships?

*News · Brussels · 2022-03-14*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/can-we-count-on-zero-emissions-planes-and-ships)

> Transport & Environment (T&E) gathered innovative industry players and policy makers to discuss the future of zero-emission shipping and aviation.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Electric aircraft developer Wright plans to launch a fully battery-electric aircraft by 2026, retrofitted from a BAe 146, carrying 100 passengers on one-hour flights; Heart Aerospace targets the same 2026 timeframe with its new 19-seater ES-19.
- Hydrogen-aviation developers ZeroAvia and Universal Hydrogen expect to offer hydrogen-powered regional commercial aircraft as early as 2024.
- Congestion in European airspace is estimated to cost airlines €37 billion/year.

- This March 2022 T&E news write-up recaps an online event ("Acting now for the zero-emissions planes and ships of tomorrow") bringing together start-ups, established companies and policymakers on zero-emission shipping and aviation technology.
- Shipping panel: speakers from the European Green Hydrogen Acceleration Center, MAN ES (ammonia/methanol engines in development), LMG Marin (hydrogen ship projects already delivered) and Madoqua (hydrogen/methanol/ammonia supply to Portuguese ports) argued zero-emission shipping tech already exists but needs policy support — noting the EU's draft FuelEU Maritime proposal does not give enough market predictability and risks locking in gas demand instead (see [How to drive the uptake of sustainable fuels in European shipping](how-to-drive-the-uptake-of-sustainable-fuels-in-european-shipping.md) for T&E's detailed critique).
- Aviation panel: covered progress in electric aviation (Pipistrel's already-EASA-certified Velis Electro, plus Wright and Heart Aerospace timelines above), hydrogen propulsion (ZeroAvia, Universal Hydrogen), and power-to-liquid SAF using direct air capture, which speakers called the most scalable synthetic-fuel pathway despite production capacity still being far below what's needed.
- T&E's policy asks reiterated at the event: a 2025 sub-mandate for direct air capture and higher PtL (power-to-liquid) e-fuel quotas in ReFuelEU Aviation, with advanced biofuels — especially used cooking oil and animal fats — capped given their limited scalability and negative side effects (e.g. cross-subsidising industrial animal farming).
- Concludes that demand management and efficiency are necessary near-term steps in both sectors, but zero-emission technology must scale in parallel, requiring stronger legislative mandates (FuelEU Maritime and ReFuelEU Aviation) than currently proposed.

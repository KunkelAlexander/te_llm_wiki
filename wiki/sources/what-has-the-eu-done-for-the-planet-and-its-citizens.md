# What has the EU done for the planet and its citizens?

*News · Brussels · 2024-05-06*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/what-has-the-eu-done-for-the-planet-and-its-citizens) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/Report-What-has-and-can-the-EU-do-for-you-and-the-planet.pdf)

> We have arrived at a critical juncture when it comes to our climate and the environment. The planet is on track towards a temperature increase of 2.9C compared to pre-industrial levels, with Europe warming even faster, and we are seeing high levels of environmental pollution and degradation.

## Summary

*(Joint pre-election publication by BirdLife Europe, Climate Action Network Europe, the European Environmental Bureau, T&E and WWF European Policy Office — a broad 10-topic "what has the EU done for citizens" voter-outreach report, not T&E-specific. The scraped fulltext runs to over 100 pages covering nature, air/water quality, energy poverty, green jobs and diets alongside transport; this summary covers only the transport-relevant sections — electromobility and rail — per the wiki's "if in doubt, leave it out" guidance on non-transport content.)*

**Key quantitative findings (transport-relevant sections):**
- Road transport caused approximately a quarter of the EU's CO2 emissions in 2021, an estimated 70,000 premature deaths/year, and up to €80 billion/year in health costs; the report cites 238,000 total EU premature deaths from air pollution in 2023.
- The EU's public EV charging network grew to almost 340,000 chargers in 2021, a three-fold increase since 2018; a new EU law requires charging points at least every 60km on key roads.
- E-car total cost of ownership is now cheaper than an equivalent petrol/diesel car in 19 EU member states; 2023 BEV sales grew 28% across Europe (more than a third within the EU alone), though slower than the 2020–2021 growth rate.
- SUVs made up ~54% of new EV sales, a trend the report flags as eroding EVs' emissions/resource-efficiency gains.
- 52% of EU citizens say they're willing to shift travel from planes to trains; 73% believe the EU should make rail travel more affordable.
- Rail electrification: 60% of the EU rail network is electrified, carrying 80% of traffic; Portugal raised its electrified-route share from 56% to 71% (2019–2022); the EU's TEN-T policy targets full electrification of the mainline network (80% of traffic) by 2050.
- Madrid–Santiago de Compostela rail travel time fell from 4h30 to 3h20 with EU-backed investment; the reopened Brno–Židlochovice (Czechia) line is ~15 minutes faster than driving at rush hour and grew from 1,200 to nearly 2,000 daily passengers.
- Night-train connections rose from 120 (2019) to 200 (2023), restoring routes like Berlin–Brussels and Vienna–Paris.

- Frames EU Green Deal-era policy (2019–2024 mandate) as the reason for tangible citizen-facing progress — cheaper/faster/greener rail, a growing EV charging network, and the 2035 combustion-engine phase-out law — while flagging remaining gaps: SUV-driven EV inefficiency, uneven national fleet-electrification incentives, and continued reliance on raw-material imports for batteries.

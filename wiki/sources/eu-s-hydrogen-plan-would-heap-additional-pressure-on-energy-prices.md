# EU's hydrogen plan would heap additional pressure on energy prices

*Press release · Brussels · 2021-12-01*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/eus-hydrogen-plan-would-heap-additional-pressure-on-energy-prices)

> The EU's hydrogen plan for transport goes beyond what is needed for hard to electrify sectors like shipping and aviation and risks driving up energy prices without additional renewables.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- The Commission's Fit for 55 RED proposal sets a 2.6% target for green hydrogen/e-fuels in transport by 2030, plus replacing 50% of industrial grey hydrogen with green hydrogen.
- T&E analysis: meeting the 2.6% transport target would raise renewable electricity demand by almost 17% of total 2030 electricity demand — equivalent to adding France's entire electricity consumption (500 TWh) — and would require more renewable electricity than all EU battery-electric vehicles (cars, buses, trucks) combined would consume in 2030.
- T&E's recommended, lower target: 1.6%, which it argues would still be "ambitious" but roughly twice the level actually needed for shipping and aviation during this period, versus the Commission's target being described as almost twice what's needed for these sectors alone.
- A hydrogen-powered car requires roughly 4x more renewable energy than an equivalent battery-electric car.

- This T&E news piece (Geert De Cock) warns the Commission's Fit for 55 green hydrogen target for transport is set too high relative to genuine demand from hard-to-electrify sectors (shipping, aviation), and risks diverting scarce renewable electricity away from the power grid at a time of already record-high energy prices.
- T&E's core concern: without a requirement that hydrogen production be matched by *additional* new renewable generation, the target risks diverting existing renewables from the grid, making the grid dirtier (backfilled by gas, the marginal fuel) and undercutting the emissions savings from electric vehicles and heat pumps — with gas prices at record highs making this an especially costly outcome.
- T&E supports hydrogen for shipping and aviation (where no direct-electrification alternative exists) but opposes over-sizing the target to also serve inefficient hydrogen/e-fuel use in road transport, where direct electrification is available and far more energy-efficient.
- See [How to ensure the sustainability of electrofuels](how-to-ensure-the-sustainability-of-electrofuels.md) and [The EU's green fuels law: A clean shift for EU transport fuels?](the-eu-s-green-fuels-law-a-clean-shift-for-eu-transport-fuels.md) for T&E's detailed RFNBO-target and additionality recommendations (including its 1.6% target proposal) referenced here.

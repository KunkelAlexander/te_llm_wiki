# Europe's largest airlines claim net zero future whilst lobbying to weaken EU's climate laws

*Press release · Brussels · 2022-04-07*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/europes-largest-airlines-claim-net-zero-future-whilst-lobbying-to-weaken-eus-climate-laws) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/IAGs-position-on-EU-legislative-proposals-of-the-Fit-for-55.pdf)

> New analysis by Transport & Environment (T&E) finds that the four legacy carriers - Air France, Lufthansa, Iberia and Aer Lingus - are working to water down EU's climate plans for aviation, letting up to 72% of EU aviation emissions off the hook [1].

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- IATA/BDL's proposal to restrict ReFuelEU's SAF mandate to intra-EU flights only would cover just 28% of total EU departing aviation emissions, cutting the Commission proposal's expected CO2 savings by 69% (rising target-scope-adjusted comparison); IAG's alternative (intra-EU scope but a higher 10% 2030 sub-target) would still cut savings by 38% in 2030 and 69% by 2050.
- Per-airline SAF-mandate coverage under the amendments: Iberia falls from >98% to 21% of emissions covered; Aer Lingus from >92% to 40%; Air France from >90% to 20% (under IATA's version); Lufthansa from >90% to 33%.
- Air France-KLM currently pays for only 8% of its emissions under the EU ETS (which covers only intra-EEA flights) while still receiving roughly half its allowances for free, and is lobbying to delay free-allowance phase-out by 3 years (to 2030) and to exempt "feeder" flights from ETS surrender obligations.
- Aviation accounted for 3.7% of European emissions pre-pandemic (up from 1.4% in 1990); non-CO2 effects make its true warming impact larger still.

- This April 2022 T&E briefing (Jo Dardenne) documents specific lobbying positions submitted by IATA, IAG, Germany's BDL and Air France-KLM to weaken the "Fit for 55" ReFuelEU Aviation and EU ETS proposals, and traces those positions directly into amendments tabled by named MEPs (e.g. Jens Gieseke's scope-narrowing amendment, EPP MEPs' free-allowance and CORSIA amendments).
- Frames this as a direct contradiction between airlines' public 2050 net-zero pledges and their private lobbying to gut the two main legislative tools (SAF mandates, carbon pricing) that would make net zero achievable.
- Also flags airline lobbying to scrap ReFuelEU's e-kerosene sub-mandate (which would leave the sector reliant on unsustainable animal fats and used cooking oil) and to weaken/delay CORSIA implementation and its offset-quality safeguards.
- T&E's counter-recommendations: increase and bring forward the e-kerosene target in ReFuelEU; address non-CO2 effects via reduced fuel aromatics; extend the EU ETS to cover all departing flights; and add a non-CO2 allowance-surrender requirement.
- See [Airlines call for an end to loopholes in carbon market and back European climate measures](airlines-call-for-an-end-to-loopholes-in-carbon-market-and-back-european-climate.md) for a contrasting case of airlines (easyJet, Ryanair, Wizz Air, Jet2) publicly backing full-scope climate measures around the same time.

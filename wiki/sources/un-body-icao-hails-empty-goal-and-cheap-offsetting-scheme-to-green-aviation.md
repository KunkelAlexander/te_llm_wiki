# UN body ICAO hails empty goal and cheap offsetting scheme to 'green' aviation

*Press release · Brussels · 2022-10-07*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/un-body-icao-hails-empty-goal-and-cheap-offsetting-scheme-to-green-aviation)

> With the offsetting scheme put forward by the UN agency for aviation, ICAO, a mere 22% of total international emissions would be offset by 2030, new data by Transport & Environment (T&E) finds.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- ICAO's 2022 General Assembly set CORSIA's baseline at 85% of 2019 emissions levels (above which airlines must offset), instead of the previously-expected 2019/2020 average — weakened further under industry pressure.
- Under the new 85% baseline, T&E calculates only 22% of total international aviation emissions (154 Mt of a projected 699 MtCO2 in 2030) would be offset by CORSIA by 2030.
- On a Europe-to-US flight, the new baseline would add just €1.70 to the ticket price to offset emissions — down from the €2.40 T&E had calculated under the previously-expected average 2019/2020 baseline.
- Major aviation markets — China, Russia, Brazil, India — do not apply CORSIA at all, further limiting its coverage.

- This October 2022 T&E press release responds to the outcome of the ICAO General Assembly in Montreal, where member states agreed a non-binding net-zero-by-2050 goal for aviation while simultaneously weakening CORSIA, the only concrete mechanism meant to deliver it.
- T&E's core argument: a non-binding aspiration paired with a scheme covering barely a fifth of emissions, at an offset cost of a few euros per long-haul ticket, provides no meaningful incentive for airlines to decarbonise or invest in SAF — contrasted explicitly with the binding EU measures (SAF mandate, departing-flight carbon pricing) T&E argues the EU should press ahead with regardless of ICAO's outcome.
- Frames the Assembly's decision as evidence ICAO "continues to adapt its measures for the benefit of the industry and not for the climate," urging the EU not to let CORSIA's existence be used as an excuse to delay its own aviation climate measures.

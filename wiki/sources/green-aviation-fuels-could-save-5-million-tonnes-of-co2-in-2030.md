# Green aviation fuels could save 5 million tonnes of CO2 in 2030

*Press release · Brussels · 2022-06-09*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/green-aviation-fuels-could-save-5-million-tonnes-of-co2-in-2030) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/20220602_E-kerosene-tracker-briefing_SB.pdf)

> Sustainable fuel manufacturers in Europe could produce 1.83 million tonnes of e-kerosene in 2030, saving about 5 Mt of CO2, a new forecast by Transport & Environment finds.

<!-- ENRICHED SUMMARY: hand-written, preserved across regenerations by 1_build_wiki_subset.py -->

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- T&E identified 10 companies with planned e-kerosene capacity of 0.16 Mt by 2025 and at least 18 companies planning 1.83 Mt by 2030 — enough to cover 3.65% of EU jet fuel demand and save about 5 MtCO2/year (the equivalent of 30,000 transatlantic flights).
- The Commission's ReFuelEU proposal set an e-kerosene sub-target of just 0.7% by 2030 (0.35 Mt) — less than a fifth of the 1.83 Mt industry already had planned — rising to 28% by 2050; T&E recommends 0.1% (0.05 Mt) in 2025 and 2% (1.00 Mt) in 2030 instead.
- Of the 22 e-kerosene producers analysed, 7 use direct air capture (DAC), 14 use biogenic carbon sources, and 8 use industrial point-source capture (some combine methods); e-kerosene production could create 202,100 additional jobs by 2050 per Commission estimates, with costs projected to fall 61% by 2050 versus 2025.

- This is the original June 2022 press release for T&E's e-kerosene production tracker briefing (see [Analysis of green jet fuel production in Europe](analysis-of-green-jet-fuel-production-in-europe.md) for the November 2022 updated figures), quantifying planned e-kerosene manufacturing capacity across Europe to argue the Commission's proposed ReFuelEU blending sub-target is set far too low to matter.
- Central argument: since 18+ companies (in Germany, France, Portugal, the Netherlands, Sweden and Norway) already have production plans exceeding the proposed mandate several-fold, a higher mandate would not outstrip supply — it would simply give the market the policy certainty needed to keep investing.
- Recommends renewable hydrogen and electricity for direct use in future electric/hydrogen/fuel-cell aircraft be included within ReFuelEU's synthetic-fuel sub-target definition, alongside e-kerosene, since these are forecast to supply a meaningful share (10-20% per T&E's own Roadmap, similar per A4E's Destination 2050 and ATAG's Waypoint 2050) of aviation's 2050 energy demand.

# Europe gets one step closer to a green flight

*Op-ed · Brussels · 2022-07-25*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/articles/europe-gets-one-step-closer-to-a-green-flight)

> A landmark vote in the European Parliament last week secured the role of sustainable fuels in the decarbonisation of European aviation.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- European aviation traffic grew 67% between 2005 and 2019 while CO2 emissions grew 24%, now 4.9% of the EU's pre-Covid total emissions (CO2 only); EASA estimates non-CO2 effects make up two-thirds of aviation's total climate impact.
- Halving corporate travel versus pre-Covid levels could cut CO2 emissions by up to 32.6 MtCO2 by 2030 — equivalent to taking 16 million polluting cars off the road.

- This July 2022 T&E op-ed (first published in Aviation Week) reflects on the European Parliament's 7 July 2022 ReFuelEU Aviation vote, which it calls a clear endorsement of "the world's most ambitious clean fuel mandate for aviation."
- Recounts how a last-minute proposal to widen the SAF definition to include palm oil by-products (PFADs) and "intermediate crops" was defeated after an unprecedented joint lobbying push by airlines and NGOs (including easyJet and T&E); edible animal fats remained in the definition as the sole unresolved loophole heading into trilogue negotiations.
- Highlights the Parliament's parallel decision to expand the synthetic-fuel definition to cover renewable electricity and green hydrogen directly, signalling EU intent to accelerate zero-emission (electric/hydrogen) aircraft development alongside e-kerosene.
- Frames demand management (reduced corporate travel, no further airport expansion) as the only lever capable of meaningfully cutting aviation emissions before 2030, with SAF/technology solutions taking over as the primary lever only later.

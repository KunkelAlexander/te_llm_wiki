# The use of animal fats in cars and planes is becoming less and less sustainable

*Original title (French): "L'utilisation de graisses animales dans les voitures et les avions est de moins en moins soutenable"*

*Press release · France · 2023-05-31*

[Read the original publication](https://www.transportenvironment.org/te-france/articles/lutilisation-de-graisses-animales-dans-les-voitures-et-les-avions-est-de-moins-en-moins-soutenable) · [PDF](https://www.transportenvironment.org/uploads/files/202304_Animal_fats_briefing_TE_2024-04-29-184744_gxcv.pdf)

> With around 8,800 dead pigs needed to fuel a Paris-New York flight, the availability of biofuels made from animal fats is increasingly in question, even though some consider them a key solution for decarbonising transport.

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Animal fats used in biofuels grew 40x since 2006, reaching 1.4 million tonnes in 2021; demand is projected to nearly triple to 3.9 million tonnes by 2030.
- A Paris-New York flight fully fuelled by animal-fat-derived SAF would need roughly 8,800 dead pigs' worth of fat each way.
- 2021 EU data show reported category 1&2 animal-fat biofuel consumption at roughly double what the industry (EFPRA) reported actually supplying — evidence of likely fraudulent downgrading of higher-value category 3 fats to claim "double counting" credits.

- This is the French-office (T&E France) press release on the same May 2023 Cerulogy-authored study covered in [Pigs do fly: the rise of animal fats in European transport](pigs-do-fly-the-rise-of-animal-fats-in-european-transport.md) — see that page for the fuller summary, including the fraud-risk mechanism and T&E's policy recommendations (excluding category 3 animal fats from eligible biofuel/SAF feedstocks, capping category 1&2 use at 2020 levels).
- Notes major oil companies are already investing heavily in refining animal fats into SAF despite the limited, competing-use nature of the feedstock.

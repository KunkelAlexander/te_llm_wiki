# Lufthansa advertises a climate-neutral future while working to weaken EU climate rules

*Original title (German): "Lufthansa wirbt mit klimaneutraler Zukunft, bemüht sich aber gleichzeitig um eine Aufweichung der EU-Klimabestimmungen"*

> A new analysis by Transport & Environment (T&E) shows that Lufthansa is working to weaken the EU's climate plans for the aviation sector, so that 67 percent of emissions remain unaccounted for. [1]

## Summary

**Key quantitative findings:**
- Under IATA's amendment (which Lufthansa's trade association BDL co-lobbied for) restricting ReFuelEU's SAF mandate to intra-EU flights only, Lufthansa's emissions coverage under the fuel mandate would fall from over 90% to just 33% — leaving over two-thirds (67%) of its emissions unaccounted for, as this page's headline states.
- The IATA/BDL scope-restriction proposal would cover only 28% of total EU departing aviation emissions overall, cutting the Commission's expected CO2 savings by 69%.
- Aviation accounted for 3.7% of European emissions pre-pandemic (up from 1.4% in 1990), a figure the briefing cites as understating true climate impact once non-CO2 effects are included.

- This is the German-office (T&E Germany) publication of T&E's April 2022 briefing exposing legacy-airline lobbying against "Fit for 55" aviation climate measures, using Lufthansa and its trade association BDL's specific lobbying record as the headline example.
- Same underlying analysis as [Europe's largest airlines claim net zero future whilst lobbying to weaken EU's climate laws](europe-s-largest-airlines-claim-net-zero-future-whilst-lobbying-to-weaken-eu-s-c.md) — see that page for the full briefing content, including per-airline coverage figures for Iberia, Aer Lingus and Air France, and details of specific amendments tabled by MEPs following industry lobbying.
- Notes BDL joined IATA in lobbying to scrap ReFuelEU's e-kerosene sub-mandate, and separately pushed the German government to rely on (already-failed) UN-level action instead of binding EU measures.
